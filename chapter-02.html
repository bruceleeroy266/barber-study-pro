CHAPTER 2

Life Skills

Success psychology, goal setting, and effective study habits

🎯 Why Study Life Skills?

Good technical skills are important, but life skills are equally essential for success in barbering. Barbering is a creative profession that requires artistic talent plus strong:

  • Communication
  • Decision-making
  • Image-building
  • Customer service
  • Self-actualization
  • Goal-setting
  • Time management

Barbers should study life skills because they lead to a more satisfying and productive career, ensure positive interactions with diverse clients and coworkers, help deal effectively with difficult circumstances, and build self-esteem.

💪 Life Skills in Action

Key life skills to put into action:

  • Being helpful and caring to others
  • Making good friends
  • Feeling good about yourself
  • Having a sense of humor
  • Maintaining a cooperative attitude
  • Approaching work with a strong sense of responsibility
  • Being consistent in your work
  • Adapting successfully to different situations

🧠 Psychology of Success

Success is defined differently by each person. Self-actualization (fulfilling your full potential) requires lifelong commitment.

Action Steps for Success:

  • Build self-esteem — Trust your ability to achieve goals; use positive affirmations
  • Visualize success — Regularly imagine yourself competently working in your dream barbershop
  • Build on your strengths — Invest time in activities you enjoy and are good at
  • Be kind to yourself — Eliminate negative self-talk; view mistakes as learning opportunities
  • Stay true to yourself — Be authentic and professional; your uniqueness is a valuable asset

Additional Action Steps:

  • Practice new behaviors (speak confidently, stand tall, use proper grammar)
  • Keep personal life separate from work → maintain healthy work-life balance
  • Keep your energy up (sleep, healthy food, time management, hobbies, family/friends)
  • Respect others (use "please," "thank you," "excuse me"; be a good listener; don't interrupt)
Avoid Three Bad Habits:
  • Procrastination — Putting things off
  • Perfectionism — Unhealthy need to do everything perfectly
  • Lack of a game plan — No organized goals

🎨 Your Creative Capability

Creativity = talent + unlimited resource of ideas and solutions

Tips to Enhance Creativity:

  • Be positive (criticism blocks ideas)
  • Look for creative inspiration (art, music, hair shows, magazines)
  • Improve your vocabulary (use words like explore, analyze, determine)
  • Surround yourself with others who share your passion (teamwork sparks better ideas)

🎯 Design a Mission Statement

Mission Statement Defines your purpose, values, and direction. Provides focus and becomes the "cultural pulse" of your life or business.

Example: "I am dedicated to pursuing a successful career with dignity, honesty, and integrity."

Tip: Write it in 1–2 sentences, keep a copy visible, and read it frequently.

🎯 Set Goals

Goal setting gives direction and helps turn dreams into reality.

Short-term Goals Accomplished in a year or less (e.g., pass an exam, graduate school)
Long-term Goals 2, 5, 10+ years (e.g., own a barbershop in 5 years)

Goal Setting Process:

  • Write goals down
  • Break long-term goals into smaller segments
  • Create action plans
  • Revisit and adjust regularly
  • Set feasible goals and focus on them consistently

⏰ Time Management

Effective time management helps you reach goals faster.

Key Strategies:

  • Prioritize tasks (most important to least important)
  • Create flexible schedules (daily, weekly, monthly) — include unstructured time if needed
  • Don't overcommit — learn to say "no" kindly
  • Use to-do lists, planners, or digital tools
  • Reward yourself for good work
  • Schedule physical activity and free time daily
  • Make time management a habit
In the Barbershop: Arrive on time, communicate with the team, stay on schedule, handle add-ons efficiently. Barbershops require team effort for smooth time management (appointments vs. walk-ins).

📚 Successful Learning Tools

Good Study Habits:

  • Attend all classes and arrive early
  • Have materials ready
  • Listen attentively, take notes, highlight key points
  • Pay attention during reviews
  • Ask for clarification when needed

Memory & Study Techniques:

  • Repetition — Repeat information (aloud, written, hands-on)
  • Organization — Break large topics into smaller segments
  • Mnemonics:
    • Word associations
    • Acronyms (e.g., SHAPES for skin functions)
    • Songs or rhymes for procedures
  • Mind Mapping — Place main topic in center, branch out with key words, use lines/colors/symbols

Taking Notes Tips:

  • Listen for emphasized words and directives ("you need to know")
  • Use templates, key words, symbols, colors
  • Complete sentences when needed
  • Review and transfer notes to long-term memory

⚖️ Practice Ethical Standards

Ethics Moral principles that guide how we live and work

Five Professional Actions:

  • Provide skilled and competent services
  • Be honest, courteous, and sincere
  • Keep client information confidential (discretion)
  • Participate in continuing education
  • Give accurate information about treatments and products

Professional Ethics Qualities:

  • Self-care — Take care of yourself first
  • Integrity — Align actions with your values

😊 Develop a Positive Personality and Attitude

Barbers deal with diverse people daily — a positive attitude is essential.

Characteristics of a Healthy, Positive Attitude:

  • Diplomacy (tact) — Deliver truthful messages kindly
  • Pleasing tone of voice — Speak clearly/softly; smile when appropriate
  • Emotional stability — Handle confrontations professionally
  • Sensitivity — Be compassionate and responsive
  • Values and goals — Guide your behavior
  • Receptivity — Listen openly to others' ideas
  • Effective communication skills — Verbal, nonverbal, and active listening
The Whole Person: Personality = sum of characteristics, attitudes, and behaviors. A pleasing attitude improves both professional and personal life.

📝 Chapter 2 Key Takeaways

  • Life skills are equally essential as technical skills for barbering success
  • Self-actualization requires lifelong commitment to personal growth
  • Avoid procrastination, perfectionism, and lack of game plan
  • Create a mission statement to guide your purpose and values
  • Set short-term and long-term goals with specific action plans
  • Master time management through prioritization and scheduling
  • Use repetition, mnemonics, and mind mapping for effective studying
  • Practice ethics, integrity, and self-care in all professional interactions
  • Develop a positive attitude through diplomacy, emotional stability, and effective communication
