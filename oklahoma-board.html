OKLAHOMA SPECIFIC

Oklahoma State Board of Barbering

Everything you need to know about getting licensed in Oklahoma — requirements, exams, fees, and more.

License Requirements

  • 1,500 hours of barber school
  • Pass NIC written exam (70%)
  • Pass practical exam (70%)
  • Be at least 16 years old
  • Have 8th grade education minimum

Fees (2025)

  • Application: $25
  • Written Exam: $75
  • Practical Exam: $75
  • License Fee: $30
  • Total: ~$205

Renewal

  • Annual renewal required
  • Fee: $30 per year
  • No continuing education required
  • Renew online at OK.gov

NIC National Exam

Oklahoma uses the National-Interstate Council (NIC) standardized exam. This is the same exam used by most states, which means your license is more easily transferable.

Important: You must pass BOTH the written and practical exams with a score of 70% or higher to receive your Oklahoma barber license.

Written Exam Breakdown

Topic Area % of Exam Approx. Questions
Scientific Concepts 30% ~30 questions
Hair Care & Services 25% ~25 questions
Facial Hair & Skin Care 20% ~20 questions
Infection Control 15% ~15 questions
Professional Practice 10% ~10 questions

Practical Exam Skills

  • Work Area & Client Preparation - Sanitation, setup, client protection
  • Haircutting - Taper, fade, and layered cuts
  • Shaving - Straight razor shave with proper technique
  • Facial Massage - Basic massage movements
  • Hair Styling - Blow-drying and finishing
  • Chemical Services - Demonstration of safety knowledge

Application Process

  1. Complete 1,500 hours at an approved Oklahoma barber school
  2. Submit application to Oklahoma State Board of Barbering with $25 fee
  3. Receive approval and exam scheduling information (usually 2-3 weeks)
  4. Schedule exams through PSI Services (the testing vendor)
  5. Pass both exams with 70% or higher
  6. Pay license fee ($30) and receive your license!

Reciprocity (Transferring from Another State)

If you're already licensed in another state, you may be eligible for reciprocity in Oklahoma:

  • Your home state must have equal or greater requirements than Oklahoma (1,500 hours)
  • You must have held your license for at least 1 year
  • Pay reciprocity fee ($50)
  • Submit verification of licensure from your home state
  • No exam required if requirements are equivalent
