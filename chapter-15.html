CHAPTER 15

Men's Hair Replacement

Hair replacement systems, wigs, and hairpiece services

🎯 Why Study Men's Hair Replacement?

Hair replacement services are an important part of barbering. Understanding these services allows you to:

  • Help clients with hair loss feel more confident
  • Provide consultation for hair replacement options
  • Fit, style, and maintain hair replacement systems
  • Understand the emotional impact of hair loss
  • Increase revenue through specialized services
Key Point: Hair loss is a sensitive subject. Always approach clients with empathy and professionalism.

📚 History of Hair Replacement

Hair replacement has evolved significantly over time:

Early Methods:

  • Toupees: Small hairpieces worn to cover bald spots
  • Wigs: Full head coverings made from human or synthetic hair
  • Hairpieces: Partial coverings for specific areas

Modern Hair Replacement Systems:

Today's hair replacement systems are more natural-looking and comfortable than ever. They can be:

  • Custom-made or stock (pre-made)
  • Made from human hair or synthetic fibers
  • Attached with tape, adhesive, or clips
  • Designed for full or partial coverage

💬 The Consultation Process

Hair loss is an emotional subject. Approach clients with sensitivity and professionalism.

Key Points for Consultation:

  • Listen to the client's concerns and desires
  • Discuss lifestyle and maintenance requirements
  • Explain different types of hair replacement systems
  • Discuss costs and maintenance schedules
  • Set realistic expectations
  • Ensure privacy and discretion

Reasons Men Seek Hair Replacement:

  • Genetic hair loss (androgenic alopecia)
  • Medical conditions or treatments
  • Desire to change appearance
  • Professional or social reasons
  • Boost self-confidence

🔬 Hair Loss Solutions

Medical/Surgical Options:

Hair Transplant Surgery — Surgical procedure that moves hair follicles from one part of the body to the balding area. Requires medical referral.
Low-Light Laser Therapy — Non-surgical treatment using low-level lasers to stimulate hair growth. Can be done in barbershops with proper training.

Non-Surgical Options:

Hair Replacement Systems — Custom or stock hairpieces that attach to the scalp using various methods.
Topical Treatments — Minoxidil (Rogaine) and other products that may slow hair loss or promote growth.
Oral Medications — Finasteride (Propecia) - prescription medication for hair loss.

🎭 Types of Hair Replacement Systems

By Construction:

Lace-Front Systems — Feature a lace material at the front hairline that creates a natural-looking hairline. The lace is nearly invisible when properly applied.
Non-Lace-Front Systems — Use other materials (polyurethane, monofilament) for the base. May have a more defined edge at the hairline.
Full Cap Systems — Cover the entire head. Used for complete hair loss or total coverage.
Partial Systems (Toupees) — Cover specific areas of hair loss, blending with the client's natural hair.

By Hair Type:

  • Human Hair: Most natural look and feel; can be styled with heat; requires more maintenance
  • Synthetic Hair: Less expensive; holds style well; cannot use heat styling; shorter lifespan
  • Blended: Combination of human and synthetic hair

📏 Making a Template

A template is used to create a custom hair replacement system that fits the client's head perfectly.

Materials Needed:

  • Grease pencil or eyeliner pencil
  • Permanent marker
  • Plastic wrap
  • Tape measure
  • ¾-inch transparent tape (dull-finish type)

Procedure:

  1. Perform client consultation
  2. Perform preliminary haircut if needed
  3. Place four fingers above eyebrow with last finger on bridge of nose
  4. Make a dot with grease pencil on forehead in line with center of nose
  5. Place tape measure on the dot
  6. Measure length to where back hair begins
  7. Measure across top, directly over sideburns
  8. Stretch plastic wrap over client's head
  9. Twist sides tightly until they conform to contour of head
  10. Mark pattern with permanent marker
  11. Place tape strips over marked area
  12. Remove template and attach hair samples for color matching
  13. Send template and measurements to manufacturer

🏺 Making a Plaster Mold Form

A plaster mold creates an exact replica of the client's head shape for custom systems.

Materials Needed:

  • Black and white eyeliner pencils
  • Permanent marker
  • Plaster gauze strips
  • Plastic bowl for mixing
  • Scissors or shears
  • Tape
  • Towels
  • Waterproof cape

Procedure:

  1. Clean and disinfect work area
  2. Drape client
  3. Cut plaster gauze strips (6 at 9-10 inches, 8 at 4 inches)
  4. Stretch plastic wrap over client's head
  5. Mark pattern and add details with eyeliner
  6. Apply towel around client's neck
  7. Mix plaster and water
  8. Dip gauze strip into plaster bath
  9. Squeeze excess water with two fingers
  10. Apply first strip from front to back
  11. Smooth out following contour of head
  12. Apply second strip from back to front
  13. Apply shorter strips across top
  14. Repeat to create second and third layers
  15. Blowdry to set plaster until completely dry
  16. When mold is dry, gently remove by lifting off head
  17. Trim excess plaster from mold
  18. Trace outline with permanent marker
  19. Write client's name and date on outside
  20. Take hair samples from crown, temple, side, and back
  21. Shampoo and condition client's hair
Note: Plaster mold must cure for 24 hours before shipping to manufacturer.

🔗 Full Head Bonding

Bonding uses adhesive to attach the hair replacement system directly to the scalp.

Materials Needed:

  • Eyeliner pencil
  • Makeup sponges
  • Safety data sheet for adhesive
  • Soft-bond adhesives
  • Towels
  • Waterproof cape

Preparation:

Important: Perform patch test with adhesive 24 hours before applying.
  1. Clean and disinfect work area
  2. Wash hands
  3. Drape client and shampoo hair with pH-balanced shampoo
  4. Towel dry and change to haircutting cape
  5. Ensure base of hair replacement system is dry

Procedure:

  1. Trim client's scalp with edgers
  2. Rinse excess hair from scalp
  3. Dry hair again - do not touch scalp with hands after this step
  4. Select correct adhesive for hair replacement system
  5. Place hair replacement system on client's head
  6. Mark exactly where system needs to be placed on front hairline, temples, and back
  7. Shake adhesive product well
  8. Apply small amount of adhesive in circular motion onto clean scalp
  9. Use makeup sponge to distribute evenly
  10. Adhesive will appear white until it dries clear
  11. When first coat is dry, apply second coat
  12. Repeat for total of four times, using small amounts
  13. Once adhesive is completely dry, apply hair solution
  14. Apply one coat of adhesive to base of hair replacement system
  15. Do not apply adhesive to any lace present
  16. Place system on marks and apply pressure without stretching
  17. Use back of comb to check around perimeter for wrinkles
  18. Smooth out any minor inconsistencies
  19. Place towel over system and pull down tightly for even fit
Reminder: Tell client to allow 24 to 48 hours before shampooing.

🧼 Cleaning Human Hair Replacement Systems

Materials Needed:

  • Brush
  • Manufacturer's recommended cleaning solution
  • Mixing bowl
  • T-pins
  • Tape removal solvent
  • Towels
  • Wide-toothed comb
  • Wig block

Procedure:

  1. Remove all old tape and clean reinforced areas with solvent
  2. Put enough cleaner in glass bowl to submerge system
  3. Invert system with inside up and place in cleaning solution
  4. Soak for 3 to 5 minutes
  5. Swish back and forth or dip up and down until residue is removed
  6. If solution darkens, replace with fresh solution and repeat
  7. Gently tap edge with small brush or fingers until adhesive is removed
  8. Do not rub or scrub
  9. Place towel on flat surface
  10. Place system on towel with inside facing up
  11. Gently press out cleaner with towel
  12. Hold system by front section and comb gently
  13. Fasten to wig block with T-pins
  14. Style with blowdryer
  15. Store until client picks up, or dry and reattach

🧹 Clean-Up and Disinfection

After every hair replacement service:

  • Clean and disinfect all tools and implements
  • Clean and disinfect the work area
  • Sweep up hair and deposit in closed receptacle
  • Dispose of single-use items
  • Place all used linens, towels, and capes in laundry
  • Dispose of used razor blades in sharps container
  • Dispose of used cleaning solution and solvents per manufacturer's recommendation
  • Wash hands thoroughly

📝 Chapter 15 Key Takeaways

  • Hair replacement has evolved from toupees to modern, natural-looking systems
  • Types: lace-front, non-lace-front, full cap, partial
  • Hair types: human, synthetic, blended
  • Template and plaster mold are used for custom systems
  • Full head bonding uses adhesive to attach systems
  • Perform patch test 24 hours before applying adhesive
  • Regular cleaning and reconditioning extends system life
  • Coloring only on 100% human hair systems
  • Always follow manufacturer's recommendations
