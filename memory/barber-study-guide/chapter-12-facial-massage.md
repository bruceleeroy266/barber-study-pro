# Chapter 12: Men's Facial Massage and Treatments
## Study Guide Notes

---

### Introduction

Providing men's skin care is a **lucrative and growing service**. Male clients make up about **20% of skin care clientele**, with growth expected as spa/salon trends continue.

Historically, barbers performed hot-towel and rolling-cream facials. Today, facials combine **psychological relaxation with physiological benefits** (improved skin tone, texture, and appearance). They also serve as excellent post-shave finishing steps.

**Key Point:** Men's facials help build clientele, increase retention, and generate referrals while meeting modern male grooming demands.

---

### Why Study Men's Facial Massage and Treatments?

- Barbers must be competent in all licensed services
- Men increasingly desire the benefits of facial massage and treatments
- Regular facials produce visible improvements in skin tone, texture, and appearance
- Facials are a natural extension of the shave service

**Key Point:** Mastering facials allows barbers to offer high-value, relaxing services that differentiate their practice.

---

### Review Subdermal Systems (LO1–LO5)

Facial treatments directly affect the **muscles, nerves, arteries, and veins** of the head, face, and neck (cross-reference Chapter 6).

| System | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Muscles** | Fibrous tissues that stretch and contract to produce movement |
| **Nerves** | Long, white cords carrying messages to/from the brain and spinal column |
| **Arteries** | Thick-walled vessels transporting oxygenated blood under high pressure |
| **Veins** | Thin-walled vessels with valves returning deoxygenated blood to the heart |

**Key Point:** Safe and effective facial services require understanding how massage and modalities influence these systems.

---

### Modalities That Affect Muscle Action and Nerve Responses

A facial service can be **calming or stimulating** depending on the modality used:

- Massage (hand or electric massager)
- Electric currents (high-frequency/Tesla, galvanic, microcurrent)
- Light rays (infrared, ultraviolet, LLLT, LEDs)
- Heat (lamps, heating caps, infrared devices, lasers)
- Moist heat (steamers, hot towels)
- Nerve impulses (via nervous system and motor points)
- Chemicals (acids, salts, microdermabrasion)

**Key Point:** Choose the modality based on the client's needs — relaxation vs. stimulation, toning, or deep cleansing.

---

### Muscles of the Scalp, Face, and Neck

**Key voluntary muscles:**

| Muscle | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Frontalis** | Draws scalp forward, creates forehead wrinkles |
| **Orbicularis oculi** | Closes the eyelid |
| **Orbicularis oris** | Puckers and wrinkles lips |
| **Masseter & Temporalis** | Chewing muscles |
| **Platysma** | Depresses lower jaw and lip |
| **Sternocleidomastoideus** | Bends and rotates the head |
| **Trapezius** | Moves shoulders |

**Key Point:** Targeted massage on these muscles produces desired effects (toning, relaxation, or stimulation). Always work **from insertion to origin**.

---

### Main Cranial Nerves Affected by Facial Massage (LO4)

| Nerve | Function |
|-------|----------|
| **Fifth (Trigeminal)** | Chief sensory nerve of the face; also motor for chewing |
| **Seventh (Facial)** | Controls motions of face, scalp, neck, palate, and tongue |
| **Eleventh (Accessory)** | Controls neck muscle motions |
| **Cervical (spinal)** | Can be affected at the back of the head and neck |

**Key Point:** Proper technique avoids nerve irritation while delivering therapeutic benefits.

---

### Arteries and Veins Affected by Facial Massage (LO5)

#### Arteries (oxygenated blood supply):

- Common carotids (main source)
- External maxillary (facial artery)
- Superficial temporal
- Occipital
- Posterior auricular

#### Veins (deoxygenated blood return):

- Internal jugular vein
- External jugular vein

**Key Point:** Facial massage improves circulation through these vessels, nourishing skin and supporting glandular activity.

### Theory of Massage (LO6 & LO7)

Skillfully applied massage influences body structures directly or indirectly.

**Beneficial results include:**

- Nourished skin and tissues
- Stimulated/strengthened muscle fibers
- Soothed/rested nerves
- Increased blood circulation
- Stimulated skin/scalp glands
- Softer, more pliable skin
- Reduced fat cells and occasional pain relief

#### Massage Manipulations:

| Technique | Description | Effect |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **Effleurage** | Light, continuous stroking | Soothing; used with creams/lotions |
| **Pétrissage** | Kneading | Deeper stimulation and circulation |
| **Friction** | Deep rubbing | Benefits circulation and glands |
| **Percussion/Tapotement** | Tapping, slapping, hacking | Stimulating |
| **Vibration** | Rapid shaking | Tones muscles; use sparingly |

#### Guidelines for Facial Massage:

- Always use **upward direction**
- Work rhythmically and systematically
- Conform fingers/palms to facial contours
- Reduce pressure gradually at the end
- Direct movements **with (not against) beard growth**
- Perform thorough consultation and skin analysis first

**Motor Points & Trigger Points:** Areas where nerves control underlying muscles — critical for electrotherapy.

**Key Point:** All movements should be moderate, upward, and rhythmic for safety and maximum benefit.

---

### Facial Equipment & Electrotherapy (LO8)

| Equipment | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Electric Massager** | Produces vibration; use direct or indirect application |
| **Brush Machine** | Mechanical exfoliation and cleansing (use with cream; vary speed/pressure) |
| **Facial Steamer** | Moist heat to soften skin, open pores, and increase circulation (use distilled water) |
| **High-Frequency Machine** (Tesla current) | Produces violet sparks; stimulates circulation, oxygenates skin, antiseptic effect (use glass electrodes) |
| **Galvanic Machine** | Direct current for desincrustation (deep cleansing) and iontophoresis (product penetration) |

#### Cautions for Electrotherapy:

**Avoid on:**
- Heart conditions
- Pacemakers
- Epilepsy
- Pregnancy
- High blood pressure
- Open skin
- Infections
- Metal implants

**Always:**
- Follow manufacturer instructions and state regulations
- Clean and disinfect electrodes properly

**Key Point:** Equipment enhances facials but requires training, client screening, and strict safety protocols.

### Identify Different Facial Treatments & Skin Types (LO9)

#### Facial Types:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Basic Facial** (Scientific Rest Facial) | Foundation treatment — cleansing, exfoliating, massage, toning, mask/pack, moisturizing |
| **Vibratory Facial** | Combines hand massage with electric massager |
| **Rolling Cream Facial** | Variation using rolling cream instead of scrub |

#### Skin Types:

| Type | Characteristics | Needs |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| **Dry** | Insufficient sebum; flaky, small lines | Moisturizing |
| **Normal** | Balanced water-oil; free of blemishes | Maintenance |
| **Oily** | Excess sebum; larger pores, blackheads/acne | Oil control |
| **Combination** | Oily T-zone with drier cheeks | Targeted treatment |

#### Skin Analysis:

- Use magnifying lamp
- Observe type, condition, texture
- Ask questions
- Record notes

#### Guidelines for Successful Facials:

- Help client relax with quiet, professional manner
- Explain benefits during consultation
- Work efficiently in a clean, quiet environment
- Protect eyes, keep nails short, test hot towels
- Avoid excessive product or conversation

**Key Point:** Always match treatment and products to the client's skin type and condition. Refer medical issues (acne, infections) to a physician.

---

### Basic Facial Procedure (Procedure 12-4)

1. Cleanse with cream/lotion using rotary/stroking movements
2. Remove cleanser with warm towel
3. Apply steam towels or steamer to open pores
4. Reapply cleanser and gently massage
5. Apply exfoliating product (or rolling cream in variation)
6. Perform effleurage, pétrissage, and tapotement
7. Remove excess; apply toner/astringent
8. Apply mask/pack and allow to set
9. Remove mask; apply moisturizer and light talc if needed
10. Raise client to sitting position

#### Clean-Up:

- Clean/disinfect tools
- Launder linens
- Wash hands

**Key Point:** Follow a systematic order for consistent, professional results. Adapt as needed for your instructor's or shop's method.
