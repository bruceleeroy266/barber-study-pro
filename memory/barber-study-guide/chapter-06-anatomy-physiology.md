### Veins of the Head, Face, and Neck

Blood returns to the heart from the head, face, and neck via **two principal veins** on each side of the neck:

| Vein | Location | Function |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Internal jugular vein** | Side of the neck | Collects blood from the brain and parts of the face and neck |
| **External jugular vein** | Side of the neck | Carries blood returning to the heart from the head, face, and neck |

**Important:** The most important veins of the face and neck **parallel the arteries** and take the same names.

### Review the Lymphatic/Immune System

The lymphatic/immune system is made up of:
- Lymph
- Lymph nodes
- Thymus gland
- Spleen
- Lymph vessels

**Function:** Carries waste and impurities away from the cells and protects the body from disease by developing immunity and destroying disease-causing microorganisms.

#### Lymph

- **Clear fluid** that circulates in the lymph spaces (lymphatics) of the body
- Helps carry wastes and impurities away from the cells before it is routed back to the circulatory system

#### Connection to Cardiovascular System

- The lymphatic/immune system **drains tissue spaces** of excess interstitial fluid (fluid found in spaces between tissue cells)
- **Closely connected** to the cardiovascular system
- Both transport fluids; difference is lymphatic system transports **lymph**, which eventually returns to the blood

#### Primary Functions:

1. Carry nourishment from the blood to the body cells
2. Act as a defense against toxins and bacteria
3. Remove by-products of infection such as pus and dead tissue
4. Remove waste material from the body cells to the blood
5. Provide a suitable fluid environment for the cells

### Review the Integumentary System

The integumentary system consists of:
- Skin
- Accessory organs (oil and sweat glands, sensory receptors, hair, and nails)

**Function:** Protective covering and helps regulate the body's temperature.

**Integument** = "a natural covering"

Think of skin as a **protective overcoat** against:
- Germs
- Chemicals
- Sun exposure

**Skin is water resistant.**

#### Skin Structures (in 1 square centimeter):

| Structure | Count |
|-----------|-------|
| Sebaceous glands | 15 |
| Hairs | 10 |
| Cells | 3,000,000 |
| Sensory apparatus for heat | 12 |
| Sensory apparatus for cold | 2 |
| Nerve endings for pain | 200 |
| Sweat glands | 700 |
| Sensory cells at nerve fiber ends | 3,000 |
| Nerves | 4 yards |
| Pressure apparatus for touch | 25 |

**Did You Know?** Every minute, you shed about **30,000 to 40,000 dead skin cells**. That can total up to about **40 pounds of skin** in your lifetime.

**Note:** Skin structure and growth are discussed in detail in **Chapter 9, The Skin—Structures, Disorders, and Diseases**.

### Review the Endocrine System

The endocrine system is a group of **specialized glands** that affect:
- Growth
- Development
- Sexual functions
- Health of the entire body

**Glands** = secretory organs that remove and release certain elements from the blood to convert them into new compounds.

#### Two Main Types of Glands:

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Endocrine glands** (ductless) | Release hormonal secretions directly into bloodstream | Thyroid, pituitary glands |
| **Exocrine glands** (duct) | Produce substance that travels through small tubelike ducts | Sweat and oil glands of skin |

#### Hormones

- Secretions (insulin, adrenaline, estrogen) that stimulate functional activity or other secretions in the body
- **Influence the welfare of the entire body**

#### Endocrine Glands Include:

- Pineal gland
- Pituitary gland
- Parathyroid glands
- Thyroid gland
- Thymus gland
- Heart
- Adrenal gland
- Stomach
- Kidney
- Pancreas
- Ovary/Testicle

### Review the Reproductive System

#### Female:
- Ovaries
- Uterine tubes
- Uterus
- Vagina

#### Male:
- Testes
- Prostate gland
- Penis
- Urethra

**Function:** Produces offspring and passes on the genetic code from one generation to another.

#### Hormones and Skin/Hair

The reproductive system produces hormones:
- **Females:** Primarily estrogen
- **Males:** Primarily testosterone

**These hormones affect and change the skin:**
- Acne
- Loss of scalp hair
- Facial hair growth and color
- Darker skin pigmentations
