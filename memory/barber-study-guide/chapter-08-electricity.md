### Introduction & Why Study Basics of Electricity?

In today's barbershop, haircutting and other services would be very uncomfortable without electricity. Most barbers rely on electrical tools daily (clippers, trimmers, dryers, hot towel cabinets, etc.).

#### Why Study It?

- Barbers use electrical appliances and must know how to use them **safely**
- Special training required to safely repair electrical tools (improper repair can cause shocks, fires, or injury)
- Periodic inspections minimize fire risk from faulty wiring or overloaded outlets
- Light-therapy and electrotherapy treatments are additional services barbers can offer clients

### Electricity

**Not matter** — does not occupy space or have mass. It is a **form of energy** that produces physical, magnetic, chemical, or thermal effects when in motion.

**Created by the flow of electrons between atoms.**

---

### Electric Current & Circuits

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Electric current** | Flow of electricity along a conductor |
| **Conductor** | Substance that easily transmits electricity (most metals, carbon, human body, watery solutions of acids and salts) |
| **Insulator (nonconductor)** | Does not easily transmit electricity (rubber, silk, dry wood, glass, cement, plastic) |
| **Electric wire** | Twisted metal threads (conductor) coated with insulator (silk, plastic, rubber) |
| **Complete electric circuit** | Path of electric current from source through conductors and back to source |

---

### Types of Electric Current

| Type | Characteristics | Production | Examples |
|------|-----------------|------------|----------|
| **Direct Current (DC)** | Constant, even flow in one direction only | Chemical means | Battery-operated tools (flashlights, cordless clippers) |
| **Alternating Current (AC)** | Rapid, interrupted flow that reverses direction | Mechanical means | Tools that plug into wall outlets (clippers, hair dryers) |

**Converter / Rectifier:** Changes DC to AC or AC to DC (used in rechargeable cordless tools and battery chargers).

---

### Electrical Measurements

| Unit | Measures | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Volt (V)** | Electrical pressure | Pushes electrons forward; higher voltage = more pressure |
| **Ampere (A) or Amp** | Strength/rate of current | |
| **Milliampere** | 1/1,000 of an ampere | Used for facial/scalp treatments |
| **Ohm (Ω)** | Electrical resistance | Higher resistance = less current flow |
| **Watt (W)** | Power | Amps × volts; indicates energy used per second |
| **Kilowatt (kW)** | 1,000 watts | Household electricity measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh) |

**Rheostat:** Adjustable resistor for controlling current (used in light dimmers).

### Practice Electrical Equipment Safety (LO3)

#### Safety Devices

| Device | Function | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| **Fuse** | Prevents overheating by melting when too much current passes | Must be replaced; common in older buildings |
| **Circuit Breaker** | Automatically interrupts circuit on overload | Can be reset; common in modern buildings |
| **Grounding** | Provides safe path for electricity in case of short circuit | Modern plugs have grounding pin (third prong) |
| **GFCI (Ground Fault Interrupter)** | Senses imbalances and shuts off power to prevent shock | Required near sinks/water sources |
| **UL Symbol** | Indicates equipment meets safety standards | Look for this mark |

#### Guidelines for Safe Use

- Study instructions before using equipment
- Disconnect when not in use
- Keep cords, plugs, and equipment in good repair
- Inspect frequently
- Do not overload outlets or power strips
- Protect clients at all times
- **Never:**
  - Touch metal while using electrical equipment
  - Leave client unattended with electrical device
  - Use near water or with wet hands

**Caution:** Older buildings may have two-prong outlets. Consult manufacturer for adapters.

### Understand Terminology Associated with Electrotherapy (LO4)

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Electrotherapy** | Electronic facial and scalp treatments using different types of electric currents (modalities) |
| **Polarity** | Negative or positive pole of electric current |
| **Anode (+)** | Positive pole (usually red) |
| **Cathode (–)** | Negative pole (usually black) |

**Modalities** (depending on state regulations): Galvanic current, microcurrent, Tesla high-frequency current.

#### Galvanic Current

- **DC, low-voltage, safe level**
- Produces chemical changes and ionic reactions
- Uses active electrode (positive or negative) on treatment area

| Pole | Effect | Process |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Positive (Anode)** | Acidic reactions, closes pores, soothes nerves, decreases blood supply, contracts vessels, hardens/firms tissues | **Cataphoresis** |
| **Negative (Cathode)** | Alkaline reactions, opens pores, stimulates/irritates nerves, increases blood supply, expands vessels, softens tissues | **Anaphoresis** |

**Desincrustation:** Uses galvanic current for deep pore cleansing (emulsifies sebum).

**Iontophoresis:** Introduces water-soluble products into skin (positive pole for acidic substances, negative for alkaline).

#### Microcurrent

- Extremely low level of electricity mirroring body's natural impulses
- Improves circulation, tones, soothes, heals inflamed tissue

#### Tesla High-Frequency Current

- High rate of oscillation producing heat and violet ray
- No muscular contractions
- Used for scalp and facial treatments

**Benefits:**
- Stimulates blood circulation and glandular activity
- Increases metabolism and waste elimination
- Improves germicidal action
- Relieves skin congestion
- Opens/closes follicles and pores
- Increases muscle tone and elasticity
- Reduces redness and inflammation

**Cautions:**
- Polarity tests can be dangerous — never perform without instructor supervision
- Do not touch metal while using galvanic current on broken capillaries or high blood pressure/metal implants

### Explain Light Energy and Light Therapy (LO5 & LO6)

#### Electromagnetic Spectrum

All forms of energy that travel as waves:
- Radio waves
- Microwaves
- Infrared
- Visible light
- Ultraviolet
- X-rays
- Gamma rays

**Long vs. Short Wavelengths:**
- **Long wavelengths:** Deeper penetration, less energy
- **Short wavelengths:** Less penetration, more energy

#### Visible Spectrum of Light

- Part of electromagnetic spectrum
- Makes up **35% of natural sunlight**
- Colors: **red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, violet**
- Produced when light passes through a prism

#### Invisible Light (65% of sunlight)

**Ultraviolet (UV) rays:**
- Short wavelength, high frequency, cold light
- Kills microorganisms, causes chemical reactions

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **UVA** | Longest UV rays, used in tanning, penetrates deeply, causes aging/wrinkling |
| **UVB** | Middle range, causes burning and skin cancer |
| **UVC** | Absorbed by atmosphere; used in germicidal lamps |

**Infrared rays:**
- Longer wavelength
- Produces heat
- Penetrates deeply

---

### Light Therapy (Phototherapy)

Application of specific light wavelengths to treat skin conditions.

#### LED (Light-Emitting Diode)

Releases different colors of light to stimulate responses:

| Color | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Blue** | Reduces acne and bacteria |
| **Red** | Increases circulation, collagen, elastin; stimulates wound healing |
| **Yellow** | Reduces swelling/inflammation, improves lymphatic flow |
| **Green** | Reduces hyperpigmentation and redness, calms/soothes |

#### Therapeutic Lamps

Dome-shaped reflector lamps with colored bulbs:

| Color | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **White** | Relieves pain, relaxes muscles, chemical/germicidal effects |
| **Blue** | Tonic effect on bare skin |
| **Red** | Heat rays, stimulates penetration of creams |
| **Ultraviolet** | Treats acne, seborrhea, dandruff (30–36 inches distance; short exposure times) |
| **Infrared** | Produces heat (rosy glow); applied at ~30 inches |

#### Cautions for Light Therapy

- Never leave client unattended
- Protect eyes with goggles or moistened cotton pads
- Check skin products for contraindications
- Break light path frequently by waving hand
- Exposure time usually **≤5 minutes**

#### Lasers

Medical devices using electromagnetic radiation for hair removal and skin treatments.

- Work by **selective photothermolysis** (light → heat)
- Classified as Level II or higher medical devices
- Usually require physician supervision

### Barbering Takeaways from Chapter 8

1. **Electricity is essential but dangerous** if misused — always prioritize safety and client protection
2. Understand **conductors/insulators, DC vs. AC, polarity**, and basic measurements (volt, amp, ohm, watt)
3. Use **safety devices** (fuses, breakers, GFCI, grounding) and follow strict guidelines
4. **Electrotherapy and light therapy** can be valuable add-on services when permitted by state law and performed with proper training
5. **Light wavelengths** (visible and invisible) produce specific effects on skin — know the differences between UV, infrared, LED colors, etc.

### KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

1. **Electricity** = flow of electrons; not matter, it's energy
2. **Conductor** = transmits electricity easily; **Insulator** = does not
3. **DC** = one direction (battery); **AC** = reverses direction (wall outlet)
4. **Volt** = pressure; **Amp** = current strength; **Ohm** = resistance; **Watt** = power
5. **GFCI** = required near water sources
6. **Anode (+)** = positive; **Cathode (–)** = negative
7. **Galvanic current** = DC, produces chemical changes
8. **Tesla high-frequency** = produces heat and violet ray, no muscle contractions
9. **Visible light** = 35% of sunlight; **Invisible** = 65%
10. **UVA** = tanning/aging; **UVB** = burning/cancer; **UVC** = germicidal
11. **LED colors:** Blue (acne), Red (circulation/healing), Yellow (swelling), Green (pigmentation)
12. **Light therapy exposure** usually ≤5 minutes
