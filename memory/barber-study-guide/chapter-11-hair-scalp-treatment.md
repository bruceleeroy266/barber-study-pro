# Chapter 11: Treatment of the Hair and Scalp
## Study Guide Notes

---

### Introduction

The treatment of the hair and scalp includes **shampooing, scalp massage, conditioning, and special treatments** for hair and scalp conditions.

When performed correctly, these services are relaxing and effective in helping maintain the health of clients' hair and scalp. Professional delivery also increases client retention, referrals, and the barber's positive reputation.

**Key Point:** These services promote cleanliness, circulation, relaxation, and healthy hair/scalp while building client loyalty.

---

### Why Study Treatment of the Hair and Scalp?

Barbers should study and understand these treatments because:
- Analyzing the client's hair and scalp helps identify conditions that may prohibit services
- Professionally delivered scalp massage during shampooing provides hygiene, circulatory, and relaxation benefits
- Special services (e.g., electrotherapy or light therapy) require advanced knowledge for safe performance

**Key Point:** Thorough analysis and proper technique prevent service on contraindicated conditions and ensure client safety and satisfaction.

---

### Discuss the Shampoo Service (LO1 & LO2)

#### Benefits of Shampooing:

- Removes oils, dirt, and product buildup that interfere with cutting tools and results
- Prepares clean hair for cutting, styling, or chemical services
- Followed by conditioning to keep hair healthy and manageable

#### Review Shampoos and Conditioners:

| Product | pH | Characteristics |
|---------|----|-----------------|
| **Shampoos** | 4.5–7.5 | Oil-in-water emulsions; gentler than soap; leave hair manageable |
| **Hair conditioners** | 3.0–5.5 | Restore oils/proteins and moisturize |
| **Scalp conditioners** | Varies | Usually creams/ointments for maintenance or specific issues |

#### Matching Products to Hair Types:

| Hair Type | Shampoo | Conditioner/Treatment |
|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| **Fine/Straight** | Volumizing | Detangler if needed, protein treatments |
| **Medium** | pH-balanced | Finishing rinse, protein treatments |
| **Coarse** | Moisturizing | Leave-in conditioner, moisturizing treatments |
| **Wavy/Curly/Extremely Curly** | Light formula | Light leave-in conditioner, moisturizing treatment |
| **Dry & Damaged** (perms, color, heat) | Gentle cleansing | Protein & moisturizing repair treatments, thermal protection |

**Key Point:** Always match shampoo/conditioner to hair texture, condition, density, porosity, and elasticity. Read labels and follow manufacturer directions.

### Know How to Drape (LO3)

Draping protects the client's skin and clothing with a cape and a neck barrier (towel or neck strip). Proper draping maintains compliance with state barber laws and infection control standards.

#### Two Main Drape Types:

| Type | Material | Use |
|------|----------|-----|
| **Shampoo (Wet) Cape** | Waterproof vinyl | Protects from water, liquids, and chemicals |
| **Haircutting Cape** | Nylon/synthetic | Less heat-retaining; better for shedding dry hair |

#### Important Draping Guidelines:

- Prepare materials and supplies
- Wash hands
- Ask client to remove jewelry
- Turn client's collar inside if applicable
- **Always use a towel or neck strip as a barrier between skin and cape**

#### Procedure 11-1: Draping for Wet and Chemical Services:

1. Hold towel lengthwise, fold diagonally
2. Place around neck/shoulders, cross ends under chin
3. Drape waterproof cape over towel and fasten at back
4. Flatten towel edge over neckline
5. Optional: Add second towel for extra barrier at sink

#### Procedure 11-2: Draping for Haircutting Services:

1. Drape nylon cape loosely across chest and shoulders
2. Place neck strip around neck from front to back
3. Hold one end against skin while wrapping the rest
4. Secure second end by tucking neatly
5. Lift cape, slide into place, fasten at back
6. Fold neck strip edge over neckline to prevent skin contact

**Key Point:** Never let the cape touch the client's skin directly — a towel or neck strip barrier is mandatory for safety and infection control.

### Understand the Shampoo Service (LO4, LO5, LO6)

#### Basic Considerations:

- Proper draping and client positioning
- Scalp manipulations during shampoo
- Barber's physical posture

#### Two Shampooing Methods:

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Reclined Method** (most common) | Client in hydraulic chair with head in bowl (faster, more comfortable) |
| **Inclined Method** | Client bends forward over sink (used when reclined bowl unavailable) |

**Shampooing Clients with Special Needs:** Wheelchair clients may use inclined method or stay seated; prioritize comfort and safety.

#### Barber's Physical Presentation (prevent strain/fatigue):

- Stand close to client's head
- Flex knees, keep body balanced over feet
- Chin parallel to floor, shoulders relaxed
- Avoid bending or twisting at waist

#### Water Temperature:

- **Test before applying** (comfortably warm)
- Cold reduces lathering
- Hot can burn or dry scalp

#### Shampoo Application Guidelines:

1. Dampen hair thoroughly
2. Distribute shampoo evenly with palms
3. Work into rich lather
4. Use rotary and back-and-forth movements
5. Rinse thoroughly; repeat if needed
6. Apply conditioner, massage, and rinse

#### Procedure 11-3: Shampoo and Shampoo Massage Manipulations:

1. Drape client
2. Consult and analyze hair/scalp
3. Position client; saturate with warm water (protect face/ears)
4. Apply shampoo; massage with rotary movements over entire scalp
5. Remove lather; rinse thoroughly
6. Apply conditioner, massage, rinse
7. Blot hair, raise client, towel-dry lightly, comb into position

#### Shampoo Variations (Procedure 11-4):

| Type | Use |
|------|-----|
| **Liquid-Dry Shampoo** | Applied with cotton for clients unable to get wet service |
| **Dry/Powder Shampoo** | Sprinkled on and brushed out |

**Key Points:**
- Thorough rinsing prevents residue
- Good posture prevents barber injury
- Document out-of-shop services in appointment book

### Scalp and Hair Treatments (LO7 & LO8)

**Purpose:** Preserve health and appearance of hair/scalp; prevent or correct disorders (dandruff, dryness, oiliness, alopecia).

#### Massage Manipulations (Figures 11-9 to 11-11):

| Movement | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Rotary** | Circular movements with thumbs/fingertips |
| **Sliding** | Straight strokes with thumbs/fingertips |
| **Back-and-forth** | Brisk movement with moderate to firm pressure |

#### Guidelines for Scalp Massage:

- Firm pressure on upward strokes
- Slow, rhythmic movements
- Hands under hair with fingertips on scalp
- Avoid pulling hair

---

### Scalp Treatment Variations (Procedure 11-7)

#### A. Treatment for Healthy Scalp and Hair:

1. Brush to loosen dead skin
2. Apply conditioner/ointment
3. Infrared lamp 3–5 min
4. Massage 10 min
5. Optional: High-frequency current 2–3 min
6. Shampoo and style

#### B. Treatment for Dry Scalp and Hair:

1. Brush and massage
2. Steam 7–10 min
3. Mild shampoo for dry hair
4. Apply scalp cream sparingly
5. Infrared lamp and high-frequency (if no alcohol)

**Caution:** Alcohol products only after high-frequency.

#### C. Treatment for Oily Scalp and Hair:

1. Gentle brushing
2. Medicated lotion/astringent to scalp only
3. Infrared or steamer
4. Balancing shampoo
5. High-frequency current and medicated lotion

#### D. Treatment for Dandruff:

1. Antidandruff shampoo/conditioner and antiseptic products
2. Steam 3–5 min, massage
3. Use UV rays (with safety goggles) or infrared
4. Apply leave-in antiseptic

#### E. Scalp Treatment for Alopecia:

1. Regular manipulations and appropriate shampoo
2. UV rays (with goggles)
3. Prescribed non-alcohol medicated ointment
4. Optional: Indirect high-frequency

#### F. Treatment for Dry or Damaged Hair:

1. Massage and mild shampoo
2. Deep-conditioning treatment under dryer (10 min)
3. Rinse and style

---

### Clean-Up and Disinfection for All Treatments:

- Clean/disinfect tools and work area
- Launder linens, towels, and capes
- Dispose of single-use items
- Wash hands

---

### Key Points for Scalp Treatments:

- Always perform hair/scalp analysis first
- Use safety goggles with UV or high-frequency current
- **Refer serious conditions** (infections, parasitic issues, open lesions) to a physician — barbers are prohibited from treating them
- Regular treatments help prevent baldness and maintain scalp health

---

### Overall Chapter Key Takeaways

- Draping is mandatory for safety and compliance
- Product selection must match hair type and condition
- Scalp massage improves circulation, relaxes the client, and supports healthy hair growth
- Special treatments are tailored to specific conditions (dry, oily, dandruff, alopecia, damaged hair)
- Client comfort, proper barber posture, and infection control are priorities in every service

### KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

1. **Shampoo pH:** 4.5–7.5; **Conditioner pH:** 3.0–5.5
2. **Two draping types:** Shampoo (waterproof vinyl) and Haircutting (nylon)
3. **Never let cape touch skin** — use towel or neck strip barrier
4. **Two shampoo methods:** Reclined (most common) and Inclined
5. **Test water temperature** before applying to client
6. **Three massage movements:** Rotary, Sliding, Back-and-forth
7. **Use safety goggles** with UV or high-frequency current
8. **Refer to physician:** Infections, parasites, open lesions
9. **Treatment types:** Healthy, Dry, Oily, Dandruff, Alopecia, Damaged
10. **Clean and disinfect** after every treatment
