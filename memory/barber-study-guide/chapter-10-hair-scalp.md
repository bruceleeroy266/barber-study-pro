# Chapter 10: Properties and Disorders of the Hair and Scalp
## Study Guide Notes

---

### Introduction

The scientific study of hair, its disorders, and care is called **trichology**.

Hair is part of the integumentary system that protects the head from heat, cold, and injury while serving as adornment and personal style.

**Key Point:** Barbers must understand hair and scalp to safely perform services and recognize when to refer clients.

---

### Why Study Properties and Disorders of the Hair and Scalp?

- Know how products and chemicals affect hair structure
- Maintain healthy hair and scalp; differentiate normal vs. abnormal conditions
- Perform accurate scalp and hair analysis before every service

**Key Point:** Proper analysis prevents damage and ensures safe, effective services.

---

### Discover the Structure of Hair (LO1 & LO2)

Hair is a **keratinized appendage** of the skin consisting of a **root and shaft**.

---

### Structures of the Hair Root (Figure 10-1)

| Structure | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Hair follicle** | Tubelike depression containing the root |
| **Hair bulb** | Club-shaped base covering the dermal papilla |
| **Dermal papilla** | "Mother of the hair" – supplies blood and nerves for growth |
| **Arrector pili muscle** | Causes goose bumps when contracted |
| **Sebaceous gland** | Produces sebum to lubricate hair and skin |

---

### Structures of the Hair Shaft (Figure 10-2)

| Layer | Description | Percentage |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| **Cuticle** | Outermost layer of overlapping scales; protects inner structure and provides shine | |
| **Cortex** | Main layer; contains melanin for color, strength, and elasticity | ~90% of hair weight |
| **Medulla** | Innermost layer; may be absent in fine or blond hair | |

**Key Points:**
- Hair is ~**90% keratin protein**
- The **cortex** is the target of all chemical services (color, perm, relaxer)
- The **cuticle** must be intact for healthy hair; damage raises scales and increases porosity

---

### Chemical Composition and Side Bonds (LO3)

Hair is composed of **amino acids** joined into **polypeptide chains**.

#### Three Main Side Bonds of the Cortex:

| Bond | Type | Strength | Broken By | Reformed By |
|------|------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Hydrogen bond** | Physical, weak | ~1/3 | Water or heat | Drying/cooling |
| **Salt bond** | Physical, weak | ~1/3 | Acids/alkalis | Normalizing pH |
| **Disulfide bond (sulfur bond)** | Chemical, strong | ~1/3 | Permanent waves, chemical relaxers | Neutralizers |

**Peptide (end) bonds:** Strong; broken only by cutting or depilatories.

**Key Points:**
- Hydrogen and salt bonds allow **temporary styling** (wet setting, blow-drying)
- Disulfide bonds determine **permanent changes** — the more disulfide bonds, the more resistant the hair
- Extreme heat can break disulfide bonds
- Water converts disulfide bonds to **lanthionine** in hydroxide relaxers

---

### Hair Pigment & Wave Pattern

#### Melanin:

| Type | Color |
|------|-------|
| **Eumelanin** | Brown/black |
| **Pheomelanin** | Red/yellow |

Gray/white hair has little or no melanin.

#### Wave Pattern:

Determined by **cross-section shape**:

| Shape | Pattern |
|-------|---------|
| **Round** | Straight |
| **Oval** | Wavy/curly |
| **Elliptical** | Extremely curly |

**Key Point:** Extremely curly hair has **low elasticity**, breaks easily, and requires gentle handling and conditioning.

---

### Hair Growth and Shedding (LO4)

#### Hair Types:

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Vellus hair** | Fine, short, unpigmented |
| **Terminal hair** | Long, coarse, pigmented |

#### Growth Cycle:

| Phase | Duration | Characteristics |
|-------|----------|-----------------|
| **Anagen** (growth) | 3–5+ years | 90% of scalp hair; active growth |
| **Catagen** (transition) | ~2 weeks | Follicle shrinks |
| **Telogen** (resting) | 3–6 months | Normal shedding occurs (75–100 hairs/day) |

**Key Points:**
- Normal daily shedding is healthy; new hair replaces shed hair
- Growth rate averages **½ inch per month**
- Influenced by: genetics, age, hormones, nutrition, and health

---

### Understand Hair Loss (LO5 & LO6)

#### Types of Abnormal Hair Loss (Alopecia):

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Androgenic alopecia** (pattern baldness) | Genetic + hormonal; causes follicle miniaturization |
| **Alopecia areata** | Sudden round patches (autoimmune) |
| **Alopecia totalis/universalis** | Complete scalp or body loss |

#### FDA-Approved Treatments:

| Treatment | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| **Minoxidil** (topical) | Stimulates growth for men and women |
| **Finasteride** (oral) | Slows loss and promotes regrowth (men only) |

**Key Point:** Early detection and medical referral are important; emotional impact of hair loss can be significant.

### Hair and Scalp Analysis (LO9)

**Always begin with scalp analysis before any service.**

#### Key Factors to Assess:

| Sense | What to Check |
|-------|---------------|
| **Sight** | Dry/oily scalp, lesions, parasites, inflammation |
| **Touch** | Texture, density, porosity, elasticity |
| **Smell & Hearing** | Client history and unusual odors |

#### Hair Texture:

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **Coarse** | Strong |
| **Medium** | Most common |
| **Fine** | Fragile |

#### Hair Density:

Number of hairs per square inch: **thick, average, thin**

#### Hair Porosity (Figures 10-26 to 10-28):

Ability to absorb moisture.

| Level | Characteristics |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Resistant (low)** | Cuticle compact, smooth |
| **Normal** | Balanced |
| **Porous/over-porous (high)** | Raised scales, absorbs quickly, feels rough, breaks easily |

#### Hair Elasticity (Figure 10-29):

Ability to stretch and return without breaking.

| Level | Result |
|-------|--------|
| **Normal** | Returns to original length |
| **Low** | Breaks easily (often from over-processing) |

#### Key Points for Analysis:

- Test porosity by sliding fingers down a dry strand (smooth = resistant; rough = porous)
- Test elasticity by gently tugging wet strands
- **Do not proceed** with chemical services on irritated skin, parasites, or unknown lesions — **refer to a physician**

### Common Disorders of Hair and Scalp (LO7 & LO8)

#### Hair Disorders:

| Disorder | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Canities** | Graying |
| **Hypertrichosis** | Excessive hair growth |
| **Split ends** | Trichoptilosis |
| **Beaded hair** | Monilethrix |

#### Scalp Disorders:

| Disorder | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Dandruff** | Pityriasis simplex or steatoides |
| **Tinea capitis** | Ringworm of scalp (fungal) |
| **Tinea barbae** | Ringworm of beard (fungal) |
| **Favosa** | Ringworm (fungal) |
| **Head lice** | Pediculosis capitis (parasitic) |
| **Scabies** | Itch mite (parasitic) |
| **Folliculitis barbae** | Bacterial infection of hair follicles |
| **Pseudofolliculitis** (razor bumps) | Ingrown hairs |
| **Furuncles** | Boils (bacterial) |
| **Carbuncles** | Cluster of boils (bacterial) |

**Key Points:**
- Many disorders are **contagious** — do not perform services
- **Refer to a physician** and use proper disinfection
- Barbers must recognize conditions that **prohibit services** for client safety

### KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

1. **Trichology** = scientific study of hair
2. **Hair is 90% keratin protein**
3. **Cortex** = target of all chemical services (~90% of hair weight)
4. **Three side bonds:** Hydrogen (weak, water/heat), Salt (weak, pH), Disulfide (strong, chemical)
5. **Hydrogen and salt bonds** = temporary styling; **Disulfide bonds** = permanent changes
6. **Melanin types:** Eumelanin (brown/black), Pheomelanin (red/yellow)
7. **Wave pattern** determined by cross-section: Round = straight, Oval = wavy, Elliptical = curly
8. **Growth phases:** Anagen (growth), Catagen (transition), Telogen (resting)
9. **Normal shedding:** 75–100 hairs/day
10. **Growth rate:** ~½ inch per month
11. **Hair analysis factors:** Texture, density, porosity, elasticity
12. **Porosity test:** Slide fingers down dry strand (smooth = resistant, rough = porous)
13. **Never proceed** with chemical services on irritated skin or parasites — **refer to physician**
14. **Contagious conditions:** Ringworm, lice, scabies, some bacterial infections
