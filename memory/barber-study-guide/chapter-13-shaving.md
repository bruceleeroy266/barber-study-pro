# Chapter 13: Shaving and Facial-Hair Design
## Study Guide Notes

---

### Introduction

When performed correctly, a full facial shave (complete with hot towels, lotions, and massage) is one of the most **relaxing and rejuvenating services** men enjoy in the barbershop.

Although safety and electric razors have made home shaving more convenient, they do not provide the preparation, relaxation, and finishing elements of a well-executed barbershop shave.

Clients also benefit from professional mustache and beard trims, as the barber is better trained to create a balanced and even facial-hair design.

**Key Point:** The professional shave is a signature barber service that combines skill, client comfort, and artistic precision.

---

### Why Study Shaving and Facial-Hair Design?

- Shaving requires careful attention, skill, and practice to perfect
- Shaving and facial-hair designs require knowledge of bone structure and facial features
- Shaving is a traditional skill that separates barbering from other professions and helps ensure the longevity of the trade

**Caution:** Student barbers must read and study the entire shaving section before practicing a facial shave.

**Key Point:** Proper technique prevents injury, protects client skin, and maintains the professional standards of barbering.

---

### Fundamentals of Shaving (LO1–LO3)

Shaving's primary objective is to **remove visible facial and neck hair without causing irritation to the skin**.

**Tools used:**
- Changeable-blade or conventional straight razors
- Hot towels
- Warm lather

#### Basic Guidelines for Shaving a Client:

- Analyze the client's skin and hair growth patterns before beginning
- **Do not proceed** if skin shows infection, pustules, chapping, or irritation
- Keep the client's skin moist; keep your fingers dry
- Position the client's head as needed and remove excess lather with the thumb before each stroke
- Right-handed barbers stand at the client's right side; left-handed at the left side
- Shift body weight as needed and use gliding strokes leading with the point of the blade

#### Hair Growth Considerations:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Grain** | Direction hair grows |
| **Grain change** | Where hair growing in one direction meets hair growing in the opposite direction |
| **Growth patterns** | Determine hairline shapes, whorls, and cowlicks |

The direction of growth dictates the razor position and stroke used.

#### Hair Type Considerations:

- **Curly facial hair:** Grows in loops and can cause ingrown hairs (pseudofolliculitis) if shaved incorrectly
- **Heavy beards:** May require more thorough lathering and extra hot-towel applications

#### Customer Satisfaction Issues (common complaints):

- Dull or rough razors
- Unclean hands/towels/drape
- Cold fingers
- Heavy touch
- Poorly heated towels or lather
- Glaring lights
- Unshaven patches
- Skin scraping
- Offensive body odor or breath

**Key Point:** Always analyze skin and hair growth first. Shave with the grain on the first pass for safety and comfort.

---

### The 14 Shaving Areas of the Face (LO2)

There are **14 distinct shaving areas** of the face. These areas are shaved systematically and sequentially using a specific razor position to shave **with the grain** in each area.

**Key Point:** Divide the face into the 14 areas and follow the natural grain in each to minimize irritation, nicks, and ingrown hairs.

---

### Razor Positions and Strokes (LO3)

#### Four Razor Positions:

1. **Freehand** (most common)
2. **Backhand**
3. **Reverse freehand**
4. **Reverse backhand**

#### Holding the Razor (Straight Razor – Procedure 13-1):

**Right hand:**
- Handle rests between the 3rd and 4th fingers
- Thumb on the shank near the shoulder
- 3rd finger on the pivot

**Left hand:**
- Keeps skin taut in the opposite direction of the stroke
- Fingers dry to prevent slipping

#### Strokes:

- Use a **gliding forward motion** leading with the point of the razor
- Stroke length: **1–3 inches**
- Adjust speed and pressure to hair density and area sensitivity
- Use smooth, continuous strokes without stopping and starting

**Key Point:** Master all four positions and strokes through repeated practice on a mannequin before working on live clients.

---

### Body Positioning

- Right-handed barbers stand at the client's right side; left-handed at the left side
- Common stances: slightly in front of the right side, centered at the right side, at the right shoulder, or behind the right shoulder
- Gently turn the client's head and shift body weight for control and comfort

---

### The Professional Shave – Procedure 13-3 (LO8)

#### Three Main Steps:

1. **Preparation**
2. **Shaving** (First-Time-Over + optional Second-Time-Over)
3. **Finishing**

#### A. Prepare Client for the Shave:

1. Seat client comfortably and loosen collar if needed
2. Drape with cape and terry towel (diagonal across chest, tucked securely)
3. Apply fresh headrest cover and adjust height
4. Recline and lock the chair
5. Wash hands
6. Tuck towel around neck and secure with paper strip or towel in neckband
7. Analyze skin, texture, and hair growth patterns

#### B. Prepare Client's Face for Shaving:

1. Apply pre-warmed hot towel (test temperature on wrist first; avoid on sensitive skin)
2. Remove towel and work warm lather evenly into the beard using rotary motions
3. Re-lather as needed during the shave

#### C. The Facial Shave (First-Time-Over Shave):

Shave the 14 areas systematically (with the grain):

| Area | Position |
|------|----------|
| Area 1 | Freehand (right sideburn to jawbone) |
| Area 2 | Backhand |
| Areas 3-14 | Continue through all areas using correct position and stroke for each |

- Wipe razor clean after each stroke
- Stretch skin properly and use light touch, especially around nostrils, upper lip, and lower neck
- Re-lather as needed

#### Second-Time-Over Shave:

1. Dampen face and check for rough or uneven spots
2. Use light freehand strokes (with or across the grain) to remove residual hair
3. Remove towel and apply a clean towel across the chest

#### D. Finishing Steps:

1. Apply light facial cream or moisturizer with effleurage and pétrissage massage movements
2. Apply a moderately warm towel, then pat the face dry
3. Apply skin toner or mild astringent with cotton pledgets (pat gently)
4. Dust a small amount of talcum powder if desired
5. Return client to sitting position
6. Comb hair neatly and remove any loose hair, lather, or powder
7. Offer a neck shave if desired

#### Clean-Up and Disinfection:

- Clean and disinfect tools and work area
- Dispose of blades in a sharps container and single-use items properly
- Launder all linens, towels, and capes
- Wash hands

**Key Point:** The full professional shave is a complete service: thorough preparation, systematic shaving with the grain, and relaxing finishing steps for maximum client satisfaction and safety.

---

### The Neck Shave (Procedure 13-4)

1. Raise chair to upright position after the facial shave
2. Tuck a towel around the back of the neck
3. Apply lather
4. Shave the right side with a freehand stroke; left side with a reverse-backhand stroke
5. Clean the shaved neckline with witch hazel or antiseptic and dry thoroughly

**Key Point:** The neck shave completes the service and follows the natural hairline for a clean, professional finish.

---

### Mustache Trim (Procedure 13-5)

1. Drape client as for a haircut service
2. Consult on desired shape and length
3. Trim to even length with outliner
4. Remove bulk using shears-over-comb or clipper-over-comb
5. Shape and detail with outliner or razor, blending into the beard if present
6. Wipe excess product and proceed with final outlining

**Key Point:** Mustache design should complement the client's facial features, nose prominence, mouth width, and personal preference.

---

### Beard Designs (Procedure 13-6)

#### Beard Trim on Medium-Textured Facial Hair:

1. Comb through the beard and check for moles or growths
2. Apply fresh headrest cover
3. Trim excess with shears, clippers, outliner, and razor
4. Establish design lines starting at the center under the chin, then work outward
5. Blend and taper carefully

#### Beard Trim on Curly-Textured Facial Hair:

1. Use clipper-over-comb technique for excess hair
2. Trim and shape mustache
3. Establish design lines under chin, jaw, sideburns, and cheeks
4. Contour carefully

#### Beard Redesign Examples:

| Style | Technique |
|-------|-----------|
| **Full Goatee** | Remove cheek and side hair, contour the goatee line |
| **Chin Goatee** | Remove excess under chin, shape mustache, and refine the patch |

**Key Point:** Beard design balances facial features, follows natural growth patterns, and considers the client's desired length, density, and daily maintenance. Always consult the client and use proper tools for even, blended results.

### Overall Chapter Key Takeaways

- Shaving is a foundational skill requiring careful skin and hair growth analysis
- Master the 14 shaving areas, four razor positions (freehand, backhand, reverse freehand, reverse backhand), and proper strokes — always leading with the point in a gliding motion

- The professional shave consists of preparation, systematic shaving (first-time-over + optional second-time-over), and relaxing finishing steps
- Facial-hair design (mustache and beard) is an artistic service that enhances facial balance and individuality
