# Chapter 7: Basics of Chemistry
## Study Guide Notes

### Introduction & Why Study Basics of Chemistry?

This chapter provides an overview of basic chemistry so barbers can understand and safely use chemical products daily in the barbershop.

#### Why Study It?

- Enables **safe and effective use** of professional products
- Chemicals are common in cleaners, disinfectants, hair care, and skin care items
- Helps **select the best products** for desired results on hair and skin
- Prevents or solves problems during services
- **Permanent changes** to hair structure (coloring, perming, relaxing) require chemical knowledge — without chemicals, permanent change is impossible

### Organic vs. Inorganic Chemistry (LO1)

**Chemistry:** Science of the composition, structure, and properties of matter and how it changes.

| Type | Definition | Examples | Burns? |
|------|------------|----------|--------|
| **Organic chemistry** | Substances containing carbon (all living things or once-alive) | Gasoline, plastics, pesticides | Yes |
| **Inorganic chemistry** | Substances without carbon (may contain hydrogen) | Metals, minerals, water, air, ammonia | No |

---

### Matter and Its Properties (LO2 & LO3)

**Matter:** Anything that occupies space (volume) and has mass (weight). Exists as solid, liquid, or gas.

#### Properties of Matter:

| Type | Definition | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Physical properties** | Observed without chemical reaction | Color, odor, density, melting/boiling point, hardness, solubility |
| **Chemical properties** | Observed only via chemical reaction that changes the substance | Rusting, burning |

#### Building Blocks:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Elements** | Simplest form of matter; one type of atom (118 known; 98 natural) |
| **Atoms** | Smallest particle retaining chemical identity (protons +, neutrons, electrons –) |
| **Molecules** | Two or more atoms combined in fixed proportions (elemental or compound) |
| **Compounds** | Different elements chemically united with fixed composition |

#### States of Matter:

- Solid, liquid, gas — changed by temperature/pressure (physical changes)
- **Example:** Water → ice (freezing) → water (melting) → steam (boiling)

#### Types of Changes:

| Change | Result | Examples |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Physical change** | Form changes, no new substance | Ice melting, temporary hair color |
| **Chemical change** | New substances formed | Haircolor oxidation, rusting |

### Oxidation-Reduction (Redox) Reactions (LO4)

| Process | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **Oxidation** | Addition of oxygen or loss of hydrogen (substance is oxidized) |
| **Reduction** | Loss of oxygen or addition of hydrogen (substance is reduced) |

**Important:** Oxidation and reduction **always occur together** (redox).

#### Examples in Barbering:

| Service | What Happens |
|---------|--------------|
| **Haircolor** | Gains oxygen from H₂O₂ (oxidized); H₂O₂ loses oxygen (reduced) |
| **Permanent wave neutralizer** | Hair oxidized by removing hydrogen; neutralizer reduced |
| **Exothermic perm activator** | Adds oxygen (creates heat); H₂O₂ reduced |

#### Heat Reactions:

| Term | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Exothermic** | Releases heat | Mixing activator + waving lotion |
| **Endothermic** | Absorbs heat | Some waving lotions needing external heat |

---

### Pure Substances vs. Mixtures

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pure substance** | Fixed composition and proportions (elements or compounds) |
| **Physical mixture** | Blended without fixed composition (uniform or nonuniform) |

**Chemical compounds:** Oxides, acids, bases, salts

---

### Solutions, Suspensions, and Emulsions (LO5)

Physical mixtures of two or more substances.

| Type | Characteristics | Appearance | Example |
|------|-----------------|------------|---------|
| **Solutions** | Stable, uniform (solute dissolved in solvent) | Clear | Saltwater |
| **Suspensions** | Unstable, visible particles, separate over time (shake well) | Cloudy | Calamine lotion, many hair tonics |
| **Emulsions** | Immiscible liquids held by surfactant/emulsifier; tend to separate | Milky | Shampoos, conditioners, mayonnaise |

**Note:** Oil-in-water emulsions are most common in barbering.

#### Surfactants:

- Reduce surface tension
- **Hydrophilic head** (water-loving)
- **Lipophilic tail** (oil-loving)
- Allow oil + water to mix

### Water and pH (LO6 & LO7)

#### Water (H₂O):

- **Universal solvent**
- **Soft water:** Lathers well
- **Hard water:** Contains minerals, does not lather well
- Purified by boiling, filtration, or distillation

#### pH Scale (0–14):

Measures **acidity/alkalinity** (potential hydrogen).

| pH Level | Classification | Effect on Hair |
|----------|----------------|----------------|
| < 7 | Acidic | Contracts/hardens hair, closes cuticle |
| 7 | Neutral | Balanced |
| > 7 | Alkaline | Softens/swells hair, opens cuticle |

**Hair & skin ideal range: 4.5–5.5**

Pure water can swell hair up to 20%.

#### Acids vs. Alkalis on Hair:

| pH Range | Type | Effect |
|----------|------|--------|
| 0–1 | Very strong acid | Dissolves hair (never use) |
| 1–4.5 | Strong–mild acid | Shrinks, hardens, improves sheen, removes residue |
| 4.5–5.5 | Neutral | Normal hair |
| 5.5–10 | Mild alkali | Swells, opens cuticle (used in tints, bleaches, cold waves) |
| 10–14 | Strong alkali | Dissolves hair (used in relaxers/depilatories — avoid scalp) |

#### Acid-Alkali Neutralization:

Equal proportions of acid and alkali form **water + salt**.

### Cosmetic Preparations Used in Barbering (LO8–LO10)

#### Shampoos (mostly emulsions):

- Cleanse oil, dirt, debris
- Work in hard/soft water
- Non-irritating
- Leave hair natural

#### Shampoo Types:

| Type | Use |
|------|-----|
| pH-balanced/acid-balanced | General use |
| Balancing | Oily scalps |
| Clarifying | Buildup/minerals |
| Color-enhancing | Maintain hair color |
| Conditioning/moisturizing | Dry/damaged hair |
| Medicated | Antidandruff |
| Neutralizing | After chemical services |
| Sulfate-free | Sensitive scalps |
| Dry/powder | Waterless cleansing |
| Therapeutic | Scalp conditions |

#### Surfactant Classifications:

| Type | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **Anionics** | Negative charge; good cleansing |
| **Cationics** | Positive charge; conditioning |
| **Nonionics** | No charge; mild |
| **Amphoterics** | Both charges; gentle |

#### Conditioners:

- Restore moisture/protein
- Close cuticle
- **pH 3.0–5.5**

**Types:**
- Instant/rinse-out
- Treatment/repair
- Leave-in

#### Other Preparations:

- Hair spray
- Hairdressings (pomades)
- Masks/packs
- Massage creams
- Moisturizing creams
- Ointments
- Pastes
- Scalp lotions
- Soaps
- Styling aids (gels/mousses)
- Suntan lotions
- Wrinkle creams
- Epilators (cold/hot wax)
- Astringents, toners, fresheners
- Cleansing creams/lotions
- Depilatories

### Common Ingredients (USP Standards)

| Ingredient | Function |
|------------|----------|
| **Alcohol** | Solvent, antiseptic (isopropyl 50–60% effective on skin) |
| **Alkanolamines** | Raise pH, neutralize acids (less odor than ammonia) |
| **Alum** | Styptic (stops minor bleeding) |
| **Ammonia** | Raises pH for waving/coloring/lightening |
| **Formaldehyde** | Preservative — toxic/carcinogenic; use caution |
| **Glycerin** | Humectant/moisturizer |
| **Hydrogen peroxide** | Oxidizing/bleaching agent (3–5% antiseptic; 20–40 vol. for bleaching) |
| **Petrolatum (Vaseline)** | Protective, emollient |
| **Quats** | Antiseptics, preservatives |
| **Silicones** | Breathable film in conditioners |
| **Witch hazel** | Astringent/freshener |

**United States Pharmacopeia (USP):** Sets standards for many ingredients used in hair/skin products.

---

### Barbering Takeaways from Chapter 7

1. **Chemistry knowledge prevents damage** (wrong pH, improper mixing, unsafe ingredients)
2. Always **read labels**, follow manufacturer directions, and check SDS
3. **Match products** to client hair/skin type and condition
4. **Perform patch tests** when required
5. **Understand pH, redox, emulsions, and common ingredients** for safe services

---

### KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

1. **Organic chemistry** = carbon-containing substances; **Inorganic** = no carbon
2. **Matter** = anything with volume and mass (solid, liquid, gas)
3. **Physical change** = no new substance; **Chemical change** = new substance formed
4. **Oxidation** = gain oxygen/lose hydrogen; **Reduction** = lose oxygen/gain hydrogen
5. **Redox reactions** always occur together
6. **Solutions** = stable/clear; **Suspensions** = unstable/shake; **Emulsions** = immiscible liquids
7. **pH scale 0-14:** <7 acidic, 7 neutral, >7 alkaline
8. **Hair ideal pH:** 4.5–5.5
9. **Acids** close cuticle, harden hair; **Alkalis** open cuticle, swell hair
10. **Shampoos** are mostly oil-in-water emulsions
11. **Conditioner pH:** 3.0–5.5
12. **USP** sets standards for cosmetic ingredients
