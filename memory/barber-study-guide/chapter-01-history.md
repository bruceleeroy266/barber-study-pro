# Chapter 1: History of Barbering
## Study Guide Notes

---

### WHY STUDY HISTORY?

Barbering is one of the **oldest documented professions** in the world. Understanding the history helps you:
- Appreciate its role across cultures
- Predict future trends
- Understand modern regulations and organizations

---

### ANCIENT ORIGINS

**Early Tools (Glacial Age)**
- Sharpened flints, oyster shells, bone, animal sinew
- Used for haircutting and styling

**Egyptians (First Beauty Culture)**
- Tools: Combs, brushes, mirrors, cosmetics, scissors, razors (copper/bronze)
- High-ranking people shaved heads for wigs and parasite prevention
- Priests shaved entire bodies daily for purity
- Barbers called **"Meryma'at"** — held high esteem

**Other Ancient Cultures**
- Hair had **spiritual significance** — believed spirits entered/exited through hair
- Tribal barbers often served as medicine men, shamans, or priests
- **African cultures**: Intricate carved combs, beads, clay, colored bands, braiding (status symbols)
- **Masai warriors**: Specific braiding patterns denoted status

---

### THE BEARD & SHAVING

**Timeline**
- **40,000–10,000 BC**: Upper Paleolithic — stone razors/scrapers for hair removal and skinning animals
- **8000–5000 BC**: Neolithic period — evidence of clean-shaven men
- **~7000 BC**: Egypt — flint-bladed razors
- **~4000 BC**: Egypt — tweezers in use
- **~1000 BC**: Greece — men visited local barbers

**Symbolism**
- Beards = wisdom, strength, manhood, religious devotion
- **Rome**: First shave = rite of passage (boyhood to manhood)
- **Alexander the Great**: Ordered soldiers to shave (beards could be grabbed in battle)
- **1096**: Archbishop of Rouen prohibited beards → led to first barber guild in France
- **Peter the Great**: Taxed beards

---

### RELIGION, POLITICS & CULTURE

**Spiritual Beliefs**
- **Pythagoras**: Cutting hair decreased intellectual capacity
- **Native Americans**: Hair/body connection beliefs
- Hairstyles indicated social status, rank, or submission

**Examples**
- **Chinese queue**: Forced hairstyle under Manchu rule (submission)
- **Gaul nobles**: Long hair = status
- **Caesar's cut**: Symbolized submission to Rome

**Hair Coloring**
- Ancient Britons: Lightened hair
- Romans: Dyes based on social class
- Queen Elizabeth era: Influenced beard dyeing

**Tonsure**
- Medieval clergy: Shaved patch on crown of head
- **Circular tonsure of St. Peter**: Preferred by many
- **Abolished**: 1972 by Roman Catholic Church

---

### BARBER-SURGEONS (Middle Ages)

**Services Provided**
- Grooming
- Bloodletting
- Minor surgery
- Herbal remedies
- Tooth pulling

**Key Dates**
- **1163**: Council of Tours — Pope forbade clergy from acting as physicians/surgeons → barbers took over
- **1096**: First barber guild in France
- **1308**: Worshipful Company of Barbers guild in London
- **1450**: English barbers and surgeons merged into Company of Barber-Surgeons
- **1540**: Henry VIII recombined barbers and surgeons via royal charter
- **1745**: Surgeons fully separated from barbers

**Barber Pole Symbolism** (from bloodletting)
- **Pole** = staff patient held
- **Red** = blood
- **White** = bandages
- **Blue** = veins (added later, varies by country)

---

### TOOLS & TECHNOLOGY

**Evolution**
- **Prehistory**: Bone, flint, antler, shell
- **3000–2000 BC**: Middle East — earliest scissors
- **Bronze/Iron Age**: Improved metallurgy → better shears
- **1–100 AD & 400–500 AD**: Rome — cross-bladed shears
- **1850–1890**: Nikola Bizumic — manual hair clippers

**Modern Era**
- **1919**: Leo J. Wahl — first practical electric hair clippers
- **1921**: Wahl Clipper Corporation founded; Matthew Andis develops electric clipper
- **1924**: John Oster markets hand-driven clipper
- **1928**: Oster motorized clipper

---

### PROFESSIONAL ORGANIZATIONS

**Late 1800s**: Barbering separates fully from religion/medicine

**Key Organizations**
- **1886–1887**: Barbers' Protective Association → Journeymen Barbers' International Union of America
- **1897**: **First barber-licensing law** (Minnesota) — sanitation, education, disinfection
- **1916**: New York "terminal methods" system for strict disinfection
- **1924**: Associated Master Barbers of America (AMBBA) — standardized training, barber code of ethics
- **1927**: National Association of Barber Schools
- **1929**: National Association of State Boards of Barber Examiners (NABBA) — represents 300,000+ barbers

**NABBA Objectives**
- Promote information exchange
- Standards for exams/licensing
- Curriculum standards
- Continuing education
- Consumer protection

---

### MODERN BARBERSHOPS (21st Century)

**1980s–1990s**: Decline due to:
- Unisex salons
- Full-service spas
- Long hair trends
- Shift to cosmetology licensing

**Late 1990s–2000s**: Resurgence
- New schools opening
- Independent/franchised barbershops
- Masculine atmosphere return

**2010s+**: "Art of manliness" movement
- Beard popularity
- Creative grooming
- Women ~30% of clientele in some shops
- Female barbers/students increasing

**Today**
- Blend tradition with new technology
- Focus on creativity, community, health/safety
- Strong growth potential

---

### KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

1. Barbering evolved: **Spiritual/Tribal → Medical (barber-surgeons) → Independent regulated profession**

2. Influenced by: **Religion, politics, fashion, technology**

3. Modern profession emphasizes: **Hygiene, education, licensing, ethics, public safety**

4. **1897**: First licensing law (Minnesota)

5. **Barber pole colors**: Red = blood, White = bandages, Blue = veins

6. **1163**: Council of Tours — barbers took over medical duties from clergy

7. **1745**: Surgeons separated from barbers

8. **1929**: NABBA founded
