# Chapter 3: Professional Image
## Study Guide Notes

---

### Introduction to Professional Image

**First impressions are critical** for job interviews, gaining new clients, and building a professional reputation.

Barbers are held to **higher image standards** because clients view them as image experts.

A positive professional image includes:
- Appearance
- Positive attitude
- Team camaraderie
- Proper ergonomics
- Good communication skills

---

### Why Study Professional Image?

- Clients rely on barbers to look good and be well-groomed → builds **trust and repeat business**
- Helps **career growth** within a barbershop culture
- Prevents **health issues** from poor working habits
- Avoids consequences like:
  - Loss of clients
  - Poor reputation
  - Lost income

---

### Apply Healthful Habits in Your Daily Routine

**LO1: Name four important personal hygiene habits.**

#### Hygiene

Basic daily cleanliness is essential, especially when working in close proximity to clients.

**Personal hygiene** = daily maintenance of cleanliness through healthy habits

One weak moment (body odor, bad breath, stained clothes) can cause clients to not return and share negative experiences.

#### Key Personal Hygiene Habits:

1. **Wash hands frequently** throughout the day, including at the start of each service
2. **Perform self-checks** and freshen under arms as needed
3. **Brush and floss teeth**; use mouthwash or breath mints
4. **Do not smoke during work hours**. If you smoke on break:
   - Brush teeth
   - Use mouthwash
   - Wash hands afterward

---

### Rest and Relaxation

- **Adequate sleep** is essential for health and efficient functioning
- Most people need **7–8 hours per night** (varies by individual)
- **Relaxation** (movies, reading, sports, dancing) provides a change of pace and helps you return to work refreshed

---

### Nutrition

What you eat affects health, appearance, personality, and job performance.

- Follow a **balanced diet** with variety of vitamins and minerals
- **Drink plenty of water** daily
- **Limit:** sugar, salt, caffeine, and highly processed/fatty foods

---

### Nail Care

Barbers use their hands constantly — do not neglect your own nail care.

- Keep nails at a **suitable length** for your style
- Keep them **thoroughly clean**
- Occasional chipped polish or broken nails is okay, but not as a regular occurrence

---

### Employ Proper Ergonomics to Protect Your Body

**LO3: Practice ergonomically correct movement, postures, and principles.**

**Physical presentation** = your posture and the way you walk/move
- Good posture conveys **confidence** and prevents **fatigue and injury**

Barbers spend most of the day standing or in repetitive motions (raising arms, bending over shampoo bowl).

#### Posture Guidelines (Standing Behind the Chair):

| Body Part | Correct Position |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Head | Up, chin parallel to floor |
| Neck | Elongated and balanced over shoulders |
| Chest | Up and out (no slouching) |
| Shoulders | Level and relaxed |
| Spine | Straight |

#### Correct Sitting Posture:

- Hips level, knees flexed slightly over feet
- Back straight, weight on thigh bones (not end of spine)
- Feet flat on floor
- Chair height so arms form **90° angle** when writing

---

### Body Movement & Ergonomics

**Ergonomics** = science of designing workplace/tools for comfort, efficiency, and safety

Repetitive motions can cause **musculoskeletal disorders:**
- Carpal tunnel syndrome
- Back injuries

**Prevention is key:** awareness + proper tools + good habits

#### Suggestions to Avoid Problems:

- Do not grip tools too tightly
- Avoid constant wrist bending
- Keep arms at **<60° angle** when raised
- Adjust chair height for client's head
- Tilt client's head as needed
- Keep wrists **neutral**
- Use **ergonomic tools**, antifatigue mats, proper stance (legs hip-width, knees bent)

#### Quick Exercises for Wrists, Fingers, Shoulders:

- Stand straight, raise arms
- Bend wrists
- Grip/release ball
- Shoulder shrugs and rolls

These relieve stress from repetitive motions.

### Follow Image-Building Basics

**LO2: Explain the concept of dressing for success.**

Being well-groomed advertises your **commitment to the profession**.

- **Personal grooming** = caring for hair, skin, nails, and overall polished look
- **Professional image** = impression you project through appearance and conduct

---

### Dress for Success

- Wardrobe should **complement the barbershop's image** and be professional
- Clothes must be **clean, pressed, stain-free**, and functional/comfortable
- Avoid overly baggy or tight clothing
- Follow **shop dress code** (barber jacket, smock, tie, specific colors)
- **Shoes:** supportive, polished, in good repair (no flip-flops or open-toed around tools)

---

### Hair Maintenance

- Keep your own **haircut/style fresh and up-to-date**
- Schedule regular appointments (barter with other barbers if needed)
- **Facial hair:** keep beard/mustache neat and trimmed
- **Clean-shaven barbers:** should shave daily

---

### Skin Care and Makeup

- Healthy skin promotes a professional image
- Develop a **skin care regimen** (cleansing, sunscreen, etc.)
- **Makeup** (if used) should enhance features subtly — **never apply at your station**
- **Caution:** Many shops have a **no-fragrance policy** due to allergies/sensitivities

---

### Practice Effective Human Relations and Communication Skills

**LO4: Demonstrate an understanding of human relations and communication skills.**

**Human relations** = interactions and relationships between people

Good human relations build **rapport** with clients and coworkers through self-esteem, confidence, and respect.

#### Guidelines for Good Human Relations:

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Greet clients by name with pleasant tone | Ignore clients or be dismissive |
| Be alert to client's mood; be a good listener | Interrupt or talk over clients |
| Choose conversation topics carefully | Discuss controversial subjects, gossip, personal issues |
| Maintain confidentiality | Share client information |
| Look and act professionally | Be sloppy or unprofessional |
| Show genuine interest and give undivided attention | Be distracted or inattentive |
| Use tact when handling problems | React emotionally or defensively |
| Be punctual and efficient | Run late or waste time |
| Practice emotional control and positive approach | Let emotions control you |

---

### Effective Communication Skills

One of the barber's **most important skills**.

Includes: listening, tone, speech, and conversational ability.

#### Steps for Determining Client Expectations:

1. **Organize your thoughts** — Ask clear questions
2. **Clarify** — Make sure you understand what the client means
3. **Repeat** — Confirm the client's description back to them

---

### Social Media

**Powerful tool when used correctly**, but can easily damage reputation if misused.

| DO | DON'T |
|----|-------|
| Manage pages professionally | Use profanity |
| Post helpful content | Argue online |
| Communicate appropriately | Post embarrassing/nude photos |
| | Forward spam |

**Maintaining a strong professional image** (in person and online) is a daily investment that directly impacts your success.
