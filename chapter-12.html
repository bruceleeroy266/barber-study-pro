CHAPTER 12

Men's Facial Massage and Treatments

Facial massage techniques, equipment, and therapeutic treatments

🎯 Why Study Men's Facial Massage?

Facial massage and treatments are valuable services that enhance the barber-client relationship. Understanding these techniques helps you:

  • Provide relaxing and therapeutic facial services
  • Understand facial muscles and their functions
  • Safely use facial equipment and electrotherapy devices
  • Analyze skin types and recommend appropriate treatments
  • Increase revenue through additional services
Key Point: A proper facial treatment can improve skin condition, promote relaxation, and create a premium service experience for clients.

💆 Facial Massage Manipulations

Facial massage requires specific techniques different from scalp massage. The movements should be gentle and follow the natural contours of the face.

Basic Facial Massage Movements:

Effleurage — Light, continuous stroking movements used to apply product and relax the client. Always work in an upward direction to avoid dragging skin down.
Petrissage — Kneading movement using fingertips or palms. Used on larger facial areas like cheeks and forehead.
Tapotement — Light tapping or percussion movements. Includes:
  • Tapping: Gentle fingertip taps
  • Slapping: Light slapping with fingers
  • Hacking: Chopping motion with outer edge of hands (use only on back and shoulders, not face)
Vibration — Rapid shaking movement using fingertips or electric massager. Used sparingly and never exceed a few seconds in one area.

Guidelines for Facial Massage:

  • Always work in an upward direction to avoid loss of skin resiliency
  • Apply pressure on the upward swing of circular movements
  • Perform movements systematically and rhythmically
  • Curve fingertips or palms to conform to facial contours
  • Gradually reduce pressure when removing hands (feather-off)
  • When facial hair is present, work in direction of beard growth
  • Avoid overstimulation - use vibration sparingly

Contraindications for Facial Massage:

Do NOT perform facial massage if client has:
  • Acute inflammation of the skin
  • Severe skin lesions
  • Pus-containing pimples
  • High blood pressure
  • Skin infections

🔌 Facial Equipment and Appliances

Electric Massager:

Handheld unit that transmits vibrations through the barber's hand to the client's skin. Used over heavy muscle tissue (scalp, shoulders) to stimulate blood supply and soothe nerves.

CAUTION: Do not use on clients with heart conditions, fever, abscesses, or skin inflammations.

Brush Machine:

Electrical appliance with interchangeable brushes that attach to a rotating head. Used for mechanical exfoliation to stimulate, cleanse, and remove dead cells.

  • Apply moisturizing cream before use
  • Use softer brushes on facial skin
  • Clean and disinfect between clients
  • Never use on skin treated with Retin-A or exfoliating drugs

Hot Towel Cabinet:

Provides ready supply of warm towels for facial services. Towels can also be prepared with witch hazel steam.

Facial Steamer:

Produces moist, uniform steam that warms skin, stimulates circulation, induces flow of sebum and sweat, and has antiseptic effects. Helps soften follicle accumulations.

  • Use only distilled or filtered water
  • Administer steam at beginning of facial treatment
  • Position 12-18 inches from face

⚡ Electrotherapy Equipment

High-Frequency Machine:

Produces high-frequency current (also called Tesla current) used for scalp and facial treatments. Sometimes called "violet ray" due to color.

Effects of High-Frequency:

  • Stimulates blood circulation
  • Helps oxygenate the skin
  • Increases glandular activity
  • Aids in elimination and absorption
  • Increases cell metabolism
  • Promotes antiseptic and germicidal action
  • Generates warm, relaxing feeling

Application Methods:

Direct Application: Electrode attached to machine makes direct contact with skin. Used with mushroom or rake-shaped electrodes for calming and germicidal effects.
Indirect Application: Client holds electrode while barber massages. Produces toning and stimulating effects; ideal for aging or sallow skin.

Galvanic Machine:

Converts alternating current to direct current. Has two poles: positive (+) and negative (-).

Galvanic Current Applications:

Desincrustation: Negative pole (cathode) used to soften sebum and emulsify follicle contents for deep pore cleansing.
Iontophoresis: Process of using galvanic current to enable water-soluble products to penetrate skin. Negative current allows negative ion products to penetrate; positive current allows positive ion products to penetrate.
Cataphoresis: Use of positive pole (anode) to introduce acid-pH products (astringents) to close follicles and decrease redness.
Anaphoresis: Use of negative pole (cathode) to force alkaline-pH products (desincrustation lotion) into skin.

Microcurrent:

Very low-level current used for toning skin and producing lifting effect on aging skin.

Microdermabrasion:

Mechanical exfoliation spraying aluminum oxide or other microcrystals across skin surface to exfoliate dead cells. Vacuumed off by suction.

Light Therapy:

Ultraviolet Rays:
  • UVA (tonic rays)
  • UVB (therapeutic rays)
  • UVC (germicidal rays)
Effects: Increase waste elimination, improve blood/lymph flow, assist vitamin D production, increase melanin.
Infrared Rays: Produce soothing heat that penetrates tissues. Effects: Heats and relaxes skin, soothes nerves, dilates blood vessels, increases circulation, relieves pain.

Contraindications for Electrotherapy:

Never perform electrotherapy on clients with:
  • Seizures or epilepsy
  • Heart conditions
  • Nerve disorders
  • Fever or any infection
  • Asthma
  • Pregnancy
  • High blood pressure
  • Open or broken skin
  • Sinus blockages
  • Pacemakers
  • Metal implants

🔍 Skin Analysis

Proper skin analysis determines the appropriate facial treatment and products.

Skin Types:

Dry Skin (Alipidic) — Does not produce enough sebum. Appears flaky or dry with small fine lines and wrinkles. Objective: Stimulate oil production and hydrate.
Normal Skin — Good water-oil balance. Normal pore size, free of blemishes. Goal is maintenance and preservation.
Oily Skin — Excess sebum production. Appears shiny or greasy with larger follicles. Requires more cleansing and exfoliation.
Combination Skin — Both oily and dry in different areas. T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) tends to be oily; cheeks tend to be dry.

Skin Analysis Procedure:

  1. Observe client's skin type, condition, and appearance; feel the texture
  2. Ask about skin's appearance and home care routine
  3. Discuss facial procedure and treatment plans
  4. Encourage questions and determine course of action together
  5. Record information for client's next visit
Use a magnifying lamp or light for best analysis. Note skin type, condition, visible appearance, and texture.

🧴 Skin Care Products

Product Categories:

Face Washes — Water-based products with neutral or slightly acidic pH. Effective on oily and combination skin.
Cleansing Lotions — Water-based emulsions for normal and combination skin containing emollients or oils.
Cleansing Creams — Heavier oil-based emulsions used primarily to dissolve dirt and makeup.
Skin Tonics (Fresheners, Toners, Astringents) — Rebalance pH and remove product residue.
  • Fresheners: Lowest alcohol (0-4%), for dry/mature/sensitive skin
  • Toners: 4-15% alcohol, for normal/combination skin
  • Astringents: Up to 35% alcohol, for oily/acne-prone skin
Moisturizers — Add moisture to skin surface. Contain humectants and emollients.
Mechanical Exfoliants — Physically remove dead cells (granular scrubs).
Rolling Cream — Thick, smooth, nongranular exfoliating cream applied in thin layer and rolled off with firm stroking motions.
Chemical Exfoliants — Alpha hydroxy acids or enzyme peels that loosen or dissolve dead cells.
Masks — Draw impurities from pores. Available in cream, gel, or clay forms.
  • Clay masks: Stimulate circulation, contract pores, absorb sebum (oily/combination skin)
  • Cream masks: Contain humectants and emollients (dry skin)
  • Gel masks: Hydrate and soothe (sensitive/dehydrated skin)
  • Paraffin wax masks: Employ pack application method

Basic Facial Treatment Order:

  1. Cleansing cream or lotion
  2. Exfoliant (scrub or rolling cream)
  3. Massage cream
  4. Cleansing cream or lotion
  5. Freshener (low alcohol content)
  6. Mask or pack
  7. Toner or astringent
  8. Moisturizer

🧖 Types of Facial Treatments

Basic Facial (Scientific Rest Facial):

Foundation for all other facial treatments. Includes cleansing, exfoliating, massage manipulations, toning, mask or pack, and moisturizing.

Vibratory Facial:

Performed using combination of hand massage and electric massager. Two application methods:

  • Direct: Attach massager to dominant hand, vibrations travel through fingertips to skin
  • Indirect: Client holds massager, barber massages with other hand (more suitable for delicate areas)

Facial for Dry Skin:

Objective is to help moisturize. Can be supplemented with infrared rays, galvanic current, or high-frequency current to stimulate sebum production.

Facial for Oily Skin:

Addresses excess sebum and blackheads (comedones). Clients with serious acne conditions require medical attention. If client is under medical care, barber may perform physician-prescribed treatments.

Wear gloves and use only effleurage movements when working on acne to avoid spreading infection.

Guidelines for Facial Treatments:

  • Help client relax by speaking quietly and professionally
  • Explain benefits of service and products during consultation
  • Provide quiet atmosphere
  • Work efficiently
  • Maintain clean conditions and arrange supplies neatly
  • Analyze skin and follow systematic procedure
  • Protect client's eyes when applying products
  • Keep nails smooth and short
  • Warm cold hands before applying to client's skin
  • Always test hot towels before applying
  • Avoid excessive or rough massage
  • Avoid leaving excess product on skin
  • Avoid excessive conversation that doesn't facilitate relaxation

⚠️ Safety Precautions for Electrical Equipment

  • Disconnect appliances when not in use
  • Study instructions before using any equipment
  • Keep all wires, plugs, and equipment in safe condition
  • Inspect electrical equipment frequently
  • Avoid getting electrical cords wet
  • Clean and disinfect all electrodes properly
  • Protect the client at all times
  • Do not touch any metal while using electrical appliances
  • Do not handle electrical equipment with wet hands
  • Do not allow client to touch metal surfaces during treatments
  • Do not leave the room when client is attached to any device
  • Do not attempt to clean around electric outlet when equipment is plugged in
  • Do not touch two metallic objects at same time while connected to electric current
  • Do not use any electrical equipment without full instruction in its care and use
