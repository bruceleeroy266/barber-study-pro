CHAPTER 20

Working Behind the Chair

Teamwork, compensation, retailing, and client building

🎯 Why Study This Chapter?

  • Working in a barbershop requires strong teamwork skills
  • Compensation structures vary; understanding them helps you choose right
  • Learn money management while building clientele
  • Increase income through retailing and upselling

🤝 Being a Good Team Player

  • Strive to help others succeed
  • Pitch in with any task (folding towels, answering phones)
  • Share knowledge and learn from others
  • Remain positive; avoid gossip
  • Build relationships
  • Resolve conflicts constructively
  • Be willing to be subordinate when starting
  • Be sincerely loyal to shop, staff, and clients
Mindset Shift: You are now a professional — accountability and reliability are non-negotiable.

💰 Employment Classifications

Employee:

  • Paid salary, commission, or salary-plus-commission
  • Employer sets hours, may provide benefits
  • Employer withholds taxes; issues W-2

Independent Contractor:

  • Rents chair or works on percentage
  • Responsible for own taxes (1099-MISC)
  • Requires written agreement

Booth Renter:

  • Leases space; operates as small business
  • Handles own clientele, supplies, accounting
  • Pays fixed rent; keeps revenue after expenses

💵 Wage Structures

  • Straight/Hourly Salary — Fixed income
  • Commission — Percentage of gross sales (40-70%)
  • Salary-Plus-Commission — Base + percentage over base
  • Tips — Must be tracked and reported on taxes

💼 Money Management

  • Honor all loans and debts
  • Report all income (paycheck, tips, commissions)
  • Create personal budget tracking expenses vs income
  • Give yourself a raise by: spending less, working more hours, increasing prices, retailing more

🛒 Retailing & Client Building

  • Upselling — Recommend additional services
  • Retailing — Sell take-home products
  • Know features/benefits of products
  • Focus on client needs, not hard selling
  • Send birthday cards with special offers
  • Use social media professionally
  • Ask for referrals

📝 Chapter 20 Key Takeaways

  • Transition demands reliability, teamwork, client-first mindset
  • Understand compensation types (employee, contractor, booth renter)
  • Manage money through budgeting and income tracking
  • Master retailing and upselling by focusing on client needs
  • Build clientele through excellent service and referrals
  • Success comes from professionalism and continuous learning
