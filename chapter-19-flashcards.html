Chapter 19: Men's Haircoloring

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

Haircolor

A product that deposits artificial pigment onto the hair to change its color.

Level System

A standardized scale from 1-10 that identifies the lightness or darkness of hair color.

Primary Colors

Red, blue, and yellow; pure colors that cannot be created by mixing other colors.

Secondary Colors

Orange, green, and violet; created by mixing equal parts of two primary colors.

Tertiary Colors

Colors created by mixing a primary color with a neighboring secondary color.

Complementary Colors

Colors opposite each other on the color wheel that neutralize each other when mixed.

Tone

The warmth, coolness, or neutrality of a hair color; described as ash, golden, or neutral.

Permanent Color

Haircolor that lifts natural pigment and deposits artificial color; lasts until hair grows out.

Demi-Permanent Color

Color that deposits without lifting; lasts 4-6 weeks and gradually fades.

Semi-Permanent Color

Color that coats the hair shaft without penetrating; lasts 4-12 shampoos.

Temporary Color

Color that coats the surface of hair and washes out with one shampoo.

Developer

Hydrogen peroxide mixed with haircolor to activate the color and develop the final result.

Gray Coverage

The process of coloring resistant gray hair to achieve uniform, natural-looking results.

Highlighting

A technique that lightens selected strands of hair to create dimension and contrast.

Lowlighting

A technique that darkens selected strands to add depth and dimension to hair.

Cap Technique

A highlighting method using a perforated cap to pull hair through for coloring.

Foil Technique

A highlighting method using aluminum foil to isolate and process selected hair sections.

Color Correction

The process of fixing undesirable haircolor results to achieve the desired shade.

Brassy Tones

Unwanted warm, orange or yellow tones that appear during lightening processes.

Toner

A semi-permanent color used after lightening to neutralize unwanted tones or add desired tones.
