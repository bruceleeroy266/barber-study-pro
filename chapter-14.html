CHAPTER 14

Men's Haircutting & Styling

Shear-over-comb, clipper techniques, and basic styles

🎯 Why Study Haircutting?

Haircutting is the foundation of barbering. Mastering it allows you to:

  • Deliver consistent, high-quality results
  • Adapt to various hair textures and lengths
  • Create balanced, customized designs
  • Build client confidence and loyalty
Key Point: A great haircut is not just about removing hair — it's about creating shape, balance, and proportion.

✂️ Basic Cutting Techniques

Shear-Over-Comb (Fingers-and-Shear):

  • Horizontal cutting — Top and crest sections; comb at 45° angle
  • Vertical cutting — Sides; comb hair outward at 90° elevation
  • Perimeter cutting — Nape and outline; use horizontal parts

Freehand Shear Cutting:

  • Cutting against skin without comb or fingers
  • Useful for fine-tuning, removing stray hairs, shaping nape

Clipper Techniques:

  • Freehand clipper — Steady hand, comb lifts hair
  • Clipper-over-comb — Comb lifts hair, clipper cuts protruding ends

Razor Cutting:

  • Produces softer, tapered ends
  • Keep hair damp; use guard for beginners
Master shear-over-comb first — it's the most versatile technique.

🔄 Hair Growth Directions

  • With the grain — Cut in same direction as growth (safest, least irritation)
  • Against the grain — Opposite direction (shorter result, more caution)
  • Across the grain — Used in transition areas for blending
Always identify natural growth pattern before cutting.

✂️ Thinning and Texturizing

  • Thinning — Use serrated shears mid-shaft to reduce density
  • Slicing/Carving — Glide open shears through elevated hair
  • Slithering — Slide shears down thin parting toward scalp
  • Notching/Point Cutting — Create chunkier texture at ends
Observe density first. Remove bulk gradually — over-thinning is difficult to correct.

💈 Basic Haircut Styles

  • Precision Cut — Uniform taper using fingers-and-shear
  • Taper Cut — Graduated length from nape to top
  • Flat Top/Crew Cut — Short sides, flat or rounded top
  • Pompadour — Medium to long top with volume, tapered sides
  • Fade/High & Tight — Extremely close sides blending to longer top
  • Caesar — Short, uniform layers styled forward

📝 Chapter 14 Key Takeaways

  • Core techniques: shear-over-comb, freehand shear, clipper-over-comb, razor
  • Hair growth directions determine tool usage
  • Thinning/texturizing reduces bulk or adds movement
  • Basic styles: taper, flat top, pompadour, fade, Caesar
  • Finish work (neck shave, trim) completes the professional look
  • Safety — use proper tools, disinfect everything, maintain ergonomics
