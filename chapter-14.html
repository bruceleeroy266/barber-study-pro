CHAPTER 14

Men's Haircutting and Styling

Haircutting techniques, styling, and design principles

🎯 Why Study Men's Haircutting?

Haircutting is the foundation of barbering. Understanding proper techniques allows you to:

  • Create customized haircuts for each client
  • Understand facial shapes and how to complement them
  • Master various cutting techniques and tools
  • Build a loyal client base through quality work
  • Increase revenue through styling and additional services
Key Point: Practice and technique are essential. The more you practice, the more confident and skilled you become.

💬 Client Consultation

A thorough consultation is the foundation of a successful haircut. Understanding what the client wants prevents misunderstandings.

Key Questions to Ask:

  • "What would you like done today?"
  • "How short do you want it?" (Use fingers to show length)
  • "Do you want a fade, taper, or all one length?"
  • "How do you usually style your hair?"
  • "Do you have any cowlicks or growth patterns I should know about?"

Understanding "Just a Trim":

When a client says "just a trim," it can mean different things to different people. Always clarify:

  • Show exactly how much hair will be removed
  • Use fingers or comb to demonstrate length
  • Confirm before cutting
  • Remember: a trim to one person might be a major cut to another

👤 Facial Shapes and Hair Design

Understanding facial shapes helps you recommend styles that complement the client's features.

Five Basic Facial Shapes:

Oval Face — Considered the ideal shape. Balanced proportions. Can wear most styles. Width of forehead and jaw are similar; face is longer than wide.
Round Face — Width and length are nearly equal. Full cheeks, rounded jawline. Goal: Add height and minimize width. Avoid styles that add volume to sides.
Square Face — Strong, angular jawline. Forehead and jaw width similar. Goal: Soften angles. Can wear most styles but avoid too much volume on top.
Inverted Triangular (Heart) Face — Wider forehead, narrower chin. Goal: Add width at jawline, minimize forehead width. Styles with volume on sides work well.
Pear-Shaped (Triangular) Face — Narrower forehead, wider jawline. Goal: Add width at temples, minimize jaw width. Styles with volume on top work well.

Design Principles:

  • Balance: Create visual equilibrium
  • Proportion: Keep features in pleasing ratio
  • Emphasis: Draw attention to best features
  • Rhythm: Create movement and flow
  • Harmony: All elements work together

✂️ Basic Haircutting Principles

Preparation for All Haircuts:

  1. Wash hands thoroughly
  2. Conduct client consultation
  3. Drape client for wet service
  4. Shampoo and towel dry hair
  5. Remove waterproof cape; replace with neck strip and haircutting cape
  6. Face client toward mirror and lock chair

Basic Techniques:

Clipper-Over-Comb — Using clippers over a comb to create precise, clean lines and gradual fades.
Shear-Over-Comb — Using shears over a comb for softer, more blended results than clippers.
Freehand Clipper — Using clippers without a comb for outlining and detail work.
Blending — Creating smooth transitions between different lengths.
Fading — Gradually tapering hair from short to shorter or to skin.

🎨 Haircutting Procedures

Basic Haircut with Clippers:

Procedure:

  1. Determine desired length on top
  2. Establish center guide on top
  3. Cut sections right and left of center from front through top
  4. Move to back section; cut from crown to nape
  5. Cut sections left and right of center through back
  6. Move to sides; cut from hairline to top
  7. Cut around ears and blend into back section
  8. Cross-check and blend as needed
  9. Outline and detail with trimmer

Shadow Fade Cut:

Performed using adjustable-blade clipper with and without guards.

Procedure:

  1. Determine length for top section
  2. Select appropriate clipper blade or guard
  3. Start at front hairline; cut center guide back to crown
  4. Cut sections right and left of center
  5. Move to back; adjust clippers to open blade position
  6. Start at nape; cut against grain to mid-occipital area
  7. Cut sections left and right of center panel
  8. Move to sides; cut from hairline to desired point
  9. Cut around ears and blend
  10. When back and sides are cut, attach guard and close blades halfway
  11. Blend at top of parietal ridge into top section
  12. Adjust blades to closed position; blend through middle parietal
  13. Attach No. 0 guard; blend around parietal ridge
  14. Remove guard; taper and blend hairline
  15. Finish with trimmer or outline shave
Reminder: Cutting against the grain achieves a closer cut than cutting with the grain.

Pompadour Fade:

Procedure:

  1. Re-dampen top section with water if necessary
  2. Part off top section following curve of head at parietal ridge
  3. Secure with clips
  4. Select appropriate blade size for back and sides
  5. On preferred side, use freehand clipper technique to establish guide
  6. Rock clipper out at point where transition will occur
  7. Repeat on opposite side
  8. Move to back; cut from nape upward
  9. Blend transition areas
  10. Release top section; comb through
  11. Create slight overdirection of parting to center top guide
  12. Cut parting to guide
  13. Continue cutting from parietal area up to top
  14. Move behind client; comb through top sections
  15. Check hair horizontally and blend as needed
  16. Comb through top section and occipital area
  17. Take vertical parting from center back
  18. Project parting to 90 degrees
  19. Using shortest length at occipital as guide, cut and blend shorter back with longer crown
  20. Pivot through crown section; cut and blend
  21. Return to center back; cut and blend toward right side
  22. Style as desired

💨 Blowdrying Techniques

Freeform Blowdrying:

  1. Hold dryer 6 to 10 inches from hair
  2. Angle nozzle pointing downward
  3. Dry layers underneath first
  4. Move dryer briskly from side to side
  5. As hair underneath dries, release next section
  6. Comb or brush hair down after each section
  7. Dry sides in same manner
  8. Dry top loosely; comb or brush into desired style

Stylized Blowdrying:

  1. Begin in back section
  2. Lift section with comb or brush
  3. Follow movement of comb with dryer
  4. Apply concentrated stream of heated air
  5. Dry from scalp to ends
  6. Repeat until section is dry
  7. Move to next section and sides

Blow Waving:

  1. Use blow-waving technique to create lift or direction in top section
  2. Work from natural part
  3. Elevate hair to create desired fullness
  4. To create definite direction in front, insert comb 1½ inches from hairline
  5. Draw comb to back then toward hairline in one motion
  6. This creates ridge or bend to set style
  7. Apply heat until soft ridge is formed
  8. Repeat in subsequent sections
  9. Apply suitable styling aid to finish

Diffused Drying:

  1. Pick hair out into basic shape of desired style
  2. Begin drying in back section, working toward crown and sides
  3. Gently pick hair out as dryer moves from section to section
  4. Dry sides in same manner
  5. Dry top section forward from crown
  6. Apply suitable styling aid to complete

🪒 The Head Shave

Complete head shave requires careful preparation and technique.

Preparation:

  1. Wash hands
  2. Conduct client consultation
  3. Drape client for haircut
  4. Face client toward mirror and lock chair
  5. Examine scalp for abrasions, lesions, or disorders
  6. Remove excess hair length with clippers if necessary
  7. Drape for wet service and shampoo remaining hair
  8. Re-examine scalp
  9. Remove shampoo cape
  10. Re-drape with haircutting cape and towels
  11. Tuck wiping cloth into neckline

Procedure:

  1. Apply shaving cream or gel and lather
  2. Follow with two or three steamed-towel treatments to soften hair
  3. Re-lather scalp
  4. Lock chair
  5. Start in back section using freehand stroke
  6. Shave with grain from crown to nape
  7. Use opposite hand to stretch skin taut
  8. Follow curve of head
  9. Move to front; tip head forward slightly
  10. Continue shaving from crown to front hairline
  11. Reapply lather as needed
  12. Keep skin moist
  13. When top is completed, work down sides
  14. Hold ear out of way with left hand
  15. Carefully shave in front of and around ears
  16. Repeat on left side
  17. Check for missed areas and re-shave as necessary
  18. Wrap warm towel around head
  19. Use towel to remove remaining lather
  20. Apply witch hazel or skin toner
  21. Follow with cool-towel application for 2-3 minutes
  22. Apply moisturizing cream or oil as requested

🎀 Cornrow Braiding

Cornrows are narrow rows of visible braids that lie close to the scalp.

Preparation:

  1. Wash hands
  2. Conduct client consultation
  3. Drape for wet service
  4. Shampoo and condition hair
  5. Remove waterproof cape
  6. Replace with towels under and over haircutting cape
  7. Face client toward mirror and lock chair

Procedure:

  1. Start with clean, damp hair
  2. Apply and massage essential oil to scalp
  3. Determine correct size and direction of cornrow base
  4. Use tail comb to part off top section on right side
  5. Secure with clip
  6. Use mirror to determine where to section left side
  7. Secure with clip
  8. Create two parallel partings in center of top section
  9. Form neat row for cornrow base
  10. Divide parting into three strands
  11. Place fingers close to base
  12. Cross left strand under center strand
  13. Cross right strand under center strand
  14. With each crossing under, pick up hair from base of panel
  15. Add to outer strand before crossing under center
  16. When braid is finished, secure ends with small rubber band
  17. Braid subsequent panels left and right of center
  18. Once top three braids complete, complete braids on left and right
  19. Maintain even tension
  20. Follow curvature of head
  21. Finish with oil sheen or appropriate styling aid

🧹 Clean-Up and Disinfection

After every haircutting service:

  • Clean and disinfect all tools and implements
  • Clean and disinfect the work area
  • Clean surfaces and chair
  • Sweep up hair and deposit in closed receptacle
  • Deposit used blades in sharps container
  • Dispose of single-use items
  • Place all used linens, towels, and capes in laundry
  • Wash hands thoroughly
Important: Never leave used blades on the counter or in regular trash. Always use a proper sharps container.

📝 Chapter 14 Key Takeaways

  • Always conduct a thorough consultation before cutting
  • Five facial shapes: oval, round, square, inverted triangular, pear-shaped
  • Basic techniques: clipper-over-comb, shear-over-comb, freehand, blending, fading
  • Shadow fade uses adjustable-blade clipper with/without guards
  • Pompadour fade requires sectioning and careful blending
  • Blowdrying methods: freeform, stylized, blow waving, diffused
  • Head shave requires steamed towels and careful stretching of skin
  • Cornrow braiding involves picking up hair with each strand cross
  • Always follow proper clean-up and disinfection procedures
  • Dispose of blades in sharps container only
