🎯 Why Study Skin Structure?

  • Decide whether a service can be safely performed
  • Protect yourself and your client from infection
  • Know when to refer a client to a physician
  • Provide better consultations and recommendations
Key Point: Never perform a service on skin that shows signs of infection, inflammation, or disease.

🏗️ Skin Structure

Epidermis (Outer Layer)

  • Thinnest layer; no blood vessels
  • Stratum germinativum — Basal layer where new cells are produced; contains melanocytes
  • Stratum corneum — Outermost layer of dead, keratinized cells; protective barrier

Dermis (True Skin)

  • Contains blood vessels, nerves, sweat glands, oil glands, hair follicles
  • Papillary layer — Upper portion with papillae forming fingerprints
  • Reticular layer — Deeper layer with collagen and elastin fibers

Subcutaneous Layer (Hypodermis)

  • Fatty tissue that insulates, cushions, and stores energy

🩺 Skin Lesions

Primary Lesions:

  • Macule — Flat, discolored spot (freckle)
  • Papule — Small, solid, elevated bump (pimple)
  • Vesicle — Small blister with clear fluid
  • Pustule — Pus-filled lesion (acne)
  • Wheal — Raised, itchy area (hives)

Secondary Lesions:

  • Scale — Flaky skin cells (dandruff)
  • Crust — Dried serum or pus (scab)
  • Fissure — Crack in the skin
  • Ulcer — Open lesion deeper than epidermis

🦠 Common Skin Disorders

  • Acne — Inflammation of sebaceous glands; avoid services on active, pustular acne
  • Dermatitis/Eczema — Red, itchy, inflamed skin
  • Psoriasis — Thick, scaly patches; usually not contagious
  • Rosacea — Chronic redness, especially on cheeks and nose
  • Contact Dermatitis — Reaction to chemicals or products
  • Seborrheic Dermatitis — Oily, scaly scalp condition (severe dandruff)

Contagious Conditions (Do Not Serve):

  • Tinea (ringworm) — Fungal; red, circular patches
  • Impetigo — Bacterial; honey-colored crusts
  • Herpes Simplex — Cold sores; viral and contagious
  • Warts — Viral; avoid shaving over them

🔍 Skin Cancer Awareness (ABCDE Rule)

  • Asymmetry — One half does not match the other
  • Border — Irregular, scalloped, or poorly defined edges
  • Color — Varied shades of brown, black, or other colors
  • Diameter — Larger than 6 mm (pencil eraser)
  • Evolving — Changing in size, shape, or color
Early detection saves lives. If you see suspicious lesions, politely suggest the client see a dermatologist without diagnosing.

📝 Chapter 9 Key Takeaways

  • Skin has three layers: epidermis (protective), dermis (blood vessels, glands), subcutaneous (fat)
  • Skin functions: protection, sensation, temperature regulation, excretion, vitamin D production
  • Lesions classified as primary or secondary
  • Common disorders: acne, dermatitis, psoriasis, rosacea
  • Contagious conditions (tinea, impetigo, herpes) require referral and disinfection
  • Follow ABCDE rule for suspicious moles
  • Always perform thorough skin analysis before any service
