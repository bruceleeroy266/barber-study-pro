## Question 21

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q21`  
**Section:** 10 — Packet D2: Advanced Techniques  
**Learning Objective:** Define overdirection and describe its effect on length and weight.  
**Cognitive Level:** Recall  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-035`

### Question

What is **overdirection** in haircutting?

A. Cutting hair at 180-degree elevation to preserve perimeter length  
B. Combing hair away from its natural falling position before cutting  
C. Using a razor instead of shears to create soft ends  
D. Removing bulk by sliding partially open shears along a section

### Correct Answer

**B. Combing hair away from its natural falling position before cutting**

### Explanation

Overdirection means combing hair away from where it naturally falls before cutting. It preserves length where the hair is pulled from and shifts weight toward where it is pulled to. The other options describe long-layered cutting, razor cutting, and slithering.

## Question 22

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q22`  
**Section:** 10 — Packet D2: Advanced Techniques  
**Learning Objective:** Identify hair types where razor cutting is inappropriate.  
**Cognitive Level:** Recall  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-036`

### Question

On which hair types is razor cutting generally inappropriate?

A. Coarse or thick hair  
B. Fine, fragile, or highly porous hair  
C. Curly or coily hair  
D. Straight hair that has never been colored

### Correct Answer

**B. Fine, fragile, or highly porous hair**

### Explanation

Razor cutting can fray or weaken fine, fragile, or highly porous strands because the razor slices through the cuticle aggressively. It is better suited to stronger hair types that can tolerate the softening effect without damage.

## Question 23

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q23`  
**Section:** 10 — Packet D2: Advanced Techniques  
**Learning Objective:** Define point cutting, notching, and slithering.  
**Cognitive Level:** Recall  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-037`

### Question

Which texturizing technique uses the tips of the shears to soften blunt lines and remove small amounts of bulk from the ends?

A. Notching  
B. Slithering  
C. Point cutting  
D. Slicing

### Correct Answer

**C. Point cutting**

### Explanation

Point cutting uses the tips of the shears to soften blunt lines and remove small amounts of bulk from the ends without dramatically shortening length. Notching removes weight more aggressively, slithering slides along the section, and slicing is a separate technique not emphasized in the approved deck.

## Question 24

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q24`  
**Section:** 10 — Packet D2: Advanced Techniques  
**Learning Objective:** Select the appropriate texturizing technique for a given hair condition and goal.  
**Cognitive Level:** Application  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-038`, `fc-ch16-053`

### Question

A client has very thick, heavy hair and needs significant weight release at the perimeter. Which texturizing technique is most appropriate?

A. Point cutting  
B. Notching  
C. Slithering  
D. Razor cutting

### Correct Answer

**B. Notching**

### Explanation

Notching removes weight in a spaced, aggressive pattern and is designed for thick, heavy hair that needs substantial weight release. Point cutting is too subtle for significant weight removal, slithering removes only a small amount of bulk, and razor cutting is not designed for aggressive weight removal at the perimeter.

## Question 25

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q25`  
**Section:** 10 — Packet D2: Advanced Techniques  
**Learning Objective:** Identify situations where overdirection should be avoided.  
**Cognitive Level:** Application  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-062`

### Question

A barber wants even, natural fall and balanced weight distribution throughout the haircut. Why should overdirection be avoided in this case?

A. Overdirection intentionally shifts length and weight away from natural fall  
B. Overdirection always creates graduation  
C. Overdirection removes too much perimeter length  
D. Overdirection requires 180-degree elevation

### Correct Answer

**A. Overdirection intentionally shifts length and weight away from natural fall**

### Explanation

Overdirection is used deliberately to shift length and weight from one area to another. When the goal is balanced, natural fall and even weight distribution, overdirection works against that goal because it moves hair out of its natural position before cutting.

## Question 26

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q26`  
**Section:** 10 — Packet D2: Advanced Techniques  
**Learning Objective:** Choose the best texturizing technique for a client's hair type and goal.  
**Cognitive Level:** Analysis  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-052`, `fc-ch16-054`, `fc-ch16-063`

### Question

A client has dense hair that feels heavy but wants to preserve overall length. Which technique best addresses the goal, and why?

A. Notching, because it aggressively removes weight from thick hair  
B. Slithering, because it removes a small amount of bulk while preserving length  
C. Point cutting, because it shortens the ends significantly  
D. Razor cutting, because it is safe for all dense hair types

### Correct Answer

**B. Slithering, because it removes a small amount of bulk while preserving length**

### Explanation

Slithering slides partially open shears along a section to remove a small amount of bulk while keeping the overall length intact. Notching would remove too much and change the perimeter. Point cutting primarily softens ends rather than reducing bulk. Razor cutting is not universally safe and is inappropriate for fragile or porous hair.

## Question 27

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q27`  
**Section:** 11 — Packet E1: Styling, Finishing & Chapter Completion  
**Learning Objective:** Identify thermal-styling safety rules.  
**Cognitive Level:** Recall  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-040`, `fc-ch16-041`, `fc-ch16-042`

### Question

Which statement about thermal styling safety is correct?

A. Thermal tools can be used on damp hair to speed up drying  
B. Heat protectant is optional for healthy hair  
C. Fine or damaged hair should be styled with lower heat, and tools should keep moving  
D. Thermal tools should be placed directly on the styling station between sections

### Correct Answer

**C. Fine or damaged hair should be styled with lower heat, and tools should keep moving**

### Explanation

Fine or damaged hair cannot tolerate high heat and should be styled at lower temperatures. Keeping tools moving prevents heat from concentrating in one spot and causing damage. Heat protectant is important for all hair types, thermal tools on damp hair can cause steam damage, and hot tools should rest on a heat-safe mat.

## Question 28

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q28`  
**Section:** 11 — Packet E1: Styling, Finishing & Chapter Completion  
**Learning Objective:** Determine what client education to provide before the end of a styling service.  
**Cognitive Level:** Application  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-055`, `fc-ch16-068`, `fc-ch16-067`

### Question

At the end of a styling service, what should a barber include in client education?

A. Only the price of the products used  
B. How to recreate the style at home, which products to use, and how to protect hair from heat  
C. A warning that the style will only look good in the shop  
D. The names of the tools used, but not how to use them

### Correct Answer

**B. How to recreate the style at home, which products to use, and how to protect hair from heat**

### Explanation

Client education should empower the client to recreate the style at home safely. This includes explaining products, blow-dry technique, brush selection, airflow direction, and heat protection. Simply naming prices or tools without instruction does not improve client satisfaction or loyalty.

## Question 29

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q29`  
**Section:** 11 — Packet E1: Styling, Finishing & Chapter Completion  
**Learning Objective:** Apply blow-dry technique knowledge to achieve a desired finish.  
**Cognitive Level:** Application  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-067`

### Question

A barber wants to create smoothness and shine during a blow-dry. How should the airflow be directed?

A. Upward at the roots to create volume  
B. Downward along the hair shaft, following the cuticle direction  
C. Directly into the ends to speed drying  
D. From side to side to increase movement

### Correct Answer

**B. Downward along the hair shaft, following the cuticle direction**

### Explanation

Airflow directed downward along the hair shaft smooths the cuticle and creates shine. Airflow directed upward at the roots creates volume. Side-to-side or end-focused airflow does not produce the smooth, polished finish described in the goal.

## Question 30

**Question ID:** `CH16-Q30`  
**Section:** 11 — Packet E1: Styling, Finishing & Chapter Completion  
**Learning Objective:** Analyze why styling is the final step of the haircut service.  
**Cognitive Level:** Analysis  
**Flashcard Reference:** `fc-ch16-064`

### Question

A barber finishes cutting and immediately dismisses the client without styling. What important opportunity is lost?

A. The chance to sell additional products  
B. The chance to reveal the true balance, movement, and weight distribution of the cut  
C. The chance to perform the final sanitation steps  
D. The chance to take a before-and-after photo

### Correct Answer

**B. The chance to reveal the true balance, movement, and weight distribution of the cut**

### Explanation

Styling is the final step of the service because it reveals how the haircut truly balances, moves, and behaves. Wet hair can hide uneven layers or heavy spots. Without styling, the barber cannot fully evaluate the cut or show the client the finished result. Sanitation and photos are important, but they do not replace the evaluative purpose of styling.
