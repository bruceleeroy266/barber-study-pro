CHAPTER 17

Chemical Texture Services

Permanent waving, chemical relaxing, and curl reformation

🎯 Why Study Chemical Texture Services?

Chemical texture services change the natural wave pattern of hair. Understanding these services allows you to:

  • Add curl or wave to straight hair (permanent waving)
  • Remove curl or wave from hair (chemical relaxing)
  • Reform curl patterns (soft-curl perm)
  • Expand your service offerings and increase revenue
  • Understand the chemistry behind hair restructuring
Key Point: Chemical texture services permanently alter the hair's structure. Proper analysis and technique are critical to prevent damage.

🔬 Types of Chemical Texture Services

Permanent Waving:

Permanent Wave (Perm) — A chemical service that adds curl or wave to straight hair by breaking and reforming the disulfide bonds.

Chemical Hair Relaxing:

Thio Relaxer — Uses ammonium thioglycolate (same as permanent wave solution) to straighten curly or wavy hair.
Hydroxide Relaxer — Uses sodium hydroxide, potassium hydroxide, or calcium hydroxide to straighten hair. Also called "lye" or "no-lye" relaxers.

Curl Reformation:

Soft-Curl Perm — A combination service that first relaxes the hair, then re-curls it to create a softer, looser curl pattern.

🔍 Hair Analysis for Chemical Services

Before performing any chemical texture service, a thorough hair analysis is essential.

Factors to Analyze:

FactorWhat to Look ForImpact on Service
PorosityAbility to absorb moistureAffects chemical absorption and timing
TextureFine, medium, or coarseDetermines rod size and processing time
ElasticityAbility to stretch and returnIndicates hair strength
DensityAmount of hairAffects sectioning and product amount
LengthShort, medium, or longDetermines wrapping pattern
Growth directionHair streams and cowlicksAffects sectioning

Porosity Levels:

Resistant (Low Porosity) — Cuticle is tight and compact. Hair is resistant to chemical absorption. May require longer processing time.
Normal (Average Porosity) — Cuticle is slightly raised. Processes as expected with standard timing.
Porous (High Porosity) — Cuticle is lifted or damaged. Hair absorbs chemicals quickly. May require shorter processing time and gentler products.

Porosity Test:

  1. Slide thumb and index finger from end to scalp on dry hair strand
  2. Smooth: Resistant (low porosity)
  3. Slight roughness: Normal porosity
  4. Very rough or breaks: Porous (high porosity)

💬 Client Consultation

A thorough consultation helps determine if the client is a good candidate for chemical texture services.

Key Questions:

  • What is your desired result? (curlier, straighter, looser curl)
  • What is the current condition of your hair?
  • Have you had any chemical services in the past?
  • Do you have any scalp conditions or sensitivities?
  • What is your lifestyle and maintenance routine?

Scalp Analysis:

Do NOT proceed if you observe:
  • Open cuts or abrasions
  • Signs of scalp disease
  • Inflammation or irritation
  • Excessive dryness or scaling

🌀 Permanent Waving

Permanent waves use chemicals to break disulfide bonds, reshape the hair around rods, and reform bonds in the new curl pattern.

Basic Process:

  1. Hair analysis and consultation
  2. Shampoo (no conditioner)
  3. Section and wrap hair on perm rods
  4. Apply waving solution
  5. Process according to manufacturer's directions
  6. Rinse thoroughly
  7. Apply neutralizer
  8. Process and rinse
  9. Remove rods and style

Rod Selection:

  • Small rods: Tighter curls
  • Medium rods: Medium curls/waves
  • Large rods: Loose waves

Wrapping Techniques:

Spiral Wrap — Hair is wrapped at an angle to create a spiral curl pattern.
Croquignole Wrap — Hair is wrapped from ends to scalp, creating a uniform curl.

🧪 Chemical Relaxers

Chemical relaxers straighten curly or wavy hair by breaking disulfide bonds and reforming them in a straight pattern.

Types of Relaxers:

TypeActive IngredientCharacteristics
Thio RelaxerAmmonium thioglycolateSame as perm solution; milder; can be used for curl reformation
Sodium Hydroxide (Lye)NaOHStrong; effective on coarse hair; requires careful application
Calcium Hydroxide (No-Lye)CaOHGentler than lye; may leave calcium buildup
Lithium/Potassium HydroxideLiOH/KOHAlternative to sodium hydroxide

Relaxer Application - Virgin Hair:

  1. Perform hair and scalp analysis
  2. Drape client for chemical service
  3. Apply protective base cream to hairline and ears
  4. Part hair into four sections
  5. Begin application at the most resistant area (usually back)
  6. Apply ¼ to ½ inch from scalp (do not touch scalp initially)
  7. Work down the section toward hairline
  8. Apply to scalp only in last few minutes
  9. Process according to manufacturer's directions
  10. Perform strand tests periodically
  11. Rinse thoroughly with warm water
  12. Shampoo with neutralizing shampoo
  13. Condition and style

Relaxer Retouch:

  • Apply relaxer only to new growth (not previously relaxed hair)
  • Overlapping relaxer on previously relaxed hair causes breakage
  • Work relaxer down to scalp only in final minutes
  • Do not relax mid-shaft or ends during retouch
CAUTION: Never intentionally overlap previously relaxed hair. This will result in damage and possible hair breakage.

🔄 Curl Reformation (Soft-Curl Perm)

Curl reformation combines relaxing and perming to create looser, softer curls.

Process Overview:

  1. Perform hair analysis (ensure hair is not previously treated with hydroxide relaxer)
  2. Apply thio cream relaxer to loosen natural curl
  3. Rinse thoroughly
  4. Section hair into panels
  5. Wrap hair on perm rods
  6. Apply thio waving lotion
  7. Process and check for curl development
  8. Rinse thoroughly
  9. Apply neutralizer
  10. Process and rinse
  11. Remove rods and style
Important: Hair that has been treated with hydroxide relaxers cannot be treated with thio products. The chemicals are not compatible.

⚠️ Safety Precautions

  • Always wear gloves when applying chemical products
  • Apply protective base cream to hairline and ears
  • Never apply relaxer directly to scalp until final minutes
  • Perform strand tests to check processing
  • Follow manufacturer's directions exactly
  • Rinse thoroughly to remove all chemicals
  • Use neutralizing shampoo after relaxers
  • Do not perform chemical services on compromised hair
  • Check for scalp conditions before starting
  • Keep chemicals away from eyes
  • Have MSDS sheets available for all products

🧹 Clean-Up and Disinfection

After every chemical texture service:

  • Clean and disinfect all tools and implements
  • Clean and disinfect work area
  • Dispose of single-use items
  • Place all used linens, towels, and capes in laundry
  • Dispose of chemical product according to manufacturer
  • Wash hands thoroughly

📝 Chapter 17 Key Takeaways

  • Chemical texture services permanently alter hair structure
  • Services include: permanent waving, chemical relaxing, curl reformation
  • Thio relaxers use ammonium thioglycolate
  • Hydroxide relaxers use sodium, potassium, or calcium hydroxide
  • Always perform hair analysis before chemical services
  • Test for porosity, texture, and elasticity
  • Three porosity levels: resistant, normal, porous
  • Never overlap relaxer on previously relaxed hair
  • Apply protective base cream to hairline and ears
  • Hydroxide and thio products are NOT compatible
  • Always use neutralizing shampoo after relaxers
  • Follow all safety precautions and manufacturer's directions
