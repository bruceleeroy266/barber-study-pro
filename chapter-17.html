CHAPTER 17

Chemical Texture Services

Permanent waves, relaxers, and curl reformation

🎯 Why Study Chemical Texture?

  • Increases versatility and services offered
  • Boosts income in the barbershop
  • Builds client loyalty and professional confidence
  • Prevents damage when performed correctly

⚗️ Chemistry of Texture Services

Services permanently alter disulfide bonds in the cortex:

  • Reduction — Alkaline solution breaks disulfide bonds (cystine → cysteine)
  • Oxidation — Neutralizer rebuilds bonds (cysteine → cystine)

Types:

  • Permanent Waving — Changes straight/wavy hair to curly
  • Chemical Relaxing — Straightens overly curly hair
  • Curl Reformation — Partially straightens then re-curls to larger pattern
Hydroxide relaxers are NOT compatible with thio products.

📊 Hair Analysis (Critical!)

Porosity is the #1 factor affecting processing time:

  • Resistant — Tight cuticle; needs stronger solution, longer processing
  • Normal — Processes as expected
  • Porous — Raised cuticle; absorbs quickly, needs gentler solution

Other Factors:

  • Texture — Coarse needs more processing; fine is more fragile
  • Elasticity — Normal stretches 50% and returns; poor indicates damage
  • Density — Thick needs smaller subsections

🌀 Permanent Waves

Rod Types:

  • Concave — Most common; tighter center curl
  • Straight — Uniform curl
  • Diameter — Controls curl size (smaller = tighter)

Wrapping Techniques:

  • Croquignole — Ends to scalp (overlapping)
  • Spiral — Vertical spiraling for uniform curl
  • Piggyback — For long/dense hair

Types:

  • Alkaline (Cold) — pH 9.0-9.6; strong, fast, room temp
  • True Acid — pH 4.5-7.0; soft curl, needs heat
  • Exothermic — Generates own heat
Test curls in 3 areas before full service. Underprocessing = limp wave; Overprocessing = frizzy, damaged hair.

📝 Chapter 17 Key Takeaways

  • Chemical texture permanently alters disulfide bonds in cortex
  • Porosity is #1 factor affecting processing time
  • Always perform thorough consultation + hair analysis
  • Match product strength, rod size, wrapping technique to hair
  • Neutralization is critical — improper neutralization causes curl relaxation
  • Safety first: Never perm damaged scalp/hair; follow manufacturer directions
