CHAPTER 7

Basics of Chemistry

Matter, elements, pH scale, and chemical reactions in barbering

🎯 Why Study Chemistry?

Barbers work with chemical products daily — shampoos, conditioners, hair tonics, shaving creams, astringents, and chemical services. Understanding chemistry helps you:

  • Choose the right products for different hair and skin types
  • Understand how products interact with hair and skin (e.g., pH effects)
  • Perform services safely and predict results
  • Avoid damage to hair or skin

⚗️ Chemistry Areas

Organic Chemistry Study of substances containing carbon (most living things). Examples: gasoline, synthetic fabrics, plastics, pesticides, many hair products.
Inorganic Chemistry Study of substances without carbon. Examples: metals, minerals, water, air, ammonia.

⚛️ Matter and Its Properties

Matter is anything that occupies space and has mass. Three physical states:

  • Solid — Definite shape and volume (ice, wax)
  • Liquid — Definite volume, takes shape of container (water, shampoo)
  • Gas — No definite shape or volume; expands to fill container (air, vapor)

Physical changes — No new substance formed (melting ice, cutting hair)

Chemical changes — New substances formed (oxidation during hair lightening)

🔬 Elements and Compounds

Element Simplest form of matter; cannot be broken down chemically. 118 known elements; ~98 occur naturally.
Atom Smallest particle of an element that retains its chemical identity. Basic building blocks of all matter.
Compound Chemical combination of two or more atoms of different elements in fixed proportions. Examples: water (H₂O), salt (NaCl).
Hair Composition: Made primarily of keratin protein containing carbon, oxygen, hydrogen, nitrogen, and sulfur (COHNS).

🔄 Chemical Reactions

  • Oxidation — Addition of oxygen or loss of hydrogen/electrons. Example: Hydrogen peroxide lightens hair by oxidizing melanin.
  • Reduction — Loss of oxygen or addition of hydrogen/electrons.
  • Redox reactions — Reduction and oxidation always occur together.
  • Endothermic — Absorbs heat/energy
  • Exothermic — Releases heat/energy

📊 pH Scale

Measures acidity or alkalinity (0-14):

  • Below 7 — Acidic (shrinks/hardens hair cuticle; conditioners, toners)
  • 7 — Neutral (pure water)
  • Above 7 — Alkaline (softens/swells cuticle; shampoos, relaxers, perms)
Natural hair and skin pH is slightly acidic (4.5–5.5). This acid mantle protects against bacteria and keeps cuticle smooth.

📝 Chapter 7 Key Takeaways

  • Organic chemistry studies carbon-containing substances; inorganic studies non-carbon
  • Atoms are basic building blocks; elements cannot be broken down; compounds combine elements
  • Redox reactions (oxidation-reduction) power permanent waving, coloring, and lightening
  • pH scale (0-14): Hair/skin prefer slightly acidic (4.5-5.5) for protection
  • Acidic products close cuticle; alkaline products open it
  • Match product pH to client's hair and skin condition to avoid damage
