**2. Hair Shaft Structures (Pages 249-250)**
- **Cuticle:** Outermost protective layer, overlapping scales like shingles
- **Cortex:** Middle layer (90% of hair weight), fibrous protein, strength, elasticity, pigment
- **Medulla:** Innermost layer, composed of round cells, may be absent in fine/blonde hair

**4. Side Bonds of Hair Cortex (Page 251)**
- **Hydrogen bonds:** Weak, physical, broken by water/heat
- **Salt bonds:** Weak, physical, changes in pH
- **Disulfide bonds:** Strong, chemical, broken by perms/thio relaxers

**6. Hair Pigment & Color (Page 253)**
- Melanin in cortex gives natural color
- Eumelanin (brown/black) vs Pheomelanin (red/ginger/yellow)
- Gray hair = lack of pigment, air spaces replace melanin
- Figure 10-9: Straight, curly, extremely curly examples

**7. Wave Pattern (Pages 253-254)**
- Straight hair: round cross-section
- Wavy/curly hair: oval to round cross-section
- Extremely curly hair: elliptical cross-section
- Figure 10-10: Hair color and texture basis diagram
- Table 10-3: Wave pattern and cross sections

**8. Growth Cycles (Pages 255-256)**
- **Anagen (growth phase):** New cells manufactured, lasts 2-10 years, 90% of scalp hair
- **Catagen (transition phase):** Follicle shrinks, bulb disappears, forms rounded club
- **Telogen (resting/shedding phase):** Old hair sheds, less than 10% of scalp hair, lasts 3-6 months
- Figure 10-11: Vellus hair vs Terminal hair diagram
- Normal shedding: 75-100 hairs per day
- Growth rate: 0.5 inch/month average

**9. Growth Patterns (Page 256)**
- Hair stream: follicles sloping in same direction
- Whorl: circular/swirl pattern
- Cowlick: tuft of hair standing straight up
- Important for cutting and styling

**10. Myths vs Facts About Hair Growth (Page 257)**
- Myth: Shaving makes hair grow back faster/darker - FALSE
- Myth: Scalp massage increases growth - FALSE (stimulates but doesn't increase)
- Myth: Gray hair is coarser - FALSE (same texture, just lacks pigment)
- Myth: Natural curl determined by race - FALSE (all races can have various curl patterns)

**11. Hair Loss (Alopecia) (Pages 257-259)**
- Normal shedding vs abnormal hair loss
- Emotional impact on clients
- 7 types of abnormal hair loss
- **Figure 10-12:** Miniaturization of hair follicle
- **Figure 10-13:** Stages of male pattern baldness (1-8)

**12. Types of Alopecia (Pages 258-259)**
- **Alopecia areata:** Autoimmune, sudden bald spots, can progress to totalis
- **Androgenic alopecia (male pattern baldness):** Genetic, age, hormones, begins early teens-40
- **Alopecia totalis:** Complete scalp hair loss
- **Alopecia universalis:** Complete body hair loss

**13. Hair Loss Treatments (Page 260)**
- **Minoxidil (Rogaine):** Topical, FDA-approved, 2% and 5%, stimulates growth
- **Finasteride (Propecia):** Oral, prescription, men only, slows hair loss
- Surgical options: hair transplantation, follicle grafting ($8,000-$20,000+)
- Nonmedical: wigs, hair systems, extensions

**14. Hair Disorders (Pages 260-261)**
- **Canities (gray hair):** Congenital or acquired, lack of pigment
- **Ringed hair:** Alternating bands of gray/pigmented
- **Hypertrichosis:** Abnormal terminal hair growth in unusual areas
- **Trichoptilosis (split ends):** Brittle hair, split/nodular swellings
- **Trichorrhexis nodosa:** Knotted hair, brittle nodular swellings
- **Monilethrix:** Beaded hair, breaks easily

**15. Dandruff Types (Pages 261-262)**
- **Pityriasis:** Excessive shedding of flaky skin cells
- **Pityriasis capitis simplex:** Classic dandruff, large visible clumps
- **Pityriasis steatoides:** Greasy/waxy scales mixed with sebum (severe)
- **Malassezia fungus:** Natural fungus that causes dandruff when overgrows
- **Figure 10-14:** Pityriasis capitis simplex example
- **Figure 10-15:** Pityriasis steatoides example

**16. Fungal Infections - Tinea (Pages 262-263)**
- **Tinea:** Medical term for ringworm, highly contagious
- **Tinea barbae:** Ringworm of beard, red papules/pustules, hair loss
- **Tinea capitis:** Ringworm of scalp, red papules on scalp, sulfer-yellow crusts
- **Tinea favosa:** Honeycomb ringworm, dry sulfur-yellow crusts, musty odor, VERY contagious
- **Figure 10-16, 10-17:** Tinea barbae and tinea favosa examples
- All tinea requires physician referral

**17. Parasitic Infestations (Page 263)**
- **Pediculosis capitis:** Head lice, severe itching, nits on hair strands
- **Scabies:** Mites burrow under skin, intense itching, highly contagious
- **Figure 10-18:** Head lice image
- **Figure 10-19:** Scabies example
- Proper cleaning and disinfection procedures required

**18. Bacterial Infections (Page 264)**
- **Folliculitis:** Inflammation of hair follicles (bacterial/viral/ingrown)
- **Folliculitis barbae and pseudofolliculitis barbae:** Barber's itch
- **Furuncle (boil):** Acute deep-seated bacterial infection
- **Carbuncle:** Multiple boils connected, larger, refer to physician
- **Pseudofolliculitis barbae (razor bumps):** Ingrown hairs, chronic inflammatory
- **Sycosis vulgaris:** Chronic bacterial infection around follicles
- **Figure 10-20:** Furuncle (boil) diagram

**19. Hair and Scalp Analysis (Pages 265-268)**
- **Observation:** Sight (dry/oily), hearing (client concerns), smell, touch
- **Scalp analysis first:** Check for disorders/contraindications
- Do NOT begin service if parasites present
- Do NOT proceed with chemical services if irritation/abrasions present

**20. Hair Texture Analysis (Pages 266-267)**
- **Coarse hair:** Largest diameter, stronger structure, requires more time/products
- **Medium hair:** Most common, normal texture
- **Fine hair:** Smallest diameter, fragile, easier to process, smaller diameter of strand
- **Testing method:** Feel single strand between fingers
- **Figures 10-21, 10-22, 10-23:** Coarse, medium, fine hair microscope images

**21. Hair Density Analysis (Page 267)**
- Number of hairs per square inch
- High, medium, or low density
- Different from texture
- Average: 2,200 strands per square inch
- **Table 10-4:** Average number of hairs by color (Blond 140K, Brown 110K, Black 108K, Red 80K)
- **Figure 10-25:** Normal hair density image

**22. Hair Porosity Analysis (Pages 267-268)**
- Ability to absorb moisture
- Determined by cuticle condition
- **Resistant (low porosity):** Compact cuticle, repels moisture
- **Normal porosity:** Average absorption
- **Overly porous (high porosity):** Damaged cuticle, absorbs too quickly
- **Testing method:** Slide strands between fingers toward scalp, rough = overporous
- **Figures 10-26, 10-27, 10-28:** Low, high porosity examples and testing

**23. Hair Elasticity Analysis (Page 268)**
- Ability to stretch and return to original length without breaking
- Indicates side bond strength
- **Normal elasticity:** Stretches and returns without breaking
- **Low/poor elasticity:** Brittle, breaks easily
- **Testing method:** Stretch wet hair, normal stretches 50%, returns to original
- **Figure 10-29:** Testing for elasticity diagram
