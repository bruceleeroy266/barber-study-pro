CHAPTER 13

Shaving & Facial-Hair Design

14 shaving areas, razor positions, and professional shave

🎯 Why Study Shaving?

The professional shave is one of the most traditional and relaxing services. Studying this helps you:

  • Master the 14 shaving areas and correct razor positions
  • Perform a safe, comfortable professional shave
  • Create balanced mustache and beard designs
  • Prevent nicks, irritation, and ingrown hairs
Key Point: Shaving is both a technical skill and an art. Precision, safety, and client comfort are equally important.

🪒 Fundamentals of Shaving

Basic Guidelines:

  • Always analyze skin and hair growth patterns before starting
  • Do not shave if skin shows infection, open sores, or severe irritation
  • Keep client's skin moist; keep your fingers dry for control
  • Position client's head as needed for access
  • Remove excess lather with thumb before each stroke
  • Right-handed barbers stand at client's right side; left-handed at left
  • Use smooth gliding strokes leading with point of blade

Hair Growth Terms:

  • Grain — Direction hair grows out of follicle
  • Grain change — Area where hair grows in opposing directions (common on neck)
Key Point: Shave with the grain on the first pass for safety. Going against the grain too early increases risk of irritation and ingrown hairs.

✋ Razor Positions and Strokes

Four basic razor positions:

  • Freehand — Most common; used on many facial areas
  • Backhand — Used for certain neck and cheek areas
  • Reverse-freehand — Often used on lower neck or under chin
  • Reverse-backhand — Used on opposite side for balance

Stroke Technique:

  • Smooth gliding motion leading with point of razor
  • Stroke length: 1–3 inches
  • Maintain consistent 30-degree angle to skin
  • Stretch skin taut with free hand

🪒 The Professional Shave

Four Main Parts:

  • A. Prepare the Client — Seat comfortably, drape with towel and cape, wash hands, analyze skin
  • B. Prepare the Face — Apply hot towel to soften beard, work warm lather evenly into beard
  • C. The Facial Shave — Shave 14 areas systematically with the grain, wipe razor after each stroke, re-lather as needed
  • D. Finishing Steps — Apply facial cream, warm towel, toner, powder if desired, comb hair, offer neck shave

Second-Time-Over (Optional):

  • Dampen face and check for rough spots
  • Use very light freehand strokes (with or across grain) for closer shave

🧔 Mustache and Beard Design

Mustache Trim:

  • Consult client on desired shape and length
  • Trim to even length using outliner
  • Remove bulk with shears-over-comb or clipper-over-comb
  • Shape and detail with outliner or razor

Beard Design:

  • Consult on length, density, and shape
  • Check for moles or growths
  • Trim excess with shears, clippers, outliner, razor
  • Establish design lines starting at center under chin, work outward
  • Blend and taper for natural look
Beard design is artistic — balance facial features, follow natural growth, consider client's maintenance ability.

📝 Chapter 13 Key Takeaways

  • Four razor positions: freehand, backhand, reverse-freehand, reverse-backhand
  • Always shave with the grain on first pass
  • Maintain 30-degree angle and stretch skin taut
  • Professional shave has four parts: preparation, face prep, shave, finishing
  • Mustache/beard design requires client consultation for balanced results
  • Safety and infection control are critical
