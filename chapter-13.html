CHAPTER 13

Shaving and Facial-Hair Design

Shaving techniques, razor handling, and beard design

🎯 Why Study Shaving?

Shaving is one of the most traditional and requested services in barbering. Mastering shaving techniques allows you to:

  • Provide a close, comfortable shave without irritation
  • Design and maintain facial hair styles
  • Understand different hair types and growth patterns
  • Perform neck shaves and mustache trims
  • Create various beard designs
Key Point: A proper shave requires understanding hair growth patterns, proper razor handling, and attention to safety.

🪒 Fundamentals of Shaving

The 14 Shaving Areas of the Face:

Razor Positions:

Freehand Stroke — Razor held in natural position with blade facing downward. Used for most shaving areas following the grain.
Backhand Stroke — Razor turned so blade faces upward. Used for specific areas like sides of mouth and jawline.
Reverse-Freehand Stroke — Razor held in freehand position but moved upward against the grain. Used for neck areas and second-time-over shaves.

✅ Guidelines for Shaving

DO:

  • Always shave with the grain on the first pass
  • Stretch the skin taut with your free hand
  • Use short, confident strokes
  • Keep the razor clean - wipe after each stroke
  • Use fresh, sharp blades
  • Apply adequate lather
  • Check for moles, blemishes, or skin conditions before shaving
  • Communicate with the client throughout the service

DON'T:

  • Never shave over the same area repeatedly without re-lathering
  • Don't use excessive pressure - let the razor do the work
  • Never shave against the grain on the first pass
  • Don't rush the service
  • Never use a dull blade
  • Don't ignore skin irritation or razor burn

🧔 Hair Type Considerations

Straight Hair:

  • Grows in consistent direction
  • Easier to determine grain direction
  • Standard shaving techniques apply

Wavy or Curly Hair:

  • May grow in multiple directions
  • More prone to ingrown hairs
  • Extra care needed when stretching skin
  • May require mapping growth pattern before shaving

Coarse Hair:

  • Requires more thorough preparation
  • May benefit from hot towel treatment
  • Second-time-over shave may be necessary

🪒 The Facial Shave Procedure

Preparation:

  1. Drape client properly with cape and neck strip
  2. Position headrest for comfort
  3. Recline chair to comfortable working angle
  4. Wash hands thoroughly
  5. Check skin for any conditions or concerns

First-Time-Over Shave:

Shave with the grain using freehand and backhand strokes as appropriate for each area:

  • Areas 1-2: Right side of face using freehand and backhand
  • Area 3: Upper lip - stretch skin by lifting nose
  • Area 4: Right neck - stretch skin taut
  • Area 5: Right lower neck - reverse-freehand stroke
  • Areas 6-10: Left side (mirror of right side)
  • Areas 11-12: Chin and below
  • Areas 13-14: Lower neck and chin - reverse-freehand

Second-Time-Over Shave (Optional):

  1. Dampen face with water
  2. Re-lather if necessary
  3. Shave against or across the grain for closer shave
  4. Use light, gentle strokes

Finishing Steps:

  1. Apply light facial cream or moisturizer with effleurage movements
  2. Apply warm towel (not hot) over face
  3. Remove towel and wipe excess product
  4. Apply skin toner or mild astringent
  5. Apply powder if desired
  6. Comb hair neatly

🦢 The Neck Shave

A neck shave cleans up hair growth below the natural hairline on the neck.

Procedure:

  1. Raise chair to upright position after facial shave
  2. Tuck towel around back of neck
  3. Apply lather to neck area
  4. Shave right side behind ear down neck using freehand stroke
  5. Shave left side using reverse-backhand stroke
  6. Shave nape area with freehand stroke
  7. Clean with witch hazel or antiseptic
  8. Dry thoroughly
  9. Style hair as desired

👨 Mustache Trim

Procedure:

  1. Consult with client about desired shape and length
  2. Comb mustache down
  3. Trim to desired length with outliner or shears
  4. Remove bulk with shear-over-comb or outliner-over-comb
  5. Shape and detail with outliner or razor
  6. Apply shaving cream if using razor
  7. Wipe off excess product

🧔 Beard Designs

Beard Trim on Medium-Textured Hair:

  1. Comb through beard and check for moles or growths
  2. Apply headrest cover and recline chair
  3. Place towel under chin if needed
  4. Trim excess hair with clipper-over-comb
  5. Trim and shape mustache
  6. Establish design line under chin
  7. Establish design line at jaw and sideburn areas
  8. Establish design line in cheek areas
  9. Contour under mustache
  10. Shave neck areas 5 and 10
  11. Shave neck areas 12 and 13
  12. Finish and style

Beard Redesign:

Creating a new beard design from an existing beard:

  1. Use outliner to create new guide starting in center under chin
  2. Cut right and left of center to establish new design line
  3. Reshape mustache and chin areas
  4. Trim excess mustache hair
  5. Trim excess hair in cheek areas
  6. Trim excess hair under chin
  7. Start in center under chin and establish guide with outliner
  8. Work left and right of center to establish design line to back of jaw
  9. Move to right side and cut design from sideburn down to guide
  10. Use trimmer to outline cheek and upper areas
  11. Taper and blend beard using shear-over-comb or clipper-over-comb
  12. Cut in new design line in cheek areas
  13. Recline client and apply shaving cream along new outlines
  14. Shave sideburn and cheek outlines

Common Beard Designs:

Full Goatee — Remove cheek and side hair to create a full goatee covering chin and connecting to mustache.
Chin Goatee — Remove excess hair under chin to create a chin goatee; shape the patch; establish mustache length at corners.
Beard Trim on Curly-Textured Hair — Similar procedure but may require different techniques due to hair texture and growth patterns.

🧼 Clean-Up and Disinfection

After every shaving service:

  • Clean and disinfect all tools and implements
  • Clean and disinfect the work area
  • Sweep up hair and deposit in closed receptacle
  • Deposit used blades in sharps container
  • Dispose of single-use items
  • Place all used linens, towels, and capes in laundry
  • Wash hands thoroughly
Important: Never leave used blades on the counter or in regular trash. Always use a proper sharps container.

⚠️ Safety Precautions

  • Always use sharp, clean blades
  • Never share blades between clients
  • Check for skin conditions before shaving
  • Use caution around moles and blemishes
  • Stretch skin properly to avoid cuts
  • Never shave over active acne or irritated skin
  • Keep work area clean and organized
  • Dispose of blades properly
  • Use gloves if client has any skin conditions
  • Stop immediately if client experiences discomfort

📝 Chapter 13 Key Takeaways

  • There are 14 shaving areas of the face
  • Three razor positions: freehand, backhand, reverse-freehand
  • Always shave with the grain on first pass
  • Stretch the skin taut with free hand while shaving
  • Know the difference between first-time-over and second-time-over shaves
  • Procedures: facial shave, neck shave, mustache trim, beard designs
  • Common beard designs: full goatee, chin goatee
  • Always follow proper clean-up and disinfection procedures
  • Dispose of used blades in sharps container
  • Check for moles, blemishes, and skin conditions before shaving
