CHAPTER 5

Implements, Tools & Equipment

Combs, brushes, shears, clippers, razors, and maintenance

🎯 Why Study This Chapter?

Choosing the right tools, maintaining them properly, and using them safely directly affects the quality of your work, client satisfaction, and your own efficiency and safety.

Key Point: Always invest in good-quality implements. Cheap or poorly maintained tools can damage hair, cause client discomfort, and lead to poor results.

🪮 Combs

A good comb should be durable, slightly flexible, and easy to clean and disinfect.

  • All-purpose styling comb — Used for general haircutting and styling; has both wide and fine teeth
  • Tail comb — Features a long, pointed tail for sectioning and parting long hair
  • Wide-toothed comb — Ideal for combing through curly or thick hair textures without causing breakage
  • Hard rubber combs — Durable and slightly flexible; can retain heat but avoid extended contact with strong disinfectants

🖌️ Brushes

  • Synthetic bristle brush — Made of flexible or rigid nylon bristles; good for general styling
  • Neck brush — Used to remove loose hair clippings from the client's neck and shoulders
  • Lather brush — Used to apply and work up shaving cream or soap lather

✂️ Haircutting Shears (Scissors)

Parts of Shears:

  • Blades (cutting edge)
  • Shank (handle area)
  • Finger grip / thumb grip
  • Finger tang (rest for the little finger to relieve pressure)
  • Tension screw (controls distance and pressure between blades)

Styles:

  • French style — Has a finger tang
  • Offset handle — More ergonomic; shorter thumb shank reduces overextension
Key Point: Even the finest shears perform poorly if not set correctly or if tension is wrong. Regular sharpening and proper tension adjustment are essential.

🔌 Clippers and Trimmers

Types:

  • Adjustable-blade clippers — Blades attached with screws; blade size can be changed
  • Detachable-blade clippers — Blades snap on and off; different sizes for different lengths
  • Outliners / trimmers — Smaller, precise tools for outlining, detailing, and cleaning necklines

Parts of Clippers:

  • Blades, motor, housing, cord or battery, switch
Key Point: Clippers cut shorter when used against the grain. Always use the appropriate guard or blade size and keep blades oiled and clean.

🪒 Straight Razors

Types:

  • Conventional straight razor — Requires honing and stropping
  • Changeable-blade razor — Uses disposable blades; safer for beginners

Parts:

  • Blade (grind determines shape; width measured in eighths or sixteenths of an inch)
  • Handle
  • Tang (part that extends into the handle)
  • Shank
  • Pivot

Honing and Stropping:

  • Hone — Abrasive stone that grinds and sharpens the edge; must be perfectly flat
  • Strop — Leather or canvas strip used to align and polish the edge after honing
Safety Note: Use a safety guard for beginners. Always keep the razor closed when not in use.

📝 Chapter 5 Key Takeaways

  • Principal tools: combs, brushes, shears, clippers/outliners, and razors
  • Combs and brushes must be chosen for the hair texture and service; disinfect properly
  • Shears require correct tension, grip, and maintenance for smooth cutting
  • Clippers are versatile; guards control length; use with or against the grain as needed
  • Straight razors demand quality, proper honing/stropping, and safe handling
  • Safety and sanitation are non-negotiable — clean, disinfect, and store tools correctly after every use
  • Always match the tool to the job for best results and client comfort
