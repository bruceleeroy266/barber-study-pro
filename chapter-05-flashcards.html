2/24

Comb Materials

Click to reveal

4 main materials:

  • Hard rubber — flexible/durable, deteriorates in disinfectant
  • Carbon — antistatic, heat/chemical resistant
  • Graphite — less rigid
  • Metal — retains heat, may corrode
3/24

Comb Types & Uses

Click to reveal
  • Narrow/fine teeth — precision cutting/detail
  • Wide/coarse teeth — detangle, distribute product
  • Combination — versatility
  • Taper combs — blending, necklines, mustaches
  • Wide-tooth — curly hair, relaxer
  • Tail combs — sectioning, partings, color
  • Pick combs — textured/tightly curled hair
4/24

Brush Types

Click to reveal
  • Synthetic bristle — general brushing/detangling
  • Natural (boar) — polishes hair, distributes sebum; best for fine/delicate hair
  • Mixed, paddle, round, vented, wet — specific needs

Purpose: Control, smooth, wave, add fullness, stimulate scalp

5/24

Cast vs. Forged Shears

Click to reveal

Cast:

  • Cheaper
  • More porous/weak
  • Less sharp

Forged:

  • Hammered/compressed
  • Denser, more durable
  • Stays sharp longer
  • Higher quality
6/24

Shear Blade Edges

Click to reveal

Convex:

  • Very sharp, glides easily
  • Clean cuts, less tension
  • Good for slide cutting
  • Sharpen every 4–6 months

Beveled:

  • Serrated, grips hair
  • More tension, durable
  • NOT for slide cutting
  • Sharpen every 9–12 months
7/24

Shear Handle Designs

Click to reveal
  • Opposing — Traditional
  • Offset — Ergonomic
  • Crane — Reduces stress on hand/wrist

Preferred size: 6½" – 7½"

8/24

Texturizing vs. Thinning Shears

Click to reveal

Texturizing/Chunking:

  • Wider teeth
  • Creates patterns/texture

Thinning/Blending:

  • Many narrow teeth
  • Removes bulk without lines
9/24

Proper Shear Holding

Click to reveal
  • Ring finger in finger grip (still blade)
  • Little finger on brace/tang
  • Thumb in thumb grip (moving blade)
  • Thumb not past cuticle

Palming: Pivot shears into palm while combing; transfer comb after combing

10/24

Shear Maintenance — NEVER

Click to reveal

Never:

  • Drop them
  • Cut anything but human hair
  • Use harsh chemicals

Always:

  • Clean/disinfect after each use
  • Adjust tension daily (25–35% drop test)
  • Protect in transport
11/24

Clipper Types

Click to reveal

Detachable-blade:

  • Blades removed/attached while running
  • Different sizes for different lengths

Adjustable-blade:

  • Open/close with lever
  • Guards extend length
12/24

Clipper Motor Types

Click to reveal
  • Rotary — High power/speed, heavy-duty, best for thick/coarse/damp hair
  • Pivot — Quieter, good for thick/coarse hair
  • Magnetic — Reliable for fine textures

Cordless: Convenient but may have less power

13/24

Outliners/Trimmers

Click to reveal
  • Fine blades for precision
  • Light pressure to avoid irritation/ingrown hairs
  • Straight or T-shaped blade
  • T-blade preferred for rounded areas
  • Essential for facial hair and detailing

Caution: Never adjust blades flush; follow manufacturer guidelines

14/24

Blades & Guards

Click to reveal

Blades:

  • Materials: carbon steel or ceramic
  • 00000 = closest cut

Guards (Attachment Combs):

  • Materials: plastic/rubber/steel
  • Allow longer lengths
  • Supplemental only — not for infection control
15/24

Straight Razor Types

Click to reveal

Changeable-blade (disposable):

  • Preferred in shops
  • Better infection control
  • More convenient

Conventional:

  • Requires honing and stropping
  • Some states prohibit — check local rules
16/24

Razor Holding by Service

Click to reveal
  • Shaving: Ball of thumb + first two fingers on shank; little finger on tang
  • Honing/stropping: Flat sides of shank
  • Haircutting: Thumb at bottom or handle straightened
  • Palming with comb: Blade away from comb
17/24

Conventional Razor Grinds

Click to reveal

Concave (hollow-ground):

  • Preferred for sharpening
  • Detects resistance

Wedge:

  • Sharp angle
  • Excellent for coarse beards
  • More challenging to use

Premium finish: Crocus finish

18/24

Hone vs. Strop

Click to reveal

Hone:

  • Abrasive stone
  • Grinds sharp edge
  • Types: natural, synthetic, combination

Strop:

  • Leather or canvas strip
  • Smooths/polishes edge
  • Types: combination, canvas, Russian, shell

Order: Hone first, then strop

20/24

Thermal Styling Tools

Click to reveal
  • Thermal irons (curling/flat/Marcel)
  • Pressing combs
  • Electric stove

Safety:

  • Test temperature on tissue

21/24

Electrotherapy Types

Click to reveal

High-frequency:

  • Stimulates blood flow
  • Enhances product absorption
  • Discovered by Nikola Tesla

Galvanic:

  • Introduces water-soluble products
  • Used during facials

Training recommended

22/24

Safe Hair Clipping Removal

Click to reveal

Modern safe methods:

  • Towel folded around hand
  • Paper neck strips
  • Vacuum systems

NOT safe: Traditional neck duster (unless disinfected after each use)

23/24

Blowdryer Safety

Click to reveal
  • Use protective agent
  • Keep moving
  • Avoid water — risk of electric shock
  • Check attachments for sharp points
24/24

Key Numbers to Remember

Click to reveal
  • 6½" – 7½" — Preferred shear size
  • 00000 — Closest blade cut
  • 4–6 months — Convex edge sharpening
  • 9–12 months — Beveled edge sharpening
  • 25–35% — Drop test for shear tension
  • 12" × 24" — Minimum disposable towel size
  • #3 — Preferred lather brush size
