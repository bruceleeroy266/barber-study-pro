## 1. Executive Summary

This blueprint defines the scope, structure, and educational intent for the Chapter 16 quiz. It maps every planned question to the approved curriculum, the supporting flashcards, and a cognitive level (Recall, Application, or Analysis).

**Recommended quiz length:** 30 questions  
**Target distribution:**
- Recall: 14 questions (46.7%)
- Application: 12 questions (40.0%)
- Analysis: 4 questions (13.3%)

The distribution closely matches the Founder directive of 45% Recall / 40% Application / 15% Analysis, with a slight emphasis on Recall to reflect the terminology-heavy nature of haircutting structures.

The quiz tests understanding, not memorization. Every question is tied to one or more Board Essential or Professional Essential flashcards, ensuring that the flashcards remain the teaching foundation and the quiz becomes the evaluation layer.

**No quiz questions will be written in this phase.** This blueprint is the plan; question writing begins after Founder approval.

## 6. Educational Rationale

### 6.1 Assessment Philosophy

The flashcards teach discrete concepts. The quiz evaluates whether the student can:

1. **Recall** the definitions and safety rules (foundational knowledge).
2. **Apply** those definitions to client scenarios and service decisions.
3. **Analyze** how changing one variable (elevation, angle, technique) affects the finished result.

This progression mirrors the lesson structure: definitions first, then structure behavior, then real-world decision-making.

### 6.2 Why Definitions Are Tested Directly

Licensing exams reward precise vocabulary. A student who cannot define "zero elevation," "traveling guide," or "cowlick" will struggle with board questions even if they can perform the technique. Recall questions ensure the vocabulary is secure before higher-order questions are attempted.

### 6.3 Why Application Questions Dominate the Haircut Structures

The four foundational cuts are distinguished by how they are used, not just by how they are defined. Application questions force the student to map client goals (e.g., "strong bottom line," "stacked fullness," "preserved length") to the correct structure. This is the exact decision-making skill required in a professional service.

### 6.4 Why Analysis Is Limited to Four Questions

Analysis in haircutting is often visual or tactile. The four Analysis items were chosen because they can be expressed clearly in text:
- Q10: Cutting-line direction changes the finished shape.
- Q14: Finger angle changes the graduated silhouette.
- Q20: Violating perimeter preservation changes the long-layered result.
- Q24: Curly-hair shrinkage and inspiration photos require integrating multiple factors.

These items test cause-and-effect reasoning without requiring an image.

### 6.5 Why Section 1 Has No Questions

The welcome section establishes context and motivation. Its content ("haircutting is a design process") is tested more rigorously in Section 3 (Q3) and across the structure questions. No dedicated question is needed.

### 6.6 Why Section 11 Has Only Two Questions

Styling and finishing are important but represent a smaller portion of the approved flashcard deck (10 of 68 cards, or 14.7%). Two questions appropriately reflect that weight while ensuring thermal safety and client education are assessed.

### 7.2 Quiz Writing Guidelines

When Phase Q2 begins, each question should follow these rules:

1. **One concept per question.** Do not combine two unrelated ideas into a single item.
2. **Four answer options.** One correct answer, three plausible distractors.
3. **Distractors must be educational.** Wrong answers should reflect common misconceptions from the curriculum's "Common Mistakes" lists.
4. **Avoid "all of the above" and "none of the above."** These reduce discriminatory power.
5. **Use original ASCYN PRO language.** Do not copy verbatim textbook phrasing.
6. **Scenario questions must be realistic.** Use client goals, hair types, and mistakes that actually occur in the lesson scenarios.
7. **No image dependency.** Questions must work without textbook figures or illustrations.
8. **Match cognitive level.** A question labeled "Application" must require more than definition recall.

### 7.3 Suggested Question Wording Patterns

| Cognitive Level | Suggested Stems |
|---|---|
| Recall | "What is...?" / "Which term describes...?" / "What is the primary purpose of...?" |
| Application | "A client wants... Which structure/technique is most appropriate?" / "What is the best approach when...?" |
| Analysis | "What is the most likely result if...?" / "Which mistake would cause...?" / "Why does... produce...?" |

### 7.4 Integration Notes for Engineering

When the quiz is integrated, ensure:
- Each question stores its `cognitive_level` (recall / application / analysis).
- Each question links to the supporting `flashcard_ids` for adaptive review.
- The quiz respects the Chapter 16 quiz exclusion in `demo-data.ts` until Phase Q2 integration is complete.
- Quiz results feed into `student_progress` so Chapter 16 can reach 100% completion.
