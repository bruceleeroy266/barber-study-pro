## Learning Objective 1: Pass Your State Licensing Examinations

**Section:** Prepare for Licensure  
**Pages:** 707–713

### Big Idea

You must pass the state written and practical exams and receive your license before you can legally work as a barber.

### Common Mistake

Some students think they can apply for jobs before they are licensed, or that a one-year apprenticeship replaces the state exam.

### Why It Matters

Shops hire licensed barbers. Working without a license can lead to fines or damage your career before it starts.

### Quick Review Path

1. Read the Phase 1 lesson section **Prepare for Licensure** (pp. 707–713).
2. Study flashcards FC19-01, FC19-04, FC19-05, FC19-06, FC19-07, FC19-08, FC19-11, FC19-12, FC19-13, FC19-14, FC19-18, FC19-22, FC19-23, FC19-25.
3. Practice a full written and practical exam review checklist with a peer.

### Instructor Tip

Build a study calendar early. Treat licensure as a legal requirement, not just a formality, and balance book study with hands-on practice.

## Learning Objective 2: Build Your Résumé and Portfolio

**Section:** Prepare for Employment  
**Pages:** 714–720

### Big Idea

Start your job search before graduation. Know your strengths and gaps, then show them with a clear résumé and a strong portfolio.

### Common Mistake

Waiting until after graduation, listing every duty on a résumé, hiding non-barber jobs, or bringing a weak portfolio.

### Why It Matters

Employers choose confident, prepared applicants. A scattered job search can force you to take the first offer instead of the right shop.

### Quick Review Path

1. Read the Phase 1 lesson section **Prepare for Employment** (pp. 714–720).
2. Study flashcards FC19-26 through FC19-39.
3. Draft a one-page résumé and collect three portfolio samples.

### Instructor Tip

Complete the Inventory of Personal Characteristics and Technical Skills in class. Then trade résumés and portfolios with a partner to check for clear accomplishments and relevant evidence.

## Learning Objective 3: Explore the Job Market and Interview Professionally

**Section:** Arrange for a Job Interview  
**Pages:** 720–730

### Big Idea

Research shops, present yourself professionally, ask good questions, follow up, and know your legal rights during the hiring process.

### Common Mistake

Arriving late, dressing too casually, leaving the phone on, skipping the thank-you note, or answering illegal questions without knowing your rights.

### Why It Matters

A strong interview shows you are trustworthy and serious. Poor etiquette or signing a bad contract can cost you the job or limit your future work.

### Quick Review Path

1. Read the Phase 1 lesson section **Arrange for a Job Interview** (pp. 720–730).
2. Study flashcards FC19-40 through FC19-60.
3. Role-play an interview with a classmate and practice asking your own questions.

### Instructor Tip

Run mock interviews in class. Give feedback on body language, answers, and questions. Teach students to redirect illegal questions politely and to take contracts home for review.

### Q19-01 — Before You Can Be Hired

**Common mistake:** Thinking an apprenticeship, a large online portfolio, or many applications can come before licensure.  
**Correct understanding:** You must pass the written and practical exams and receive your license before you can legally be hired.

### Q19-02 — What Does Test-Wise Mean?

**Common mistake:** Believing test-wise means memorizing every word, finishing first, or guessing by intuition.  
**Correct understanding:** Test-wise students read carefully, manage time, eliminate wrong answers, and use reasoning.  
**Review path:**
- Read test-wise study habits (p. 709).
- Study FC19-06.
- Practice three test-taking strategies on a sample exam.

---

### Q19-03 — Biggest Factor in Exam Performance

**Common mistake:** Thinking your outfit, the number of other test takers, or arrival time matters most.  
**Correct understanding:** Mastering the course content is the single most important factor in passing.  
**Review path:**
- Read written exam preparation (p. 709).
- Study FC19-07 and FC19-08.
- Make a study schedule that spreads review over several days.

---

### Q19-04 — Best First Step on Test Day

**Common mistake:** Starting with the hardest questions, asking the proctor to explain every item, or guessing quickly.  
**Correct understanding:** Read the directions, scan the entire test, and budget your time before answering.  
**Review path:**
- Read test-day strategies (p. 710).
- Study FC19-11 and FC19-18.
- Practice scanning a practice test and marking easy and hard items.

---

### Q19-05 — Identical Answer Choices

**Common mistake:** Thinking two identical choices both must be correct, or skipping the question as a trick.  
**Correct understanding:** Identical choices cannot both be the single best answer. Cross them out and pick from the rest.  
**Review path:**
- Read deductive reasoning (pp. 710–711).
- Study FC19-12 and FC19-13.
- Try five multiple-choice questions using elimination.

---

### Q19-06 — Absolute Words in True/False

**Common mistake:** Choosing true because words like "always," "never," "all," or "none" sound confident.  
**Correct understanding:** Absolutes are rarely accurate in real-world barbering. Treat them as warning flags.  
**Review path:**
- Read true/false test tips (p. 711).
- Study FC19-14.
- Practice spotting absolutes in ten statements.

---

### Q19-07 — Practical Exam Skills

**Common mistake:** Thinking practical exams test business, marketing, or law skills.  
**Correct understanding:** Practical exams test haircutting, shaving, shampooing, infection control, and related hands-on skills.  
**Review path:**
- Read practical examinations (p. 713).
- Study FC19-22, FC19-23, FC19-25.
- Practice each practical skill on a mannequin while timing yourself.

---

### Q19-08 — Purpose of the Self-Inventory

**Common mistake:** Thinking the inventory is a state board requirement, a salary guide, or a portfolio replacement.  
**Correct understanding:** The inventory shows your strengths and the skills you still need to practice.  
**Review path:**
- Read the self-inventory topic (p. 714).
- Study FC19-26 and FC19-28.
- Complete your own inventory and pick one skill to improve this week.

---

### Q19-09 — What Is a Résumé?

**Common mistake:** Confusing a résumé with a bound portfolio, a contract, or a complete list of every job.  
**Correct understanding:** A résumé is a short summary of your education, skills, and relevant work experience.  
**Review path:**
- Read the résumé topic (p. 716).
- Study FC19-31 and FC19-35.
- List your education, skills, and two work experiences for a draft résumé.

---

### Q19-10 — What to Emphasize on a Résumé

**Common mistake:** Listing salary history, every duty, or using fancy fonts and colors.  
**Correct understanding:** Emphasize accomplishments, especially with numbers or percentages.  
**Review path:**
- Read résumé content (pp. 716–717).
- Study FC19-32, FC19-33, FC19-36.
- Rewrite one past job duty as a numbered accomplishment.

---

### Q19-11 — Framing Past Jobs

**Common mistake:** Hiding non-barber jobs because they do not seem related.  
**Correct understanding:** Customer service, communication, and teamwork from other jobs strengthen a barbering résumé.  
**Review path:**
- Read transferable skills (p. 716).
- Study FC19-34.
- List three skills from a past job that would help in a barbershop.

---

### Q19-12 — Strong Portfolio Items

**Common mistake:** Including personal items like favorite shows, menus, or passwords.  
**Correct understanding:** A portfolio should include before-and-after photos, awards, certificates, reference letters, your résumé, and a career statement.  
**Review path:**
- Read employment portfolios (pp. 719–720).
- Study FC19-37, FC19-38, FC19-39.
- Collect or take three before-and-after photos.

---

### Q19-13 — After Researching a Shop

**Common mistake:** Posting a public review, waiting a month, or asking to skip the interview.  
**Correct understanding:** Send a résumé and tailored cover letter that mentions your visit and why you want the job.  
**Review path:**
- Read employer research and networking (pp. 720–722).
- Study FC19-41, FC19-42, FC19-43.
- Write a sample cover letter for a shop you have researched.

---

### Q19-14 — Interview Etiquette and Image

**Common mistake:** Chewing gum, keeping the phone on, dressing casually, bringing food, or criticizing a former employer.  
**Correct understanding:** Arrive early, dress appropriately, turn off your phone, and send a thank-you note afterward.  
**Review path:**
- Read interview wardrobe, etiquette, and follow-up (pp. 724, 726, 727).
- Study FC19-45, FC19-51, FC19-52, FC19-54.
- Do a mock interview and ask for feedback on appearance and manners.

---

### Q19-15 — Illegal Interview Questions

**Common mistake:** Thinking age is never relevant, that barbers cannot know their age, or that the question can wait until after hire.  
**Correct understanding:** Questions about age, medical history, citizenship, and similar topics are usually improper because they can support discrimination. Respond politely.  
**Review path:**
- Read legal aspects of interviews and contracts (pp. 728–730).
- Study FC19-56, FC19-57, FC19-60.
- Practice two polite responses to illegal questions.
