# ASCYN PRO Flashcards Study Guide

## Milady Standard Barbering — Chapter 19: Preparing for Licensure and Employment

**Total flashcards:** 60

Use this guide to review the chapter flashcards by section. Cover the back of each card and try to recall the answer before checking.

## Introduction & Career Mindset

**1. Front:** What is the first step toward employment success as a barber?

*Back:* Passing your state board exam and becoming licensed. (p. 707)

**2. Front:** What personal qualities help top barbers achieve success?

*Back:* Self-motivation, energy, persistence, planning, and self-confidence. (p. 707)

**3. Front:** Why should you research the local job market before accepting your first barbering position?

*Back:* It helps you choose a shop that fits your goals and avoids unnecessary career delays. (p. 707)

**4. Front:** When should you begin preparing for licensure and employment?

*Back:* Before graduation, so you are ready for opportunities as they arise. (p. 708)

---

## Licensure & Exam Preparation

**5. Front:** What two exams are usually required for barber licensure?

*Back:* A written exam and a practical exam. (p. 708)

**6. Front:** What does it mean to be test-wise?

*Back:* Understanding strategies for successful test taking. (p. 709)

**7. Front:** What is the most important factor in performing well on a licensing exam?

*Back:* Mastery of course content. (p. 709)

**8. Front:** What good study habits help prepare for the written licensing exam?

*Back:* Planned study schedule, organized notes, vocabulary list, reviewing past quizzes, and active listening. (p. 709)

**9. Front:** What holistic steps help you prepare mentally and physically for test day?

*Back:* Get rest, dress comfortably and professionally, avoid cramming, and stay positive. (pp. 709-710)

**10. Front:** What should you do if you need testing accommodations for a documented disability?

*Back:* Ask your instructor and contact your state licensing board well in advance. (p. 709)

**11. Front:** What should you do first when you receive the written exam?

*Back:* Read all directions, scan the entire test, and budget your time before answering. (p. 710)

---

## Test-Taking Strategies

**12. Front:** What is deductive reasoning?

*Back:* Reaching logical conclusions by eliminating incorrect options and identifying clues. (p. 710)

**13. Front:** What is the stem of a test question?

*Back:* The basic question or problem that may provide clues to the correct answer. (p. 711)

**14. Front:** In true/false questions, why are statements with absolutes usually false?

*Back:* Words like all, none, always, and never are rarely accurate. (p. 711)

**15. Front:** What should you watch for when answering multiple-choice questions?

*Back:* Qualifying words like not, except, and but; eliminate clearly wrong answers. (p. 711)

**16. Front:** What is a good strategy for matching questions?

*Back:* Read all items in each list before beginning and check off choices as you eliminate them. (p. 712)

**17. Front:** How should you approach an essay test question?

*Back:* Outline first, organize around cue words, and keep the answer complete and relevant. (p. 712)

**18. Front:** Why should you answer easy questions first on a timed exam?

*Back:* It saves time for harder questions and builds confidence. (p. 710)

**19. Front:** What should you do with questions you skip during a written exam?

*Back:* Mark or note them so you can return and answer them later if allowed. (p. 710)

## Barber Law & Practical Exams

**20. Front:** What is the main purpose of state barber boards?

*Back:* To protect the health, safety, and welfare of the public. (p. 712)

**21. Front:** Why must you study your own state's barber laws for the written exam?

*Back:* Rules about board members, fees, renewal, and prohibited acts vary by state. (p. 712)

**22. Front:** What basic technical skills are usually evaluated on the practical barber exam?

*Back:* Haircutting, shaving, shampooing, infection control, and sometimes blowdrying or chemical services. (p. 713)

**23. Front:** What are practical exams?

*Back:* Hands-on testing on a live model or mannequin. (p. 713)

**24. Front:** Why should you practice on the model you will use for the practical exam?

*Back:* It helps you learn the hair texture and haircut, reducing nervousness on exam day. (p. 713)

**25. Front:** What should you do with all equipment before the practical exam?

*Back:* Make sure it is clean, in good working order, and that you have everything required. (p. 713)

## Employment Readiness

**26. Front:** What is the purpose of the Inventory of Personal Characteristics and Technical Skills?

*Back:* It helps you identify strengths and areas to improve before seeking employment. (p. 714)

**27. Front:** What is a strong work ethic in barbering?

*Back:* Taking pride in your work and consistently doing a good job for clients, employer, and team. (p. 716)

**28. Front:** What personal characteristics help you get and keep a barbering job?

*Back:* Motivation, integrity, strong work ethic, enthusiasm, and good technical and communication skills. (pp. 715-716)

**29. Front:** What are some employment options for licensed barbers besides traditional barbershops?

*Back:* Beauty or unisex salons, hotels, resorts, spas, and booth rental. (p. 716)

**30. Front:** Why is networking with guest speakers and using school placement assistance valuable?

*Back:* They can provide job leads, industry tips, and help you find the right barbershop. (pp. 714-715)

## Résumé & Portfolio

**31. Front:** What is a résumé?

*Back:* A written summary of your education and work experience. (p. 716)

**32. Front:** How much time does an employer typically spend scanning a résumé?

*Back:* About 20 seconds. (p. 716)

**33. Front:** What should you emphasize on a résumé instead of just listing duties?

*Back:* Accomplishments, using numbers or percentages when possible. (p. 716)

**34. Front:** What are transferable skills?

*Back:* Skills mastered at other jobs that can be used in a new position. (p. 716)

**35. Front:** What key information should be included on a résumé?

*Back:* Contact information, education, skills, accomplishments, references, and a cover letter. (pp. 716-717)

**36. Front:** What should you avoid including on a résumé?

*Back:* Salary history, reasons for leaving past jobs, and untrue statements. (p. 717)

**37. Front:** What is an employment portfolio?

*Back:* A collection of photos and documents that reflect your skills, accomplishments, and abilities. (p. 719)

**38. Front:** What items belong in a strong employment portfolio?

*Back:* Diplomas, awards, résumé, reference letters, certificates, before-and-after photos, and a career statement. (p. 719)

**39. Front:** Why should you bring a printed portfolio even if you have an online version?

*Back:* Employers may not take extra time to visit a website or view digital files. (p. 720)

## Job Search & Networking

**40. Front:** When should you begin your barbering job search?

*Back:* Before graduation, so you can compare options instead of taking the first offer. (p. 720)

**41. Front:** What is an effective way to research potential barbershops?

*Back:* Visit shops, observe operations, talk to owners and barbers, and use online resources. (pp. 720-721)

**42. Front:** What should you do after visiting a barbershop, even if you do not want to work there?

*Back:* Send a thank-you note to maintain a positive professional network. (p. 722)

**43. Front:** What should a cover letter include?

*Back:* A brief introduction, the position you seek, and a request to discuss your qualifications. (p. 722)

**44. Front:** When should you follow up after submitting a résumé?

*Back:* About one week later. (p. 723)

## Interview Preparation

**45. Front:** What three things should you bring to a job interview?

*Back:* A résumé, a list of facts and references, and your employment portfolio. (p. 724)

**46. Front:** Why should you have two interview outfits ready?

*Back:* You may be invited back for a second interview. (p. 724)

**47. Front:** What should you do before an interview to ensure you arrive on time?

*Back:* Visit the location beforehand, plan your route, and arrive early. (pp. 724, 726)

**48. Front:** What is one purpose of role-playing common interview questions?

*Back:* It helps you organize your thoughts and answer confidently during the interview. (p. 724)

**49. Front:** What should you do if a barbershop asks you to perform a service during the interview?

*Back:* Confirm requirements, bring your own model if needed, and bring tools and supplies. (p. 725)

**50. Front:** What types of questions should you be prepared to ask the interviewer?

*Back:* Questions about training, benefits, advancement, compensation, shop culture, and expectations. (p. 727)

---

## Interview Etiquette & Follow-Up

**51. Front:** What should you do with your cell phone during an interview?

*Back:* Turn it off and avoid earbuds or hands-free devices. (p. 726)

**52. Front:** Why is it important to send a thank-you note after an interview?

*Back:* It shows appreciation and keeps you memorable to the interviewer. (p. 727)

**53. Front:** What is a positive way to close a job interview?

*Back:* Express genuine interest in the position and thank the interviewer for their time. (p. 728)

**54. Front:** What should you avoid doing during an interview?

*Back:* Chewing gum, smoking, bringing food or drinks, and criticizing former employers. (p. 726)

**55. Front:** How long should your answers to interview questions generally be?

*Back:* About two minutes or less, speaking clearly and honestly. (p. 726)

## Legal & Professional Conduct

**56. Front:** Which topics are off-limits for illegal interview questions?

*Back:* Age, medical history, U.S. citizenship, native language, marital status, or children. (p. 728)

**57. Front:** What is a legal way for an employer to ask about your ability to work in the United States?

*Back:* "Are you authorized to work in the United States?" (p. 728)

**58. Front:** What is a noncompete agreement?

*Back:* A contract that may limit where and when you can work for a competitor after leaving a job. (p. 729)

**59. Front:** What should you do before signing an employment contract?

*Back:* Take it home, read it carefully, and consult a labor-law attorney if needed. (p. 729)

**60. Front:** Why should you complete an employment application even if you submitted a résumé?

*Back:* Employers require it, and it must be filled out accurately using your prepared facts. (pp. 729-730)
