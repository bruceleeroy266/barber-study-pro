Treatment of the Hair and Scalp

Master scalp health, therapeutic treatments, and client consultation skills

Why Treat Hair and Scalp Conditions?

Client Health & Comfort

Healthy hair begins at the scalp. When clients experience itching, flaking, or irritation, it affects their daily comfort and confidence. As barbers, we provide relief and improve quality of life through proper treatment.

Hair Appearance & Manageability

Treated hair looks better, feels softer, and styles more easily. Scalp treatments remove buildup that weighs hair down, allowing natural movement and shine to come through.

Professional Revenue

Treatment services represent additional income streams. Clients who understand scalp health become repeat customers for maintenance treatments and retail product purchases.

Early Problem Detection

Regular scalp examination helps identify conditions before they worsen. Barbers often spot issues clients haven't noticed, enabling early intervention and referrals when needed.

Types of Scalp Treatments

Click each treatment type to learn more

Moisturizing Treatments

For dry, tight, or flaky scalps

Purpose: Restore hydration balance to parched scalp tissue and relieve tightness or itching caused by dryness.

Key Ingredients: Aloe vera, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, natural oils (jojoba, coconut, argan), shea butter.

Application: Apply to clean scalp, massage gently, leave for 10-15 minutes under warm towel or steamer. Rinse thoroughly.

Clarifying Treatments

For product buildup and oily scalps

Purpose: Remove accumulated product residue, excess oil, hard water minerals, and environmental pollutants from the scalp.

Key Ingredients: Apple cider vinegar, salicylic acid, tea tree oil, citrus extracts, activated charcoal.

Application: Use before other treatments to ensure maximum penetration. Massage into scalp, leave 3-5 minutes, rinse with warm water. Follow with conditioner on hair only.

Stimulating Treatments

For thinning hair and circulation improvement

Purpose: Increase blood flow to hair follicles, encouraging healthy growth and awakening dormant follicles.

Key Ingredients: Peppermint oil, rosemary extract, biotin, caffeine, niacin (vitamin B3), eucalyptus.

Application: Apply to scalp using fingertips or applicator brush. Massage vigorously for 5-10 minutes to generate heat and tingling sensation. Do not rinse immediately.

Dandruff Control Treatments

For flaking, itching, and microbial imbalance

Purpose: Control Malassezia yeast overgrowth, reduce inflammation, and normalize skin cell turnover rate.

Key Ingredients: Pyrithione zinc, ketoconazole, selenium sulfide, coal tar, salicylic acid, sulfur.

Application: Apply to affected areas, massage gently, leave for 3-5 minutes (or as directed). Use 2-3 times weekly until controlled, then weekly for maintenance.

Types of Hair Treatments

Deep Conditioning

Intensive moisture treatment that penetrates the hair shaft to repair damage, improve elasticity, and restore softness. Ideal for dry, brittle, or chemically treated hair.

Protein Reconstruction

Strengthens hair by depositing hydrolyzed proteins that fill gaps in the cuticle layer. Essential for severely damaged, over-processed, or breaking hair. Use sparingly to avoid protein overload.

Hot Oil Treatments

Warm oil penetrates the cuticle to lubricate, seal in moisture, and add shine. Excellent for natural hair, protective styles, and preventing breakage. Oils include coconut, olive, and argan.

Bond Builders

Advanced treatments that repair disulfide bonds broken during chemical services. Can be added to color or lightening formulas or used as standalone treatments to prevent and reverse damage.

Keratin Smoothing

Semi-permanent treatment that coats the hair with keratin protein to reduce frizz, increase manageability, and create a smooth, shiny finish. Results last 2-4 months with proper care.

Color-Protecting Treatments

Specially formulated to seal the cuticle after color services, lock in pigment molecules, and prevent fading. Often contain UV filters and antioxidants to maintain vibrancy.

Treatment Procedures & Techniques

1

Consultation & Analysis

Begin with thorough hair and scalp analysis. Ask about allergies, sensitivities, previous chemical services, and current home care routine. Document findings and explain recommended treatment to client.

2

Shampoo & Prep

Shampoo with appropriate cleanser for scalp condition. Use clarifying shampoo for buildup, moisturizing for dryness. Rinse thoroughly with warm water. Towel dry to remove excess moisture before treatment application.

3

Application

Section hair into 4-6 parts. Apply treatment product directly to scalp (for scalp treatments) or from roots to ends (for hair treatments). Use applicator brush or fingertips for even distribution. Ensure complete coverage.

4

Processing

Cover with plastic cap and warm towel, or place under hood dryer/steamer for 10-20 minutes depending on product instructions. Heat opens cuticle and enhances penetration. Monitor client for any adverse reactions.

5

Rinse & Style

Rinse thoroughly with cool to lukewarm water. Cool water seals the cuticle. Apply conditioner if needed (avoid scalp for oily conditions). Towel dry, detangle gently, and proceed with desired styling service.

Massage During Treatments

Benefits of Scalp Massage

  • Increases blood circulation to hair follicles
  • Distributes natural oils (sebum) along hair shaft
  • Enhances product penetration and effectiveness
  • Relieves tension and promotes relaxation
  • Exfoliates scalp by loosening dead skin cells
  • Creates memorable, luxurious service experience

Massage Techniques

Effleurage (Stroking)

Long, smooth, gliding movements to relax and spread product. Use palms and fingertips.

Petrissage (Kneading)

Circular pressure and lifting motions with fingertips. Stimulates circulation deeply.

Friction (Rubbing)

Small circular movements using fingertips or pads. Creates heat and stimulates follicles.

Tapotement (Tapping)

Light, rapid tapping with fingertips. Invigorates scalp and awakens nerve endings.

Pro Tip

Always use your fingertips, not nails. Work from hairline toward crown, then down to nape. Vary pressure based on client preference—some prefer gentle touch, others want firm stimulation. Ask for feedback!

Home Care Recommendations

Client Education is Key

Professional treatments deliver immediate results, but home maintenance determines long-term success. Teach clients how to care for their hair between visits.

Cleansing Routine

  • • Shampoo frequency based on scalp type
  • • Use lukewarm water, not hot
  • • Focus shampoo on scalp, not ends
  • • Rinse thoroughly to prevent buildup

Conditioning Habits

  • • Condition after every shampoo
  • • Apply from mid-lengths to ends
  • • Leave in 2-3 minutes minimum
  • • Use deep conditioner weekly

Protection Practices

  • • Use heat protectant before styling
  • • Wear hat in direct sun
  • • Rinse hair after swimming
  • • Sleep on silk or satin pillowcase

Maintenance Schedule

Daily

Gentle brushing to distribute oils, scalp massage (2-3 minutes)

Weekly

Deep conditioning treatment, clarifying shampoo (if needed)

Monthly

Protein treatment, trim split ends, scalp exfoliation

Quarterly

Professional salon treatment, hair analysis, product review

Retail Product Sales

The Consultation-Sale Connection

Every service is a sales opportunity. When you analyze a client's hair and scalp, you're identifying problems they need solutions for. The products you recommend are those solutions.

"Based on what I'm seeing with your dry scalp, I'd recommend our moisturizing shampoo with tea tree oil. It'll help with the flaking between visits."

Benefits of Retail Sales

  • Increased Revenue: Retail can add 15-30% to service income
  • Client Retention: Clients who buy products return more frequently
  • Professional Authority: Builds trust as an expert
  • Consistent Results: Clients maintain salon quality at home

Effective Selling Techniques

1. Educate, Don't Push

Explain why the product helps their specific concern. Knowledge builds confidence in the purchase.

2. Let Them Experience It

Use products during service. When clients feel and smell the difference, they want to take it home.

3. Make Specific Recommendations

Don't ask "Do you want shampoo?" Say "I'm using our volumizing line on you today—it's perfect for your fine hair."

4. Create Systems

Recommend complete systems (shampoo + conditioner + styling) rather than single items.

5. Follow Up

Ask about product use at next visit. Shows you care and opens door to additional recommendations.

6. Display Strategically

Keep retail area clean, organized, and well-lit. Rotate displays to feature seasonal or promotional items.

5 What is protein overload and how can it be avoided?

Protein overload occurs when hair receives too much protein, becoming stiff and brittle. Avoid by balancing protein treatments with moisturizing treatments and not overusing protein products.

6 List three benefits of scalp massage during treatments.

Increases blood circulation, distributes natural oils, enhances product penetration, relieves tension, exfoliates scalp, and creates a luxurious service experience.

7 What is the difference between a moisturizing treatment and a protein reconstruction?

Moisturizing treatments restore hydration and softness to dry hair. Protein reconstructions strengthen hair by filling gaps in the cuticle with hydrolyzed proteins.

8 Why is client consultation important before performing a treatment?

To identify allergies, sensitivities, previous chemical services, scalp conditions, and to determine the appropriate treatment for their specific needs.

9 What are bond builders and when should they be used?

Bond builders repair disulfide bonds broken during chemical services. Use during color/lightening or as standalone treatments for damaged hair.

10 How often should a client with dandruff use anti-dandruff treatment initially?

2-3 times per week until controlled, then weekly for maintenance.

11 What is the "touch test" during hair analysis?

Feeling the hair's texture, thickness, and elasticity to assess its condition and porosity.

12 Why is retail product sales beneficial for client retention?

13 What ingredients are commonly found in stimulating scalp treatments?

Peppermint oil, rosemary extract, biotin, caffeine, niacin (vitamin B3), and eucalyptus.

14 What is the proper way to apply shampoo during the prep phase?

Focus shampoo on the scalp where oil and buildup accumulate, not the ends. Rinse thoroughly with warm water.

Explaining why a product helps the client's specific concern rather than aggressively pushing for a sale. Knowledge builds confidence in the purchase.
