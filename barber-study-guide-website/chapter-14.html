Chapter 14

Men's Haircutting and Styling

Master the foundational techniques of men's haircutting including shear work, clipper cutting, razor techniques, and styling methods.

📚 Student-Created Study Notes: These notes summarize key concepts from classroom instruction and the Milady Barbering textbook. They are personal study materials created for educational purposes.

Introduction

Men's haircutting and styling is a foundational skill in barbering. Modern haircuts require a combination of techniques (shear-over-comb, clipper-over-comb, freehand, razor, etc.) to create balanced, customized designs. Barbers must master tool usage, hair growth directions, tapering, blending, and finishing to meet client expectations and build repeat business.

🎯 Key Point

Successful haircuts rely on understanding hair texture, growth patterns, head shape, and proper tool coordination for precision and safety.

Why Study Men's Haircutting and Styling?

  • Haircutting is the primary service in most barbershops
  • Mastery allows you to create a wide variety of styles and variations
  • Proper techniques ensure client satisfaction, safety, and professional growth

🎯 Key Point

Practice on mannequins first to build confidence before working on live clients.

Basic Cutting Techniques (LO1–LO7)

Shear-Over-Comb (Fingers-and-Shear) Cutting – Procedure 14-1

  • Horizontal cutting above fingers (top/crest sections): Palm shears, create part, comb hair forward, position comb at 45°, cut projecting hair at second knuckle
  • Vertical cutting above fingers (sides): Create vertical parts, comb hair outward, cut at 90° elevation
  • Below fingers at perimeter (nape, sides): Horizontal parts, comb downward, cut below fingers

Key motion: Roll the comb out (key-turning) to position hair for cutting.

Freehand Shear Cutting

  • May or may not use comb/fingers to control hair while cutting against the skin
  • Useful for fine-tuning, removing stray hairs, shaping nape, or trimming tight curl textures

Clipper Cutting Techniques

  • Freehand clipper cutting: Steady hand, use comb/pick to lift hair; remove excess from previous cuts; best for short to medium lengths
  • Clipper-over-comb: Comb lifts hair; clipper cuts ends protruding from comb; excellent for blending tapers

Guidelines: Start in center nape with blades open, tilt gradually, glide upward, roll out at ends.

Directions of Hair Growth and Tool Usage

Direction Description Usage
With the grain Cut in same direction as growth Safest, least irritation
Against the grain Opposite direction of growth Shorter result, more caution needed
Across the grain Transition areas Blending between sections

Razor Cutting

Tapers hair ends more smoothly than shears/clippers; good for blending, softening, texturizing.

Techniques:

  • Razor-over-comb: Similar to shear-over-comb technique
  • Razor rotation: Rotating motion with comb
  • Fingers-and-razor: Hold section between fingers

Taper-Blending Levels:

  • Light: Razor almost flat, minimal pressure
  • Heavier: Up to 45° tilt, more pressure
  • Terminal: Nearly 90°, least taper

⚠️ Safety

Use guard for beginners; keep hair damp; avoid over-tapering.

Hair Thinning and Texturizing

  • Thinning: Reduce bulk/weight (serrated shears mid-shaft)
  • Slicing/Carving: Remove bulk on surface or by elevating (45–90°)
  • Slithering: Thin parting held between shears, sliding motion toward scalp
  • Notching/Point cutting: Chunkier texture at ends

Remove weight from ends with clipper-over-comb, reverse rotation, or razor.

🎯 Key Point

Always observe hair density first. Remove bulk gradually; avoid cutting too close to scalp or part lines.

Basic Haircut Styles (LO8)

Precision Cut

Uniform taper/blend using fingers-and-shear; clean, controlled

Taper Cut

Graduated length increase from nape to top; conforms to head shape (basic taper, Ivy League, Princeton, etc.)

Flat Top / Crew Cut

Short sides/back, flat or slightly rounded top; level guide needed

Pompadour

Medium-to-long top with volume; tapered sides; can include fade variations

Fade / High & Tight

Extremely close sides/back blending to longer top; many variations (temple fade, shadow fade, etc.)

Caesar / Quo Vadis

Short, uniform layers; forward styling; quo vadis often uses clippers for even length on thicker hair

🎯 Key Point

Style names vary regionally; always confirm client expectations and head shape before cutting.

Haircut Finish Work (LO9)

After the haircut:

  • Neck/Outline Shave: Traditional neck shave with razor or outline with trimmers/shears
  • Eyebrow Trim: Shear-over-comb or outliner; protect eyes
  • Nostril & Ear Hair: T-blade or nose trimmer; gentle technique
  • Head Shave (if requested): Analyze scalp, prepare with hot towels/lather, use straight razor carefully

🎯 Key Point

Finish work gives a polished, professional look and completes the service.

Identify Styling Techniques (LO10)

Drying Methods

  • Natural Drying: Air-dry with fingers for texture
  • Finger Styling: Manipulate with fingers for textured look
  • Scrunch Styling: Squeeze curly/wavy hair for tousled effect

Blowdrying Techniques

  • Freeform: Quick, natural fall
  • Stylized: Definite direction with comb/brush + dryer
  • Diffused: Maintain natural wave on curly hair
  • Blow Waving / Air Waving: Create waves with dryer + comb/brush

Building Volume

Lift sections with brush while drying; use horizontal/vertical partings.

Braids and Locks

  • Braids/Cornrows: Close to scalp, various patterns
  • Locks (Dreadlocks): Natural coiling; maintenance includes palm rolling, twisting; can take months to mature

🎯 Key Point

Choose styling method based on hair texture, length, and client preference. Use appropriate products (gels, pomades, sprays) for hold without stiffness.

Safety Precautions for Haircutting and Styling (LO11)

  • Use tools safely and for their intended purpose
  • Keep razors closed when not in use; use guards for beginners
  • Maintain proper posture and chair control
  • Clean and disinfect all tools and work area after each client
  • Dispose of blades in sharps container; launder linens
  • Avoid startling clients; keep scalp protected from heat
  • Sweep and organize workspace

🎯 Key Point

Safety and sanitation are non-negotiable; they protect clients and maintain professional standards.

Overall Chapter Key Takeaways

1

Master multiple techniques (shear-over-comb, clipper-over-comb, freehand, razor) and understand when to use each

2

Always analyze hair texture, density, growth direction (with/against/across grain), and head shape

3

Create balanced designs by tapering, blending, and removing bulk appropriately

4

Finish every haircut with outline work, trims, and styling for a complete professional service

5

Practice safety and sanitation daily

  • A) Slide cutting
  • B) Roll the comb out (key-turning)
  • C) Scissor over fingers
  • D) Clipper-over-comb

2. Which cutting direction is safest and causes least irritation?

  • A) Against the grain
  • B) Across the grain
  • C) With the grain
  • D) Against growth patterns

3. What is the primary purpose of finish work in a haircut?

  • A) To speed up the service
  • B) To give a polished, professional look
  • C) To use more products
  • D) To charge more money

4. Which technique is best for maintaining natural wave on curly hair?

  • A) Blow waving
  • B) Diffused drying
  • C) Finger styling
  • D) Freeform drying

  • A) Client's face shape
  • B) Hair density
  • C) Current fashion trends
  • D) Time of day
