Chapter 17: Hair Replacement

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

Hair Replacement

Methods and systems used to add hair or cover areas of hair loss on the scalp.

Hair System

A non-surgical hair replacement option consisting of a base material with attached hair.

Toupee

A small hairpiece designed to cover a specific area of hair loss on the top of the head.

Wig

A full head covering made of human or synthetic hair worn to conceal baldness.

Lace Front

A hair system with a fine lace material at the front hairline for a natural appearance.

Monofilament Base

A thin, breathable mesh material used as the foundation for hair systems.

Polyurethane Base

A thin, skin-like material that creates an invisible base for hair attachment.

Human Hair

Natural hair used in systems that can be styled with heat and colored like natural hair.

Synthetic Hair

Man-made fiber used in hair systems; holds style well but cannot withstand high heat.

Hair Integration

A system that blends with the client's existing hair for a natural look.

Adhesive

A bonding agent used to attach hair systems to the scalp or skin.

Tape Tabs

Double-sided adhesive strips used to secure hair systems to the scalp.

Clip Attachment

Small clips sewn into hair systems that attach to existing hair for temporary wear.

Hair Weaving

A technique of adding hair by braiding or weaving extensions into existing hair.

Hair Extensions

Additional hair strands attached to existing hair to add length or volume.

Scalp Micropigmentation

A cosmetic tattoo procedure that creates the appearance of hair follicles on the scalp.

Density

The amount of hair per square inch on a hair system; affects the natural appearance.

Hairline

The front edge of a hair system that frames the face; crucial for a natural look.

Ventilating

The process of knotting individual hairs into the base material of a hair system.

Maintenance

Regular care and servicing of hair systems including cleaning, reattachment, and styling.
