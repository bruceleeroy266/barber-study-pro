CHAPTER 7

Basics of Chemistry

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

1/18

Chemistry

Click to reveal

The science that deals with the composition, structure, and properties of matter. Essential for understanding hair products.

2/18

Matter

Click to reveal

Anything that occupies space and has mass. Can be solid, liquid, or gas. All physical substances are matter.

3/18

Element

Click to reveal

The simplest form of matter that cannot be broken down into simpler substances. Examples: carbon, oxygen, hydrogen.

4/18

Compound

Click to reveal

A substance formed when two or more elements are chemically combined. Has properties different from its component elements.

5/18

Mixture

Click to reveal

A combination of two or more substances that are not chemically combined. Can be separated by physical means.

6/18

Solution

Click to reveal

A uniform mixture of two or more substances. The solute dissolves in the solvent. Example: salt water.

7/18

Suspension

Click to reveal

A mixture in which particles are dispersed throughout but are not dissolved. Will settle over time.

8/18

pH Scale

Click to reveal

Measures acidity or alkalinity from 0-14. 7 is neutral. Below 7 is acidic, above 7 is alkaline. Hair is 4.5-5.5.

9/18

Acid

Click to reveal

A substance with pH below 7. Contracts and tightens the hair cuticle. Makes hair stronger and shinier.

10/18

Alkaline (Base)

Click to reveal

A substance with pH above 7. Swells and raises the hair cuticle. Used in chemical services like relaxers.

11/18

Oxidation

Click to reveal

A chemical reaction that involves the addition of oxygen or the loss of hydrogen. Occurs in haircolor processing.

12/18

Reduction

Click to reveal

A chemical reaction that involves the addition of hydrogen or the loss of oxygen. Opposite of oxidation.

13/18

Exothermic Reaction

Click to reveal

A chemical reaction that releases heat. Some haircolor and perm solutions produce exothermic reactions.

14/18

Endothermic Reaction

Click to reveal

A chemical reaction that absorbs heat from its surroundings. Requires external heat to proceed.

15/18

Catalyst

Click to reveal

A substance that speeds up a chemical reaction without being consumed in the process. Heat can act as a catalyst.

16/18

Surfactant

Click to reveal

A substance that reduces surface tension between oil and water. Allows shampoos to cleanse by mixing oil with water.

17/18

Emulsion

Click to reveal

A mixture of two or more immiscible substances held together by an emulsifier. Example: mayonnaise, conditioners.

18/18

Volatile

Click to reveal

Describes a substance that evaporates quickly at room temperature. Many hair products contain volatile solvents.
