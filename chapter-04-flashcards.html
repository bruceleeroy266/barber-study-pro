CHAPTER 4

Infection Control

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

1/20

Microorganism

Click to reveal

Any organism of microscopic or submicroscopic size. Includes bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites that can cause infection.

2/20

Pathogen

Click to reveal

A disease-causing microorganism. Can be bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites that invade the body and cause illness.

3/20

Bacteria

Click to reveal

One-celled microorganisms that can be beneficial or harmful. Some cause disease (pathogenic) while others are harmless.

4/20

Virus

Click to reveal

A submicroscopic infectious particle that replicates only inside living cells. Smaller than bacteria and harder to kill.

5/20

Fungi

Click to reveal

Simple organisms including molds and yeasts. Can cause infections like ringworm and athlete's foot.

6/20

Parasites

Click to reveal

Organisms that live on or in a host and derive nutrients at the host's expense. Includes lice and mites.

7/20

Direct Transmission

Click to reveal

Transfer of pathogens through direct contact like touching, kissing, or contact with open wounds or bodily fluids.

8/20

Indirect Transmission

Click to reveal

Transfer of pathogens through contaminated objects, airborne particles, vectors, or contaminated food and water.

9/20

Cross-Contamination

Click to reveal

The spread of bacteria or other microorganisms from one surface to another, or from person to person.

10/20

Contamination

Click to reveal

The presence of blood or other potentially infectious materials on an item or surface.

11/20

Sanitation

Click to reveal

Cleaning to remove visible dirt and debris. Lowers bacterial count but does not kill all microorganisms.

12/20

Disinfection

Click to reveal

Kills most microorganisms on non-porous surfaces. Required for tools and surfaces in barbering. Does not kill spores.

13/20

Sterilization

Click to reveal

Destroys all microbial life including bacterial spores. Required for instruments that penetrate skin.

14/20

EPA-Registered Disinfectant

Click to reveal

Disinfectants approved by the Environmental Protection Agency that are proven effective against specific pathogens.

15/20

Bloodborne Pathogens

Click to reveal

Disease-causing microorganisms spread through contact with blood or bodily fluids. Includes HIV, HBV, and HCV.

16/20

Universal Precautions

Click to reveal

Treating all blood and bodily fluids as if they are infectious. Standard safety approach to prevent disease transmission.

17/20

Autoclave

Click to reveal

Device that uses steam under pressure to sterilize tools and equipment. Most effective sterilization method.

18/20

Ultrasonic Cleaner

Click to reveal

Device that uses high-frequency sound waves to clean debris from tools before disinfection or sterilization.

19/20

Biohazard

Click to reveal

Any biological material that poses a threat to human health. Includes blood, bodily fluids, and contaminated items.

20/20

OSHA

Click to reveal

Occupational Safety and Health Administration. Sets standards for workplace safety including bloodborne pathogen exposure.
