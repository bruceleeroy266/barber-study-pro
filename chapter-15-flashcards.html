Chapter 15: Men's Haircutting

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

Haircutting

The art of shaping and styling hair using various cutting techniques and tools.

Shears

Professional haircutting scissors used to cut hair with precision and control.

Clippers

Electric cutting tools used for creating short haircuts, fades, and precise outlines.

Trimmers

Smaller electric tools used for detailing, edging, and creating clean lines.

Guard

Attachments for clippers that determine the length of hair being cut.

Blade

The cutting component of clippers that moves back and forth to cut hair.

Stationary Guide

A section of hair used as a reference point that does not move during cutting.

Traveling Guide

A section of hair that moves with each cut to create graduated or layered effects.

Overdirection

Combing hair away from its natural fall position to create weight or graduation.

Elevation

The angle at which hair is held from the head while cutting; affects the amount of weight removed.

Zero Elevation

Cutting hair at the scalp level with no lift; creates a blunt, solid line.

Low Elevation

Holding hair at less than 45 degrees from the head; maintains more weight in the cut.

High Elevation

Holding hair at 90 degrees or higher; creates more layers and removes more weight.

Cross-Checking

Checking the haircut from different angles to ensure evenness and balance.

Texturizing

Techniques used to remove bulk and create movement without shortening the overall length.

Thinning Shears

Shears with teeth on one blade used to remove bulk and blend sections.

Razor Cutting

Using a razor to create soft, textured edges and remove weight from the hair.

Clipper Over Comb

A technique using clippers against a comb to create precise, graduated lengths.

Scissor Over Comb

A technique using shears against a comb for more control in blending and tapering.

Freehand Cutting

Cutting without a guide or comb, used for creating texture and artistic effects.
