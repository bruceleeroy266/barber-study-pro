# CHAPTER 11 COMPLETE MATERIAL SUMMARY
**Treatment of the Hair and Scalp**  
**Date:** May 16, 2026

## MATERIAL COVERAGE: 9 PAGES (276-285)

### Complete Content:

**1. Product Matching (Table 11-1, Page 277)**
- Fine hair: Volumizing shampoo, detangling conditioner, protein treatments
- Medium hair: pH-balanced products
- Coarse hair: Moisturizing products, leave-in conditioners
- Wavy/curly: Light leave-in, protein treatments
- Dry/damaged: Gentle cleansing, deep moisturizing, protein/moisturizing repair

**2. Draping Procedures (Pages 277-278)**
- Two drape types: Shampoo capes (waterproof), Haircutting capes (nylon/synthetic)
- Draping methods: Wet service, chemical service, haircut service, mustache/beard trim
- Safety: Prevent liquids/chemicals on skin/clothing, state law compliance, infection control

**3. Shampoo Service (Pages 279-280)**
- Reclined method (most common, hydraulic chair)
- Inclined method (client forward over bowl)
- Special needs clients (wheelchair-bound, disabled)
- Barber physical presentation: posture, balance, parallel feet

**4. Superior Shampoo Service (Page 280)**
- Individual client attention
- Proper shampooing technique
- Correct water temperature
- Proper scalp massage
- Common service faults: improper selection, insufficient massage, extreme water temp, face wetting, scraping scalp, improper blotting

**5. Preparation & Setup (Page 280)**
- Consultation first
- Scalp/hair analysis (condition, texture, density, porosity, elasticity, abrasions/disorders)
- Proper water temperature testing

**6. Scalp/Hair Treatments Purpose (Page 283)**
- Preserve health and appearance
- Combat disorders: dandruff, dry hair/scalp, oily hair/scalp
- Cleanliness/stimulation essential
- Common issues: dry scalp (oil gland inactivity), poor circulation, oily scalp (overactive sebaceous glands), dandruff (malassezia fungus), alopecia, corrective hair treatments (dry/damaged hair)

**7. Scalp Massage (Table 11-2, Page 283)**
- Guidelines: firm pressure upward, rotary movements loosen tissues, slow/rhythmic, avoid pulling, hands under hair
- Sections: Sides to top (sliding, auricularis superior/temporalis, 5th/7th cranial nerves)
- Behind ears to crown (rotary/sliding, auricularis posterior/occipitalis, 11th cranial/spinal)
- Forehead to crown (sliding/rotary, frontalis/aponeurosis/temporalis, 5th/7th cranial)
- Front hairline (rotary, frontalis, 5th cranial)

**8. Scalp Massage Manipulations (Page 282)**
- Start at hairline, even pressure, continuous synchronized movements
- Effects: increased blood/lymph flow, soothed nerves, stimulated muscles/glands, flexible scalp
- Three types: Rotary (thumbs/fingertips overlapping circular), Sliding (thumbs/fingertips gliding strokes), Back & forth (thumbs/fingertips brisk back/forth moderate to firm)

**9. Scalp/Hair Treatment Details (Page 284)**
- Cleansing with suitable shampoo
- Steaming scalp
- Massage with hands or electrical appliance
- Infrared/ultraviolet lamps or high-frequency current
- Cosmetic antiseptics/ointments application

**10. Scalp Steam (Page 284)**
- Effective for preparing scalp for massage/treatments
- Relaxes pores, softens scalp/hair, increases blood circulation
- Use: Fill container with water, fit hood over client's head, turn on current
- Hood models have side openings for scalp massage during steam

**11. Hot Towel Usage (Page 285)**
- Can substitute for scalp steamer
- Prepare in hot-towel cabinet or hot water
- One towel applied or series of applications

**12. Scalp Treatment Series (Page 285)**
- Should be series, once a week for several weeks
- More frequent under dermatologist direction
- Review procedures for steam/scalp massage

**13. Electric Massager Treatment (Page 285)**
- Vibrator/hand massager - electrical device
- Stimulating scalp massage, same movements as hand massage
- Before use: adjust on back of hand (thumb/fingers free)
- Regulate intensity/duration, avoid excessive pressure
- Most effective in series for general maintenance, promote growth, stimulate condition

**14. Hair Tonic Treatments (Page 285)**
- Scalp steamers, steam towels, vibrators, manipulations with hair tonics
- Steps: Apply tonic, massage scalp, apply steam, massage with hands/vibrator, comb into desired style
- CAUTION: Barbers prohibited from treating scalp disorders caused by parasitic infestations/staphylococcus infections - refer to physician
