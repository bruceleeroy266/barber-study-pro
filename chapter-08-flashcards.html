CHAPTER 8

Basics of Electricity

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

1/18

Electricity

Click to reveal

A form of energy that produces light, heat, and magnetic and chemical changes. Powers barbering equipment.

2/18

Electric Current

Click to reveal

The flow of electricity along a conductor. Measured in amperes (amps). Must complete a circuit to flow.

3/18

Conductor

Click to reveal

A material that allows electricity to flow through it easily. Examples: copper, aluminum, water.

4/18

Insulator

Click to reveal

A material that does not allow electricity to flow through it easily. Examples: rubber, plastic, glass.

5/18

Circuit

Click to reveal

The path of negative and positive electric currents moving from the generating source through conductors.

6/18

Closed Circuit

Click to reveal

A complete circuit where electricity can flow. Required for electrical devices to operate.

7/18

Open Circuit

Click to reveal

An incomplete circuit where electricity cannot flow. Turning a switch off creates an open circuit.

8/18

Short Circuit

Click to reveal

Occurs when electricity flows through an unintended path, bypassing the normal circuit. Can cause fires.

9/18

Voltage

Click to reveal

The pressure or force that pushes electric current through a conductor. Measured in volts (V).

10/18

Amperage

Click to reveal

The strength of an electric current. Measured in amperes or amps (A). Higher amps = stronger current.

11/18

Resistance

Click to reveal

The opposition to the flow of electric current. Measured in ohms. Causes some energy to convert to heat.

12/18

Ohm

Click to reveal

The unit of electrical resistance. Named after Georg Ohm who discovered the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance.

13/18

Direct Current (DC)

Click to reveal

Electric current that flows in one direction only. Used in battery-operated devices.

14/18

Alternating Current (AC)

Click to reveal

Electric current that rapidly reverses direction. Used in homes and businesses. Standard in the US is 60 Hz.

15/18

Grounding

Click to reveal

Connecting electrical equipment to the earth to safely dissipate electrical current. Essential for safety.

16/18

Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI)

Click to reveal

A safety device that shuts off electricity when it detects current flowing through an unintended path. Required near water sources.

17/18

Overload

Click to reveal

When too many appliances are connected to a single circuit, drawing more current than the circuit can safely handle.

18/18

Circuit Breaker

Click to reveal

A safety device that automatically stops the flow of electricity when a circuit is overloaded. Can be reset.
