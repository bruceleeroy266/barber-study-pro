🎯 Why Study Anatomy & Physiology?

  • Perform services safely and effectively
  • Recognize normal versus abnormal conditions
  • Avoid causing injury or discomfort to clients
  • Make informed decisions about when to refer a client to a physician
Key Point: Anatomy tells you "what" the body is made of; physiology tells you "how" it works. Together, they form the foundation for safe, professional barbering.

🔬 Cells – Basic Unit of Life

All living organisms are composed of cells. A typical cell contains:

  • Protoplasm — Colorless, jelly-like substance containing proteins, fats, carbohydrates, mineral salts, and water
  • Nucleus — Control center; directs cell reproduction and contains DNA
  • Cytoplasm — Fluid surrounding the nucleus containing organelles
  • Cell membrane — Outer layer that encloses the cell and regulates what enters/exits

Cells reproduce by mitosis (cell division), allowing tissues to grow and repair.

🧬 Tissues – Groups of Similar Cells

TypeFunctionExamples
EpithelialProtective coveringSkin, mucous membranes, glands
ConnectiveSupports, protects, bindsBone, cartilage, fat, blood
MuscularContracts and movesSkeletal, smooth, cardiac muscle
NervousCarries messagesNeurons, brain, spinal cord
In barbering, you work most directly with epithelial tissue (skin and hair) and muscular tissue (facial and neck muscles during massage and shaving).

🏗️ Body Systems Relevant to Barbering

Skeletal System

  • Provides framework, support, and protection
  • Key areas: skull (cranium and facial bones), cervical vertebrae (neck), mandible (lower jaw)

Muscular System

  • Produces movement
  • Key muscles: frontalis, occipitalis, orbicularis oculi, masseter, platysma, sternocleidomastoid

Nervous System

  • Controls and coordinates body activities
  • Cranial nerves V (trigeminal) and VII (facial) supply sensation and motor function to face and scalp

Circulatory System

  • Transports blood, oxygen, nutrients, and waste
  • Blood supply nourishes skin and hair follicles

Integumentary System

  • Skin and accessory organs: hair, nails, sweat and oil glands
  • This is the system barbers interact with most directly

Endocrine System

  • Glands that secrete hormones affecting hair growth, skin condition, oil/sweat gland activity
  • Major glands: pituitary, thyroid, adrenals, gonads

📝 Chapter 6 Key Takeaways

  • Anatomy studies body structures; physiology studies how they function
  • The cell is the basic unit of life; tissues are groups of similar cells; organs are groups of tissues
  • Barbers focus on the head, face, neck, skin, and hair
  • The integumentary system (skin, hair, glands) is most relevant for daily services
  • Muscular and nervous systems are critical for facial massage and shaving
  • The endocrine system influences hair growth, skin condition, and oil production
  • Understanding anatomy helps perform services safely and recognize problems
