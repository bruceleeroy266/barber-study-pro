CHAPTER 18

Haircoloring and Lightening

Haircolor theory, application techniques, and lightening

🎯 Why Study Haircoloring?

Haircoloring is one of the most popular and profitable services in barbering. Understanding haircolor allows you to:

  • Cover gray hair and restore natural color
  • Create fashion colors and highlights
  • Correct unwanted tones
  • Add dimension and depth to hair
  • Increase revenue through add-on services
Key Point: Haircoloring requires understanding color theory, hair structure, and chemical processes. Safety and proper technique are essential.

📚 History of Haircoloring

Haircoloring has been practiced for thousands of years:

  • Ancient Egypt: Used henna and other natural dyes; evidence found in tombs
  • 1880s America: Men began using haircolor to cover gray
  • Modern Era: Advanced chemical formulations provide natural-looking, long-lasting results

🧬 Natural Hair Color

Natural hair color comes from two types of melanin pigment in the cortex:

Eumelanin — Provides brown and black color. The more eumelanin, the darker the hair.
Pheomelanin — Provides red and yellow color. The more pheomelanin, the lighter and redder the hair.

Contributing Pigment (Undertone):

The underlying warm tones that exist in all natural hair color:

Understanding contributing pigment is crucial for color correction and achieving desired results.

🎨 Color Theory

Primary Colors:

  • Yellow — Warmest color; lightest primary
  • Red — Medium primary color
  • Blue — Coolest color; darkest primary

Secondary Colors (mix two primaries):

  • Green = Blue + Yellow
  • Orange = Red + Yellow
  • Violet = Blue + Red

Tertiary Colors (mix primary + secondary):

  • Yellow-green, blue-green, blue-violet, red-violet, red-orange, yellow-orange

Complementary Colors:

Colors opposite each other on the color wheel that neutralize each other:

Undesired ToneNeutralizing Color
RedGreen
OrangeBlue
YellowViolet
Use complementary colors to neutralize unwanted tones. For example, violet-based toners neutralize yellow.

🧪 Types of Haircolor

Temporary Haircolor:

  • Coats the hair shaft
  • Lasts 1 shampoo
  • No developer required
  • Used for special effects or color rinses

Semi-Permanent Haircolor:

  • Deposits color without lifting
  • Lasts 4-6 shampoos
  • No developer required
  • Good for enhancing natural color or covering gray

Demi-Permanent Haircolor:

  • Deposits color with minimal lifting
  • Lasts 12-24 shampoos
  • Low-volume developer (5-10 volume)
  • Good for gray blending and toning

Permanent Haircolor:

  • Lifts and deposits color
  • Lasts until hair grows out
  • Requires developer (10, 20, 30, or 40 volume)
  • Best for covering gray and dramatic color changes

🔬 Developer (Hydrogen Peroxide)

Developer activates the haircolor and determines the amount of lift:

✂️ Single-Process Permanent Color

Single-process color lifts and deposits in one step.

Virgin Application Procedure:

  1. Perform patch test 24-48 hours before service
  2. Conduct client consultation
  3. Drape client and apply protective cream
  4. Perform strand test
  5. Put on gloves and part dry hair into four sections
  6. Prepare color formula
  7. Apply color to mid-shaft area first (stay ½ inch from scalp)
  8. Work through all four sections
  9. Apply to scalp area in final 10-15 minutes
  10. Process according to manufacturer's directions
  11. Rinse thoroughly with warm water
  12. Shampoo and condition
  13. Style as desired

Retouch Application:

  1. Apply color to new growth only
  2. Do NOT overlap onto previously colored hair
  3. Process according to manufacturer's directions
  4. Pull color through to ends in final 5 minutes if needed
  5. Rinse, shampoo, and condition
Important: Overlapping permanent color on previously colored hair causes breakage and damage.

⚡ Double-Process Haircoloring

Double-process involves two steps: lightening first, then toning.

Step 1: Lightening (Pre-Lightening)

  1. Perform patch test 24-48 hours before
  2. Section dry hair into four quadrants
  3. Apply protective cream around hairline
  4. Prepare lightening formula
  5. Apply lightener ½ inch away from scalp first
  6. Work through mid-strands to porous ends
  7. Apply to scalp area last
  8. Process until desired level is reached
  9. Rinse thoroughly
  10. Shampoo with cool water

Step 2: Toner Application

  1. Towel-dry hair
  2. Apply toner to achieve desired tone
  3. Process according to manufacturer's directions
  4. Rinse thoroughly
  5. Shampoo and condition
  6. Style as desired

✨ Special Effects: Foil Techniques

Foil techniques create highlights, lowlights, and special effects.

Basic Foil Procedure:

  1. Perform patch test 24-48 hours before
  2. Section hair into six sections
  3. Prepare lightening formula
  4. Starting in back, take thin diagonal slices
  5. Weave out strands to be lightened
  6. Place foil under woven section
  7. Apply lightener 2 inches from scalp to ends
  8. Fold foil in half to enclose hair
  9. Continue working up the section
  10. Process according to strand test results
  11. Remove foils one at a time at shampoo area
  12. Rinse immediately to prevent color bleeding

Foil Application Order:

Back → Lower crown → Sides → Top → Front

🧔 Coloring Mustaches and Beards

Procedure:

  1. Seat client comfortably and drape for chemical service
  2. Apply clean headrest cover
  3. Recline chair slightly (45 degrees) to access under chin
  4. Place clean towel across chest and shoulders
  5. Cleanse facial hair with warm, soapy water
  6. Pat dry with cloth towel
  7. Apply petroleum jelly around facial hairline
  8. Prepare coloring solution
  9. Apply solution to mustache and/or beard only
  10. Avoid touching the skin
  11. Set timer according to manufacturer's directions
  12. Remove stains with manufacturer's stain remover
  13. Wash gently with soap and cool water
  14. Style as desired
CAUTION: Do not use aniline derivative tint with facial coloring. This may cause serious irritation and damage.

🧹 Clean-Up and Disinfection

After every haircoloring service:

  • Wash and disinfect all tools and implements
  • Clean and disinfect the work area
  • Sweep up hair and deposit in closed receptacle
  • Dispose of single-use items
  • Place all used linens, towels, and capes in laundry
  • Dispose of chemical products per manufacturer
  • Wash hands thoroughly
  • Record results on service record card

📝 Chapter 18 Key Takeaways

  • Natural hair color comes from eumelanin (brown/black) and pheomelanin (red/yellow)
  • Contributing pigment (undertone) must be considered when formulating color
  • Primary colors: yellow, red, blue
  • Complementary colors neutralize each other
  • Level system: 1 (black) to 10 (lightest blonde)
  • Four types of haircolor: temporary, semi-permanent, demi-permanent, permanent
  • Developer volumes: 10, 20, 30, 40 (higher = more lift)
  • Never overlap permanent color on previously colored hair
  • Double-process: lighten first, then tone
  • Foil application order: back → crown → sides → top → front
  • Do NOT use aniline derivative tint on facial hair
  • Always perform patch test 24-48 hours before chemical service
