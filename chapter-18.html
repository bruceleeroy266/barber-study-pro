CHAPTER 18

Haircoloring & Lightening

Color theory, products, application, and safety

🎯 Why Study Haircoloring?

  • Builds reliable and lucrative clientele
  • Increases versatility and services offered
  • Requires understanding of hair structure and color theory

🎨 Color Theory

Primary Colors:

  • Blue — Strongest, cool tone
  • Red — Medium
  • Yellow — Weakest, brightens/lightens

Secondary Colors:

  • Yellow + Blue = Green
  • Blue + Red = Violet
  • Red + Yellow = Orange

Complementary Colors (Neutralize Each Other):

  • Blue ↔ Orange
  • Red ↔ Green
  • Yellow ↔ Violet

Level System (1-10):

  • 1 = Black
  • 10 = Lightest blonde

🧴 Haircolor Products

Nonoxidative (No Lift, Deposit Only):

  • Temporary — Coats cuticle only; washes out with one shampoo
  • Semipermanent — Stains cuticle; lasts 6-8 shampoos

Oxidative (Can Lift and Deposit):

  • Demipermanent — Mixed with low-volume developer; blends gray
  • Permanent — Mixed with developer (usually 20-volume); can lighten and deposit

Developers:

  • 10-volume — Gentle deposit
  • 20-volume — Standard lift
  • 30/40-volume — Higher lift

⚠️ Safety

  • Patch test required 24-48 hours before aniline derivative products
  • Strand test determines processing time and color result
  • Protect skin and scalp during application
  • Keep detailed client records
Contributing pigment (undertone) — The pigment that remains after lightening. Critical for predicting final color results.

📝 Chapter 18 Key Takeaways

  • Always analyze porosity, texture, elasticity, density, natural level, contributing pigment
  • Color theory (primaries, secondaries, complementaries) is foundation for predictable results
  • Choose product based on desired outcome: temporary, semi/demi, or permanent
  • Safety: Patch test for aniline derivatives; strand test; protect skin
  • Gray hair and porous hair require special formulation
  • Record keeping and thorough consultation prevent mistakes
