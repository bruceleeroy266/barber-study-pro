CHAPTER 19

Preparing for Licensure and Employment

State board exams, job search, and career preparation

🎯 Why Study Licensure and Employment?

Passing your state board examination is required to become a licensed barber. Understanding the process helps you:

  • Successfully pass both written and practical exams
  • Develop effective study habits and test-taking strategies
  • Navigate the job search process effectively
  • Create professional resumes and portfolios
  • Prepare for interviews and employment
  • Understand legal aspects of employment
Key Point: Your license is your livelihood. Preparation is essential for passing the exam and starting your career.

📝 Preparing for the Written Exam

Study Habits and Time Management:

  • Start early: Don't wait until the last minute to study
  • Create a schedule: Set aside specific study times each day
  • Break it down: Study one chapter or topic at a time
  • Use multiple methods: Read, write notes, create flashcards, take practice tests
  • Study with others: Form study groups to review material
  • Take breaks: Study in 45-50 minute blocks with 10-minute breaks
  • Review regularly: Don't just cram; review material multiple times

Test-Taking Strategies:

Multiple Choice Questions:

  • Read the entire question carefully before looking at answers
  • Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
  • Watch for key words: "always," "never," "except," "not"
  • If unsure, make an educated guess (no penalty for wrong answers)
  • Don't second-guess yourself unless you're certain

True/False Questions:

  • Look for absolute words: "always," "never," "all," "none" (usually false)
  • Watch for qualifying words: "sometimes," "usually," "often" (usually true)
  • If any part is false, the entire statement is false

Matching Questions:

  • Read through all items before starting
  • Match the ones you're certain about first
  • Eliminate options as you go

Essay Questions:

  • Read the question carefully and identify what's being asked
  • Create a brief outline before writing
  • Answer all parts of the question
  • Use specific examples when possible
  • Write clearly and legibly

Deductive Reasoning:

When you don't know the answer, use logic:

  • Eliminate answers you know are wrong
  • Look for the most complete or specific answer
  • Consider what makes the most sense based on what you've learned
  • Trust your instincts

📅 On Test Day

Before the Exam:

  • Get a good night's sleep
  • Eat a healthy breakfast
  • Arrive early (30 minutes before exam time)
  • Bring required identification and materials
  • Wear comfortable, professional clothing
  • Stay calm and positive

During the Exam:

  • Listen carefully to instructions
  • Read all directions before starting
  • Budget your time (know how many questions and how much time)
  • Answer easy questions first
  • Mark difficult questions and return to them
  • Review your answers if time permits
  • Stay focused on your own work

After the Exam:

  • Don't dwell on questions you're unsure about
  • Wait for official results
  • If you don't pass, review weak areas and retake

✂️ The Practical Exam

The practical exam tests your hands-on skills. Requirements vary by state but typically include:

Common Practical Exam Components:

  • Haircutting (taper, fade, or specific style)
  • Shaving
  • Shampooing
  • Infection control and safety
  • Proper draping
  • Tool sanitation

Preparation Tips:

  • Practice each skill repeatedly until mastered
  • Time yourself to ensure you can complete within exam limits
  • Practice on different hair types and textures
  • Know exactly what supplies to bring
  • Ensure all tools are clean and in good working condition
  • Review the specific requirements for your state
  • Consider observing a practical exam if allowed

During the Practical Exam:

  • Listen carefully to examiner instructions
  • Follow all infection control and safety procedures
  • Work confidently but carefully
  • Focus on your own work, not other candidates
  • Look professional and neat

💼 Resume Development

What to Include:

  • Contact Information: Name, address, phone, email
  • Objective: Brief statement of career goals
  • Education: Barber school, graduation date, hours completed
  • Licensure: License number and status (if already licensed)
  • Skills: Haircutting, shaving, coloring, etc.
  • Experience: Any relevant work, internships, or school clinic
  • Achievements: Awards, honors, competitions
  • References: Professional contacts (instructors, employers)

Resume Tips:

  • Keep it to one page
  • Use clear, professional formatting
  • Proofread carefully for errors
  • Be honest and accurate
  • Tailor it to the specific position
  • Include numbers when possible (e.g., "Served 20+ clients daily")
  • Use action verbs: created, developed, managed, increased

Resume Don'ts:

  • Don't include salary requirements
  • Don't explain why you left previous jobs
  • Don't stretch the truth or lie
  • Don't include personal information (age, marital status)
  • Don't use unprofessional email addresses

📁 Employment Portfolio

A portfolio showcases your skills and professionalism.

What to Include:

  • Professional resume
  • Diploma and certificates
  • Before and after photos of your work
  • Awards and achievements
  • Letters of recommendation
  • Continuing education certificates
  • Professional organization memberships
  • Statement about why you chose barbering

Portfolio Tips:

  • Keep it organized and professional
  • Use high-quality photos
  • Include variety (different cuts, styles, hair types)
  • Make it easy to carry and present
  • Consider both physical and digital versions
  • Update regularly with new work

🔍 Job Search Strategies

Where to Look:

  • Online job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn, industry-specific sites)
  • Barbershop websites
  • Social media (Facebook, Instagram)
  • Networking with classmates and instructors
  • Walk-ins to local barbershops
  • Barber school placement services
  • Industry events and trade shows

Researching Potential Employers:

  • Visit the barbershop as a customer
  • Observe the atmosphere and clientele
  • Check online reviews
  • Look at their social media presence
  • Talk to current or former employees if possible

🤝 Interview Preparation

Before the Interview:

  • Research the barbershop thoroughly
  • Prepare questions to ask the employer
  • Practice answering common interview questions
  • Prepare your portfolio
  • Plan your outfit (professional, similar to shop's style)
  • Get directions and plan to arrive early

What to Bring:

  • Resume (multiple copies)
  • Portfolio
  • Identification
  • Social Security card
  • Driver's license
  • Professional references
  • Notepad and pen

Common Interview Questions:

  • Why do you want to work here?
  • What did you like best about your training?
  • What are your strongest skills?
  • How do you handle difficult clients?
  • What are your career goals?
  • Are you comfortable with retail sales?
  • How do you stay current with trends?
  • What makes you a good fit for our team?

Questions to Ask the Employer:

  • What is the commission or pay structure?
  • Are there opportunities for advancement?
  • What continuing education is offered?
  • How does the shop promote itself?
  • What is the typical client volume?
  • Is there a training period?
  • What are the expectations for retail sales?

Interview Appearance:

  • Dress professionally and appropriately for the shop's style
  • Ensure your own hair is well-groomed
  • Clean, professional attire
  • Minimal, tasteful accessories
  • Subtle or no fragrance
  • Clean, manicured nails

During the Interview:

  • Arrive 10-15 minutes early
  • Turn off your phone
  • Greet with a firm handshake and smile
  • Maintain good eye contact
  • Listen carefully and answer thoughtfully
  • Speak clearly and confidently
  • Show enthusiasm for the position
  • Thank the interviewer for their time

After the Interview:

  • Send a thank-you note or email within 24 hours
  • Follow up if you haven't heard back in a week
  • Continue your job search until you have an offer

⚖️ Legal Aspects of Employment

Illegal Interview Questions:

Employers cannot ask about:
  • Age or date of birth (unless verifying you're over 18)
  • Marital status or family plans
  • Race, ethnicity, or national origin
  • Religion
  • Disabilities or health conditions
  • Sexual orientation

If asked an illegal question, you can politely redirect: "I'd prefer to focus on my qualifications for this position."

Employee Contracts:

  • Read any contract thoroughly before signing
  • Understand non-compete clauses (may restrict where you can work after leaving)
  • Know what you're agreeing to regarding:
    • Pay structure (commission vs. hourly)
    • Booth rental fees (if applicable)
    • Product sales requirements
    • Scheduling expectations
    • Termination conditions
  • Consider having a lawyer review before signing

🎯 Employment Options

Types of Employment:

Commission-Based — Paid percentage of services performed. Common split is 60/40 or 70/30 (barber/shop).
Hourly/Salary — Paid set hourly rate or salary regardless of services performed.
Booth Rental — Rent a station/space and keep all earnings. Most independent option but requires business skills.
Independent Contractor — Self-employed but working within a shop. Handle own taxes and insurance.

Where to Work:

  • Traditional Barbershops: Focus on men's cuts, shaves, beard grooming
  • Unisex Salons: Serve both men and women; broader service menu
  • Upscale/Grooming Lounges: Premium services, higher prices
  • Chain Barbershops: Consistent walk-in traffic, structured environment
  • Mobile/On-Location: Travel to clients; weddings, events, house calls
  • Hotels/Resorts: Tourist clientele, steady hours
  • Corporate/On-Site: Provide services at corporate locations

📝 Chapter 19 Key Takeaways

  • Passing the state board exam is required for licensure
  • Written exam requires study habits, time management, test-taking strategies
  • Know your state's barber laws thoroughly
  • Practical exam tests hands-on skills with strict time limits
  • Create a professional resume highlighting skills and achievements
  • Build an employment portfolio with before/after photos
  • Research employers before applying and interviewing
  • Prepare for interviews with practice questions and professional appearance
  • Know your rights regarding illegal interview questions
  • Understand employment contracts before signing
  • Choose employment type that fits your goals: commission, hourly, booth rental
  • Send thank-you notes after interviews
