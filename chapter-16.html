CHAPTER 16

Women's Haircutting and Styling

Women's haircutting techniques and styling methods

🎯 Why Study Women's Haircutting?

Many barbershops now offer services to women. Understanding women's haircutting allows you to:

  • Expand your client base and increase earnings
  • Provide complete family services
  • Understand different cutting techniques for longer hair
  • Master wet setting, wrapping, and styling methods
  • Offer specialized services like pressing and flat ironing
Key Point: Women's services can significantly increase your revenue and make your barbershop a destination for the whole family.

✂️ Four Basic Women's Haircuts

1. Blunt Cut (0-Degree Elevation)

Characteristics: All hair is cut to the same length with no elevation. Creates a solid, heavy line.
  • Hair is combed to natural fall
  • Cut at 0 degrees (no elevation)
  • Creates weight and density at the bottom
  • Good for fine hair or one-length styles

2. Graduated Cut (45-Degree Elevation)

Characteristics: Hair is cut with elevation to create a graduated effect, shorter at the nape and gradually longer toward the crown.
  • Hair is elevated to 45 degrees
  • Creates a wedge shape
  • Builds weight in the perimeter
  • Classic bob shape

3. Uniform-Layered Cut (90-Degree Elevation)

Characteristics: All hair is cut to the same length when elevated to 90 degrees, creating equal layers throughout.
  • Hair is elevated to 90 degrees (perpendicular to head)
  • Creates equal layers
  • Removes weight evenly
  • Good for adding volume

4. Long-Layered Cut (180-Degree Elevation)

Characteristics: Hair is overdirected to create longer layers, with the shortest layers at the crown and longest at the perimeter.
  • Hair is overdirected (elevated beyond 90 degrees)
  • Creates longer layers
  • Maintains length while adding movement
  • Most common for long hair

✂️ Men's vs Women's Haircutting

Key Differences:

Men's CuttingWomen's Cutting
Usually shorter lengthsUsually longer lengths
More clipper workMore shear work
Tapering and fading commonLayering common
Dry or damp cuttingWet cutting preferred
Strong, defined linesSoft, blended lines

Similarities:

  • Both require proper sectioning
  • Both use guides for consistency
  • Both need cross-checking for accuracy
  • Both require understanding of head shape

✂️ Haircutting Reminders

  • Always work with clean, damp hair
  • Section hair properly before cutting
  • Establish a stationary or traveling guide
  • Keep consistent tension on the hair
  • Use the wide teeth of the comb for control
  • Cross-check the haircut when finished
  • Stand in proper body position for each section
  • Keep elbows up for better control

🔍 Cross-Checking

Cross-checking ensures the haircut is even and balanced.

How to Cross-Check:

  1. Comb hair in opposite direction from how it was cut
  2. Check for unevenness or weight lines
  3. Look at the haircut from all angles
  4. Check perimeter for balance
  5. Make adjustments as needed
Cross-checking horizontally shows length consistency. Cross-checking vertically shows layer consistency.

💇 Blunt Cut Procedure

Procedure:

  1. Perform client consultation
  2. Drape for wet service and shampoo
  3. Towel dry and change to haircutting cape
  4. Face client toward mirror and lock chair
  5. Detangle hair with wide-tooth comb
  6. Establish center profile parting from front to nape
  7. Take horizontal sections at nape
  8. Comb hair to natural fall at 0 degrees
  9. Cut parallel to parting
  10. Continue taking horizontal sections up the head
  11. Cross-check by combing hair opposite direction
  12. Detail perimeter and finish

💇 Graduated Cut Procedure

Procedure:

  1. Perform client consultation
  2. Shampoo and towel dry
  3. Establish center profile parting
  4. Take diagonal forward sections at nape
  5. Elevate hair to 45 degrees
  6. Cut at 45-degree angle to create graduation
  7. Continue with diagonal sections up the head
  8. Overdirect each section to previous guide
  9. Cross-check for balance
  10. Detail and finish

💇 Uniform-Layered Cut Procedure

Procedure:

  1. Perform client consultation
  2. Shampoo and towel dry
  3. Create center profile parting
  4. Take radial sections from crown
  5. Elevate hair to 90 degrees (perpendicular to head)
  6. Cut parallel to floor
  7. Continue with pivoting sections around the head
  8. Maintain 90-degree elevation throughout
  9. Cross-check horizontally and vertically
  10. Detail and finish

💇 Long-Layered Cut Procedure

Procedure:

  1. Perform client consultation
  2. Shampoo and towel dry
  3. Create center profile parting
  4. Take vertical sections from front to back
  5. Overdirect hair back to previous section
  6. Elevate beyond 90 degrees (180 degrees/overdirection)
  7. Cut to create longer layers
  8. Continue with vertical sections
  9. Cross-check for consistency
  10. Detail perimeter and finish

🌀 Hair Wrapping

Hair wrapping creates a smooth, straight style by wrapping hair around the head.

Materials Needed:

  • Boar-bristle paddle brush
  • Bobby pins
  • Conditioner
  • Duckbill clips
  • Haircutting cape
  • Hairnet
  • Hood dryer
  • Neck strip
  • Shampoo
  • Shampoo cape
  • Styling product (light oil optional)
  • Wide-tooth comb

Procedure:

  1. Wash hands
  2. Conduct client consultation
  3. Drape for wet service
  4. Shampoo and condition; blowdry if necessary
  5. Remove waterproof cape
  6. Escort to styling chair
  7. Face client toward mirror and lock chair
  8. Drape with neck strip and haircutting cape
  9. Press dry hair
  10. Remove tangles with wide-tooth comb
  11. Apply light oil or styling aid
  12. Create parting from recession to crown
  13. Comb hair flat to right of parting
  14. Starting on heavy side, wrap hair smooth to head shape
  15. Use duckbill clips or bobby pins to hold hair
  16. Continue wrapping in clockwise direction
  17. Smooth down with hand or brush
  18. When all hair is wrapped, stretch neck strip or hairnet around head
  19. Place under hood dryer 45 minutes to 1 hour
  20. Style as desired

💨 Blowdrying Blunt or Long-Layered Hair

Materials Needed:

  • Blowdryer with attachments
  • Conditioner
  • Haircutting cape
  • Neck strip
  • Paddle brush
  • Round brush
  • Sectioning clips
  • Shampoo
  • Shampoo cape
  • Styling and finishing products
  • Towels
  • Wide-tooth comb

Procedure:

  1. Wash hands
  2. Conduct client consultation
  3. Drape for wet service
  4. Shampoo and towel dry
  5. Remove waterproof cape
  6. Escort to styling chair
  7. Face client toward mirror and lock chair
  8. Place clean neck strip
  9. Drape with cutting/styling cape
  10. Fold neck strip over cape
  11. Remove tangles with wide-tooth comb
  12. Apply light gel or straightening gel
  13. Attach nozzle or concentrator
  14. Part and section hair
  15. Using 1-inch subsections, start at nape
  16. Use classic styling brush to dry straight and smooth
  17. Place brush under first section and hold hair low
  18. Follow brush with dryer nozzle
  19. Bend ends in desired direction
  20. Work up to crown in 1-inch sections
  21. On longer sections, switch to paddle brush
  22. Use curve of brush to add bends to ends
  23. After each section, use cool button to set
  24. For fuller look, switch to round brush
  25. Continue on sides with 1-inch sections
  26. Hold at low elevation
  27. Bend ends under or outward

🔥 Hair Pressing (Hot Comb)

Pressing straightens curly or coarse hair using a heated pressing comb.

Materials Needed:

  • Blowdryer with attachments
  • Brush
  • Clips
  • Comb
  • Electric heater (stove)
  • Haircutting cape
  • Neck strip
  • Pressing comb
  • Pressing cream, wax, or oil
  • Shampoo
  • Shampoo cape
  • Styling pomade
  • Towels

Procedure:

  1. Clean and disinfect work area
  2. Conduct consultation and hair analysis
  3. Wash hands
  4. Drape and shampoo
  5. Towel-blot hair
  6. Drape with chair cloth and neck strip
  7. Heat the pressing comb
  8. Blowdry hair
  9. Apply pressing cream, wax, or oil
  10. Comb and part into four sections
  11. Test comb temperature with paper - if it scorches, it's too hot
  12. Divide first section into 1-inch subsections
  13. Apply additional pressing cream as needed
  14. Hold subsection away from scalp
  15. Insert teeth of comb into top side of section
  16. Draw out comb slightly, making quick turn
  17. Press comb slowly through hair until ends pass through
  18. Continue until all hair is pressed
  19. Add pomade if desired
  20. Style and comb or finish with thermal irons
Safety: Always test comb temperature before using on hair to prevent burning.

🔥 Flat Iron Pressing

Flat irons provide a modern alternative to hot combs for straightening hair.

Materials Needed:

  • Blowdryer with attachments
  • Brush
  • Clips
  • Electric flat iron
  • Finishing spray
  • Haircutting cape
  • Leave-in thermal styling spray
  • Neck strip
  • Shampoo
  • Shampoo cape
  • Styling comb
  • Tail comb
  • Towels

Procedure:

  1. Clean and disinfect work area
  2. Conduct consultation and hair analysis
  3. Wash hands
  4. Drape and shampoo
  5. Towel-blot and apply leave-in thermal spray
  6. Drape with styling cape and neck strip
  7. Heat the flat iron
  8. Blowdry and style according to cut
  9. Comb and part into four sections
  10. Divide first section into subsections based on density
  11. Test iron temperature with paper
  12. Hold subsection away from scalp
  13. Grasp between plates of flat iron
  14. Insert comb under top side to protect scalp
  15. Draw iron smoothly through subsection
  16. Recomb and reapply as necessary
  17. Repeat through each section
  18. To increase lift, comb and elevate before ironing
  19. Comb and style
  20. Finish with styling spray

🧹 Clean-Up and Disinfection

After every women's haircutting service:

  • Clean and disinfect all tools and implements
  • Clean and disinfect work area
  • Clean and disinfect chair and workstation
  • Sweep up hair and deposit in closed receptacle
  • Dispose of single-use items
  • Place all used linens, towels, and capes in laundry
  • Dispose of used blades in sharps container
  • Wash hands thoroughly

📝 Chapter 16 Key Takeaways

  • Four basic women's cuts: blunt (0°), graduated (45°), uniform-layered (90°), long-layered (180°)
  • Women's cutting uses more shear work and wet cutting
  • Always cross-check haircuts for accuracy
  • Hair wrapping creates smooth, straight styles
  • Blowdrying techniques vary by brush type
  • Hot comb pressing requires temperature testing
  • Flat iron pressing is a modern alternative
  • Women's services expand client base and revenue
  • Proper sectioning and elevation are critical
  • Always follow clean-up and disinfection procedures
