CHAPTER 16

Women's Haircutting & Styling

Blunt, graduated, and layered cuts with styling techniques

🎯 Why Study Women's Haircutting?

  • Expands your client base and earning potential
  • Builds confidence in serving all clients
  • Many women now visit barbers for cuts and styling
Never assume a client wants a men's-style service. Always conduct a thorough consultation.

✂️ Four Basic Women's Haircuts

1. Blunt Cut (0° elevation)

  • One-length cut forming heavy weight line at perimeter
  • Head must remain upright

2. Graduated Cut (45° elevation)

  • Wedge or stacked shape with weight at perimeter
  • Builds fullness along design line

3. Uniform-Layered Cut (90° elevation)

  • All hair cut to same length straight out from head
  • Uses traveling guides

4. Long-Layered Cut (180° elevation)

  • Increased layering from top to perimeter
  • Hair on top shorter than at perimeter
Elevation controls the balance between weight and layering.

🔄 Other Techniques

  • Overdirection — Shifting hair away from natural fall to increase perimeter length
  • Razor Cutting — Produces softer ends; use 45° angle on damp hair
  • Point Cutting — Steep shear angle at ends
  • Slithering/Slicing — Sliding partially open shears

💇 Curly Hair

  • Curly hair has crest (top of wave) and trough (bottom)
  • Cutting in trough may cause ends to flip inward
  • Curly hair shrinks when dry — cut conservatively
  • Maintenance cuts every 4 weeks common

💨 Styling Techniques

  • Wet Hairstyling — Finger waves, pin curls, roller sets
  • Blowdry Styling — Brush and products for straight or wavy looks
  • Thermal Styling — Curling irons, flat irons, pressing combs
  • Hair Wrapping — Keeps hair smooth and straight
Thermal Safety: Test temperature first, use protectants, never overheat.

📝 Chapter 16 Key Takeaways

  • Four basic cuts: Blunt (0°), Graduated (45°), Uniform-layered (90°), Long-layered (180°)
  • Elevation is key control for weight vs. layering
  • Analyze texture and density before choosing technique
  • Texturizing adds movement after basic shape
  • Curly hair requires attention to wave pattern and shrinkage
  • Match styling method to haircut and client preference
