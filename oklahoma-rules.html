Oklahoma Barber Licensing Requirements

Everything you need to know about becoming a licensed barber in Oklahoma

📋 Quick Requirements Overview

  • Training Hours Required: 1,500 hours at a licensed barber school
  • Minimum Age: 16 years old
  • Education: 8th grade education or equivalent
  • Passing Score: 75% on both written and practical exams
  • Exam Provider: PSI (Psychological Services Inc.) / Prov

🎓 Training & Education Requirements

Barber Program (1,500 Hours)

To qualify for the Oklahoma barber license, you must complete 1,500 hours of training at a licensed barber school. The curriculum covers:

  • Hair cutting and styling techniques
  • Shaving and facial treatments
  • Sanitation, sterilization, and infection control
  • Oklahoma state laws and rules
  • Anatomy and physiology
  • Chemistry of hair and scalp
  • Business management and professional ethics

📝 Oklahoma State Board Exam

The Oklahoma State Board of Cosmetology and Barbering uses PSI (Psychological Services Inc.), now part of Prov, to administer the barber licensing examination.

Exam Components

Written Examination

  • Multiple choice questions covering theory and Oklahoma laws
  • Must score 75% or higher to pass
  • Topics include: sanitation, anatomy, chemistry, and state regulations

Practical Examination

  • Hands-on demonstration of barbering skills
  • Must score 75% or higher to pass
  • Includes: haircutting, shaving, sanitation procedures

Scheduling Your Exam

After completing your 1,500 hours, your school will submit your application to the state board. Once approved, you'll receive authorization to schedule your exam through PSI/Prov.

  • Exams are held at PSI testing centers throughout Oklahoma
  • You can schedule online or by phone
  • Bring valid photo ID to the exam
  • Arrive 30 minutes early for check-in

🔄 Reciprocity & Transfer

If you're already licensed in another state, Oklahoma may offer reciprocity if:

  • Your current license is in good standing
  • You have at least 1,500 hours of training (or equivalent)
  • You passed a written and practical exam in your original state
  • You pass the Oklahoma state law exam

Contact the Oklahoma State Board directly for current reciprocity requirements.

🔄 License Renewal

  • Oklahoma barber licenses must be renewed annually
  • Continuing education may be required
  • Renewal fees apply
  • Late renewals may incur additional penalties
