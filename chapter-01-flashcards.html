CHAPTER 1

History of Barbering

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

1/18

Meryma'at

Click to reveal

Egyptian barbers who held such high esteem that they were sculpted for posterity. They used copper and bronze tools including combs, brushes, mirrors, and razors.

2/18

Barba

Click to reveal

Latin word meaning "beard" from which the word "barber" is derived.

3/18

Tonsorial

Click to reveal

Term referring to the cutting, clipping, or trimming of hair with shears or a razor.

4/18

Ticinius Mena

Click to reveal

Brought barbering and shaving to Rome in 296 BC. Barbershops became centers for daily news and gossip.

5/18

Barber-Surgeon

Click to reveal

Medieval barbers who performed medical services including bloodletting, minor surgery, tooth pulling, and herbal remedies.

6/18

Council of Tours (1163)

Click to reveal

Pope forbade clergy from acting as physicians or surgeons, leading barbers to take over these medical duties.

7/18

Worshipful Company of Barbers

Click to reveal

First barber guild formed in London, England in 1308.

8/18

Company of Barber-Surgeons

Click to reveal

Formed in 1450 when English barbers and surgeons merged. Lasted until 1745 when surgeons separated fully.

9/18

Barber Pole Symbolism

Click to reveal

Red = Blood
White = Bandages
Blue = Veins
Pole = Staff patient held
Basin = Blood bowl

10/18

Tonsure

Click to reveal

Shaved patch on the crown of the head worn by medieval clergy. Abolished by Roman Catholic Church in 1972.

11/18

A.B. Moler

Click to reveal

Established America's first barber school in Chicago, Illinois in 1893.

12/18

First Barber Licensing Law

Click to reveal

Passed in Minnesota in 1897, focusing on sanitation, education, and disinfection.

13/18

AMBBA

Click to reveal

Associated Master Barbers of America, founded in 1924. Standardized training, created education council, and established barber code of ethics.

14/18

NABBA

Click to reveal

National Association of State Boards of Barber Examiners, formed in 1929. Now represents over 300,000 barbers and promotes standards for licensing.

15/18

Alexander the Great

Click to reveal

Ordered soldiers to shave so beards couldn't be grabbed in battle, making shaving gain popularity.

16/18

Electric Hair Clippers

Click to reveal

Leo J. Wahl invented first practical electric hair clippers in 1919. Wahl Clipper Corporation founded in 1921.

17/18

Beatles on Ed Sullivan

Click to reveal

February 9, 1964 appearance marked a symbolic turning point affecting barbershop culture with the rise of longer hair trends.

18/18

Barbering Resurgence

Click to reveal

Began in the 2000s with new schools opening, independent/franchised barbershops offering masculine atmosphere, and the "art of manliness" movement.
