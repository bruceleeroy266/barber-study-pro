CHAPTER 3

Professional Image

Hygiene, ergonomics, and communication skills

🎯 Why Study Professional Image?

First impressions are critical for job interviews, gaining new clients, and building a professional reputation. Barbers are held to higher image standards because clients view them as image experts.

A positive professional image includes: appearance, positive attitude, team camaraderie, proper ergonomics, and good communication skills.

Key Point: One weak moment (body odor, bad breath, stained clothes) can cause clients to not return and share negative experiences.

🧼 Apply Healthful Habits

Personal Hygiene

Basic daily cleanliness is essential when working in close proximity to clients:

  • Wash hands frequently throughout the day, including at the start of each service
  • Perform self-checks and freshen under arms as needed
  • Brush and floss teeth; use mouthwash or breath mints
  • Do not smoke during work hours. If you smoke on break, brush teeth, use mouthwash, and wash hands afterward

Rest and Relaxation

  • Adequate sleep is essential for health and efficient functioning
  • Most people need 7–8 hours per night (varies by individual)
  • Relaxation (movies, reading, sports, dancing) provides a change of pace and helps you return to work refreshed

Nutrition

  • What you eat affects health, appearance, personality, and job performance
  • Follow a balanced diet with variety of vitamins and minerals
  • Drink plenty of water daily
  • Limit sugar, salt, caffeine, and highly processed/fatty foods

Nail Care

  • Barbers use their hands constantly — do not neglect your own nail care
  • Keep nails at a suitable length for your style and keep them thoroughly clean
  • Occasional chipped polish or broken nails is okay, but not as a regular occurrence

🪑 Employ Proper Ergonomics

Ergonomics The science of designing workplace/tools for comfort, efficiency, and safety

Posture Guidelines (Standing Behind the Chair):

  • Keep head up, chin parallel to floor
  • Neck elongated and balanced over shoulders
  • Chest up and out (no slouching)
  • Shoulders level and relaxed
  • Spine straight

Correct Sitting Posture:

  • Hips level, knees flexed slightly over feet
  • Back straight, weight on thigh bones (not end of spine)
  • Feet flat on floor
  • Chair height so arms form 90° angle when writing

Suggestions to Avoid Problems:

  • Do not grip tools too tightly
  • Avoid constant wrist bending
  • Keep arms at <60° angle when raised
  • Adjust chair height for client's head
  • Keep wrists neutral
  • Use ergonomic tools, antifatigue mats, proper stance (legs hip-width, knees bent)

👔 Follow Image-Building Basics

Dress for Success

  • Wardrobe should complement the barbershop's image and be professional
  • Clothes must be clean, pressed, stain-free, and functional/comfortable
  • Avoid overly baggy or tight clothing
  • Follow shop dress code (e.g., barber jacket, smock, tie, specific colors)
  • Shoes: supportive, polished, in good repair (no flip-flops or open-toed around tools)

Hair Maintenance

  • Keep your own haircut/style fresh and up-to-date
  • Schedule regular appointments (barter with other barbers if needed)
  • Facial hair: keep beard/mustache neat and trimmed; clean-shaven barbers should shave daily

Skin Care and Makeup

  • Healthy skin promotes a professional image
  • Develop a skin care regimen (cleansing, sunscreen, etc.)
  • Makeup (if used) should enhance features subtly — never apply at your station
  • Caution: Many shops have a no-fragrance policy due to allergies/sensitivities

💬 Practice Effective Communication

Human Relations Interactions and relationships between people

Guidelines for Good Human Relations:

  • Greet clients by name with a pleasant tone
  • Be alert to client's mood; be a good listener
  • Choose conversation topics carefully (avoid controversial subjects, gossip, personal issues)
  • Maintain confidentiality
  • Look and act professionally at all times
  • Show genuine interest and give undivided attention
  • Use tact when handling problems
  • Be punctual and efficient
  • Practice emotional control and a positive approach

Steps for Determining Client Expectations:

  • Organize your thoughts — Ask clear questions
  • Clarify — Make sure you understand what the client means
  • Repeat — Confirm the client's description back to them

Social Media

  • Powerful tool when used correctly, but can easily damage reputation if misused
  • Do: Manage pages professionally, post helpful content, communicate appropriately
  • Don't: Use profanity, argue online, post embarrassing/nude photos, forward spam

📝 Chapter 3 Key Takeaways

  • Barbers are held to higher image standards because clients view them as image experts
  • Four key personal hygiene habits: hand washing, freshening, dental care, no smoking during work
  • Ergonomics prevents musculoskeletal disorders from repetitive motions
  • Dress for success: clean, pressed, professional wardrobe matching shop image
  • Keep your own haircut fresh and facial hair neat
  • Practice effective communication: organize thoughts, clarify, repeat
  • Use social media professionally — it can build or destroy your reputation
  • Maintaining a strong professional image is a daily investment that directly impacts your success
