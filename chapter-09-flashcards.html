CHAPTER 9

Properties of the Hair & Scalp

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

1/20

Hair

Click to reveal

A slender, threadlike outgrowth of the skin composed of keratin. Grows from hair follicles in the dermis.

2/20

Keratin

Click to reveal

A fibrous protein that is the primary component of hair, nails, and skin. Provides strength and structure.

3/20

Hair Follicle

Click to reveal

The tube-like depression in the skin from which a hair grows. Contains the hair root and is attached to sebaceous glands.

4/20

Hair Root

Click to reveal

The part of the hair located below the skin surface, inside the follicle. The living, growing part of hair.

5/20

Hair Shaft

Click to reveal

The portion of hair that extends above the skin surface. Composed of dead, keratinized cells.

6/20

Dermal Papilla

Click to reveal

A small, cone-shaped elevation at the base of the hair follicle that contains blood vessels and nerves. Feeds the hair.

7/20

Hair Bulb

Click to reveal

The lowest part of the hair strand, surrounding the dermal papilla. Contains the dividing cells that produce new hair.

8/20

Sebaceous Gland

Click to reveal

Oil glands connected to hair follicles that secrete sebum. Lubricates the hair and skin.

9/20

Sebum

Click to reveal

The oily substance secreted by sebaceous glands. Natural conditioner that keeps hair and skin soft and pliable.

10/20

Cuticle

Click to reveal

The outermost layer of the hair shaft. Made of overlapping scales that protect the inner layers.

11/20

Cortex

Click to reveal

The middle layer of the hair shaft. Contains melanin (color) and gives hair strength and elasticity.

12/20

Medulla

Click to reveal

The innermost layer of the hair shaft. A hollow tube or series of cells. Not present in all hair types.

13/20

Melanin

Click to reveal

The pigment that gives hair its color. Produced by melanocytes in the hair bulb. Two types: eumelanin and pheomelanin.

14/20

Anagen Phase

Click to reveal

The growth phase of hair. Lasts 2-6 years. About 90% of scalp hair is in this phase at any time.

15/20

Catagen Phase

Click to reveal

The transitional phase of hair growth. Lasts 2-3 weeks. Hair follicle shrinks and detaches from dermal papilla.

16/20

Telogen Phase

Click to reveal

The resting phase of hair growth. Lasts 3-4 months. Old hair sheds and new hair begins to grow.

17/20

Porosity

Click to reveal

The hair's ability to absorb moisture. Determined by the condition of the cuticle layer. High, normal, or low porosity.

18/20

Elasticity

Click to reveal

The hair's ability to stretch and return to its original length without breaking. Indicates hair health.

19/20

Hair Density

Click to reveal

The number of individual hair strands per square inch on the scalp. Average person has about 100,000 hairs.

20/20

Hair Texture

Click to reveal

The thickness or diameter of individual hair strands. Classified as fine, medium, or coarse.
