Chapter 14: Shaving and Facial Hair Design

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

Straight Razor

A traditional shaving tool with a single blade that folds into the handle; requires skill and proper sanitation.

Safety Razor

A razor with a protective guard to reduce the risk of cuts; uses disposable blades.

Shaving Cream

A product applied to the skin to provide lubrication and protection during shaving.

Shaving Soap

A hard soap that is lathered with a brush to create rich foam for traditional shaving.

Shaving Brush

A brush used to apply lather and lift beard hair for a closer shave.

Strop

A leather strap used to sharpen and polish a straight razor blade before use.

Honing

The process of sharpening a straight razor on a whetstone to maintain the blade edge.

Grain of the Beard

The direction in which facial hair grows; important for determining shaving technique.

With the Grain

Shaving in the same direction as hair growth; reduces irritation and ingrown hairs.

Against the Grain

Shaving opposite to the direction of hair growth; provides the closest shave but may cause irritation.

Aftershave

A product applied after shaving to soothe skin, close pores, and provide fragrance.

Alum Block

A natural mineral block used to stop bleeding from minor nicks and cuts after shaving.

Beard Design

The art of shaping and styling facial hair to complement a client's facial features.

Mustache

Facial hair grown on the upper lip; can be styled in various shapes and sizes.

Goatee

A beard style featuring hair on the chin only, often combined with a mustache.

Sideburns

Facial hair grown on the sides of the face in front of the ears.

Neckline

The defined edge where beard hair ends on the neck; proper shaping is essential for a clean look.

Cheek Line

The upper boundary of beard growth on the cheeks; should follow natural growth patterns.

Ingrown Hair

A hair that grows back into the skin causing inflammation; common after improper shaving.

Razor Burn

Skin irritation caused by shaving; appears as redness, burning, or small bumps.
