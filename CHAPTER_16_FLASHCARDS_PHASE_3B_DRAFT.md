## 21. Cutting-Line Direction

**Front:**  
How does **cutting-line direction** affect the finished shape of a blunt cut?

## 26. Weight Distribution and Consistent Elevation in Uniform Layers

**Front:**  
How do **uniform layers** distribute weight, and why is consistent elevation critical?

**Back:**  
Uniform layers spread weight evenly throughout the head instead of concentrating it at the perimeter or nape. Consistent elevation is required because changing the angle changes layer length, breaking the even, balanced result that defines a uniform layer.

## 36. Razor Cutting

**Front:**  
What is **razor cutting**, and on which hair types is it inappropriate?

**Back:**  
Razor cutting uses a razor to soften ends and create movement. It is not appropriate for fine, fragile, or highly porous hair because it can fray or weaken the strands.

## 41. Thermal Tools on Damp Hair

**Front:**  
Why should thermal tools never be applied to damp hair unless designed for it?

**Back:**  
Applying heat to damp hair causes rapid steam expansion inside the strand, which can damage the cuticle and weaken the hair.

## 42. Thermal Tool Temperature and Client Protection

**Front:**  
How does hair condition affect thermal tool temperature selection, and how do you protect the client?

**Back:**  
Fine or damaged hair needs lower heat; healthy, coarse hair can tolerate more. Always keep tools moving, protect skin and ears from hot surfaces, and place tools on a heat-safe mat when not in use.
