### Complete Anatomical & Technical Content:

**1. FACIAL ANATOMY - MUSCLES**
- **Frontalis:** Forehead, raises eyebrows
- **Corrugator:** Between eyebrows, draws brows together (frown)
- **Procerus:** Bridge of nose, wrinkles nose
- **Orbicularis oculi:** Surrounds eye, closes eyelid
- **Nasalis:** Nose, flares nostrils
- **Levator labii superioris:** Upper lip, elevates lip/nostril
- **Zygomaticus major/minor:** Cheekbone to mouth, smile muscles
- **Risorius:** Corner of mouth, grin/smile
- **Orbicularis oris:** Surrounds mouth, closes lips
- **Mentalis:** Chin, raises/wrinkles chin
- **Triangularis (Depressor anguli oris):** Lowers corner of mouth
- **Buccinator:** Cheek wall, compresses cheeks (blowing)
- **Masseter:** Jaw, closes jaw (chewing)
- **Temporalis:** Temple/side of head, closes jaw
- **Platysma:** Neck to chin, lowers jaw/lip

**2. FACIAL ANATOMY - NERVES**
- **Fifth cranial (Trigeminal):** Chief sensory nerve of face, motor nerve for chewing
  - Ophthalmic branch: Upper face, forehead, eyes
  - Maxillary branch: Mid-face, upper lip, nose, cheeks
  - Mandibular branch: Lower face, jaw, lower lip, chin
- **Seventh cranial (Facial):** Chief motor nerve for facial expression
  - Controls all facial muscles except chewing muscles
  - Temporal, zygomatic, buccal, mandibular, cervical branches
- **Eleventh cranial (Spinal accessory):** Neck/shoulder movement (sternocleidomastoid, trapezius)

**3. FACIAL ANATOMY - BLOOD SUPPLY**
- **Arteries (carry oxygenated blood TO face):**
  - Common carotid → External carotid → Facial artery
  - Superficial temporal artery
  - Infraorbital, submental, frontal arteries
- **Veins (carry deoxygenated blood FROM face):**
  - Facial vein, superficial temporal vein
  - Drain to jugular vein

**4. MASSAGE MANIPULATIONS**
- **Effleurage:** Light stroking, soothing, begins/ends massage
- **Pétrissage:** Kneading, grasping, lifting tissue, deep pressure
- **Friction:** Deep rubbing, circular or back-and-forth, stimulates circulation
- **Tapotement (Percussion):** Tapping, slapping, hacking movements, stimulates nerves
- **Vibration:** Rapid shaking movement, soothing or stimulating

**5. MASSAGE TECHNIQUES**
- **Direction:** Always toward heart (improves venous/lymph flow)
- **Pressure:** Firm but gentle, adjust to client comfort
- **Rhythm:** Slow, steady, continuous
- **Duration:** 10-20 minutes typical
- **Order:** Effleurage → Pétrissage/Friction → Effleurage

**6. MODALITIES**
- **Electric Currents:**
  - Faradic: Muscle stimulation/toning
  - Galvanic: Deep product penetration (iontophoresis)
  - High-frequency (Tesla): Germicidal, healing, stimulation
- **Light Rays:**
  - Infrared: Heat therapy, increases circulation
  - Ultraviolet: Germicidal, limited exposure
- **Heat Therapy:**
  - Hot towels: Opens pores, softens tissues
  - Steamers: Hydrates, softens, opens pores
- **Moist Heat:** Prepares skin for deep cleansing/extraction

**7. CONTRAINDICATIONS (DO NOT PERFORM)**
- Active acne/severe inflammation
- Open wounds, cuts, abrasions
- Contagious skin conditions
- Severe sunburn
- Recent facial surgery
- Uncontrolled high blood pressure
- Heart conditions (without physician approval)
- Skin cancer or suspicious lesions
- Fever or illness
- Client discomfort or refusal

**8. TREATMENT PROTOCOLS**
- **Consultation:** Always assess skin, medical history, contraindications
- **Sanitation:** Disinfect all tools, wash hands, use clean linens
- **Draping:** Professional, protect clothing
- **Product Selection:** Match to skin type (dry, oily, normal, combination, sensitive)
- **Order:** Cleanse → Steam → Massage → Mask → Tone → Moisturize
- **Documentation:** Record services, products, client reactions

**9. PROFESSIONAL SAFETY**
- Never diagnose skin conditions (refer to dermatologist)
- Never treat infectious/contagious conditions
- Always test product sensitivity first
- Never use modalities without proper training
- Follow manufacturer guidelines for all equipment
- Maintain proper posture to prevent injury
- Use gloves when appropriate

**10. CLIENT COMMUNICATION**
- Explain each step before performing
- Ask about comfort, pressure, temperature
- Educate on home care
- Set realistic expectations
- Document preferences for future visits
- Professional boundaries always
