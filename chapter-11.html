CHAPTER 11

Treatment of the Hair and Scalp

Draping, shampooing, scalp massage, and therapeutic treatments

🎯 Why Study Hair and Scalp Treatments?

Hair and scalp treatments are among the most relaxing and beneficial services a barber can provide. Understanding proper treatment techniques helps you:

  • Keep the hair and scalp in a healthy, attractive condition
  • Understand the purpose of various products and when to use them
  • Recognize disorders and know when to refer to a physician
  • Build client loyalty through excellent basic services
  • Provide therapeutic value beyond just cutting hair
Key Point: A great shampoo and scalp treatment can turn a simple haircut into a memorable, relaxing experience that keeps clients coming back.

🛡️ Draping Procedures

Proper draping protects the client's clothing and skin while maintaining comfort and sanitation.

For Wet or Chemical Services:

Procedure:

  1. Seat client comfortably
  2. Place towel around shoulders
  3. Position waterproof cape over towel (don't touch neck)
  4. Fasten securely at back
  5. Fold second towel over cape edge if extra protection needed

For Haircutting:

Procedure:

  1. Place neck strip or paper towel around neck
  2. Drape nylon cape loosely over shoulders
  3. Tuck under neck strip without touching skin
Always use a barrier (towel or neck strip) between cape and skin to prevent irritation.

Always analyze before any treatment to determine the appropriate products and techniques.

🚿 Shampooing Procedure

Proper shampooing is the foundation of all hair services.

Complete Shampoo Procedure:

  1. Drape client properly with waterproof cape and towels
  2. Perform hair and scalp analysis
  3. Adjust chair and position client at sink comfortably
  4. Test water temperature on wrist (warm, not hot)
  5. Wet hair thoroughly from scalp to ends
  6. Apply appropriate amount of shampoo
  7. Work into lather using massage manipulations
  8. Massage scalp thoroughly (not just hair)
  9. Rinse completely until water runs clear
  10. Repeat if necessary for very dirty hair
  11. Apply conditioner to mid-lengths and ends if needed
  12. Leave conditioner on for recommended time
  13. Rinse thoroughly with cool water
  14. Towel-blot hair gently (don't rub)

Water Temperature:

  • Warm water — For cleansing and opening cuticle
  • Cool water — For final rinse to close cuticle and add shine
  • Hot water — Avoid; can damage hair and irritate scalp

💆 Scalp Massage Manipulations

Scalp massage stimulates blood circulation, relaxes muscles, and promotes healthy hair growth.

Basic Massage Movements:

Effleurage (EF-luh-rahzh) — Light, continuous stroking movement applied with fingers and palms in a slow, rhythmic manner. Used to relax and spread product.
Friction — Deep rubbing movement requiring pressure on the skin while moving over an underlying structure. Used to stimulate circulation.
Petrissage (PEH-trih-sahzh) — Kneading movement performed by lifting, squeezing, and pressing the tissue. Stimulates deeper tissues.
Tapotement (tah-POHT-mahnt) — Fast, tapping, slapping, or hacking movements. Stimulates blood flow and energizes the scalp.
Vibration — Rapid shaking movement applied with fingertips or palms. Stimulates nerves and increases circulation.

Scalp Massage Procedure:

  1. Position client comfortably with neck supported
  2. Begin with effleurage to relax the client
  3. Use friction movements on the scalp
  4. Apply petrissage to stimulate circulation
  5. Finish with effleurage to relax
  6. Duration: typically 3-5 minutes
Benefits of Scalp Massage: Increases blood circulation, relaxes muscles, distributes natural oils, promotes hair growth, and provides relaxation.

🧪 Types of Hair and Scalp Treatments

Conditioning Treatments:

Instant Conditioners — Applied after shampooing and rinsed out immediately. Detangle and add shine.
Deep Conditioners — Left on hair for extended time (10-30 minutes). Penetrate cortex for intensive repair.
Leave-in Conditioners — Applied to towel-dried hair and not rinsed out. Provide ongoing protection.

Scalp Treatments:

Scalp Conditioners — Used to treat dry, oily, or dandruff-prone scalps. Applied directly to scalp.
Astringent Tonics — Used on oily scalps to remove excess oil and tighten pores.
Medicated Treatments — Contain active ingredients to treat specific scalp conditions like dandruff or seborrhea.

Specialized Treatments:

Protein Treatments — Rebuild strength in damaged hair by penetrating the cortex.
Moisture Treatments — Add hydration to dry, brittle hair.
Hot Oil Treatments — Warm oil applied to hair and scalp to moisturize and condition.

🦠 Safety and Sanitation

Universal Precautions:

  • Wash hands before and after each service
  • Wear gloves if client has open sores or scalp conditions
  • Disinfect all tools and equipment between clients
  • Use clean towels and capes for each client
  • Never use the same neck strip or towel on multiple clients

When to Refer to a Physician:

  • Suspected fungal infection (tinea)
  • Signs of bacterial infection (pus, severe inflammation)
  • Parasitic infestation (lice, scabies)
  • Severe or persistent dandruff not responding to treatment
  • Unusual hair loss patterns
  • Open sores or lesions on scalp

📝 Chapter 11 Key Takeaways

  • Proper draping protects clothing and maintains sanitation
  • Always perform hair and scalp analysis before choosing products
  • Select products based on hair type and scalp condition
  • Shampooing involves proper wetting, lathering, massage, and rinsing
  • Five basic massage movements: effleurage, friction, petrissage, tapotement, vibration
  • Scalp massage increases circulation and promotes relaxation
  • Types of treatments: conditioning, scalp, protein, moisture, hot oil
  • Electrical equipment can enhance treatment effectiveness
  • Follow universal precautions for safety
  • Refer to physician for infections, parasites, or severe conditions
