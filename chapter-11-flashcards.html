Chapter 11: Properties and Disorders of the Hair and Scalp

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

Hair Follicle

The tube-like depression or pocket in the skin or scalp that contains the hair root.

Hair Shaft

The portion of hair that projects above the epidermis; composed of keratinized cells.

Hair Root

The part of the hair located below the surface of the epidermis.

Cuticle

The outermost layer of the hair; consists of overlapping scale-like cells that protect the inner layers.

Cortex

The middle layer of the hair shaft; provides strength and elasticity and contains melanin for hair color.

Medulla

The innermost layer of the hair shaft; often absent in fine or vellus hair.

Sebaceous Gland

Oil glands connected to hair follicles that secrete sebum to lubricate the hair and skin.

Arrector Pili Muscle

Tiny involuntary muscles that cause goosebumps by contracting and raising hair follicles.

Anagen Phase

The growth phase of hair that lasts 2-6 years; about 90% of scalp hair is in this phase.

Catagen Phase

The transitional phase lasting 2-3 weeks when hair growth stops and the follicle shrinks.

Telogen Phase

The resting phase lasting 3-4 months; hair eventually sheds and new growth begins.

Dandruff

A common scalp condition causing flaking of dead skin cells; can be caused by fungus or dry skin.

Alopecia

Partial or complete loss of hair; can be caused by genetics, hormones, or medical conditions.

Trichorrhexis Nodosa

A hair disorder where nodes form on the hair shaft causing it to break easily.

Tinea Capitis

Ringworm of the scalp; a contagious fungal infection requiring medical treatment.

Pediculosis Capitis

Head lice infestation; contagious condition requiring proper treatment and sanitation.

Scabies

A contagious skin condition caused by mites burrowing into the skin; requires medical treatment.

Porosity

The ability of hair to absorb moisture; determined by the condition of the cuticle layer.

Elasticity

The ability of hair to stretch and return to its original length without breaking.

Melanin

Natural pigment found in the cortex of hair that determines hair color.
