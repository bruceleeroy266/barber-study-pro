CHAPTER 10

Disorders of the Hair & Scalp

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

1/20

Dermatology

Click to reveal

The branch of medical science that studies the skin and its diseases, disorders, and treatments.

2/20

Trichology

Click to reveal

The scientific study of hair and its diseases and care. Specialists are called trichologists.

3/20

Dandruff

Click to reveal

A common scalp condition causing flaking of dead skin cells. Can be caused by dry skin, oily skin, or fungus.

4/20

Seborrheic Dermatitis

Click to reveal

A severe form of dandruff with inflammation, oily scaling, and itching. Requires medical treatment.

5/20

Psoriasis

Click to reveal

A chronic autoimmune disorder causing red, scaly patches on the scalp and skin. Not contagious but not curable.

6/20

Alopecia

Click to reveal

The technical term for hair loss. Can be temporary or permanent, with various causes and types.

7/20

Androgenetic Alopecia

Click to reveal

Pattern baldness caused by genetics and hormones. Most common type of hair loss in men and women.

8/20

Alopecia Areata

Click to reveal

Autoimmune disorder causing patchy hair loss. Hair may regrow on its own or require treatment.

9/20

Traction Alopecia

Click to reveal

Hair loss caused by prolonged tension on hair from tight hairstyles, braids, or extensions. Can be permanent.

10/20

Tinea Capitis

Click to reveal

Ringworm of the scalp. A contagious fungal infection causing scaly patches and hair breakage. Requires medical treatment.

11/20

Folliculitis

Click to reveal

Inflammation of hair follicles, often caused by bacterial infection. Appears as small red bumps or pustules.

12/20

Pediculosis Capitis

Click to reveal

Head lice infestation. Contagious parasites that live on the scalp and feed on blood. Requires special treatment.

13/20

Scabies

Click to reveal

Contagious skin condition caused by mites burrowing into the skin. Causes intense itching and requires medical treatment.

14/20

Seborrhea

Click to reveal

Excessive secretion of sebum causing oily scalp and hair. Can lead to seborrheic dermatitis if severe.

15/20

Dry Scalp

Click to reveal

Condition where the scalp lacks sufficient moisture, causing itching and flaking. Different from dandruff.

16/20

Split Ends

Click to reveal

Condition where the hair shaft splits at the ends due to damage. Can only be removed by cutting.

17/20

Trichoptilosis

Click to reveal

The technical term for split ends. Caused by physical or chemical damage to the hair cuticle.

18/20

Canities

Click to reveal

The technical term for gray hair. Caused by loss of melanin production in the hair follicle.

19/20

Hirsutism

Click to reveal

Excessive growth of terminal hair in areas where hair is normally minimal or absent, often in women.

20/20

Hypertrichosis

Click to reveal

Abnormal growth of hair in unusual areas or excessive growth. Can be congenital or acquired.
