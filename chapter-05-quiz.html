CHAPTER 5 QUIZ

Implements, Tools & Equipment

Test your knowledge — 40 questions

📝 Chapter 5 Review Quiz

1. What is the difference between cast and forged shears?

Forged shears are hammered/compressed, making them denser and more durable. Cast shears are cheaper but more porous and weaker.

2. What are the two main types of clipper blades?

Detachable-blade clippers have blades that snap on/off; adjustable-blade clippers use a lever to open/close the blade.

3. What are the three motor types in clippers?

Rotary motors are high power/speed; pivot motors are quieter and good for thick hair; magnetic motors are reliable for fine textures.

4. Which blade edge is better for slide cutting?

Convex edges are very sharp and glide easily, making them ideal for slide cutting. Beveled edges are serrated and grip hair.

5. Why are changeable-blade razors preferred in shops?

Changeable-blade (disposable) razors are preferred for infection control and convenience — no honing or stropping needed.

6. What is the purpose of a strop?

A strop (leather or canvas) smooths and polishes the razor edge after honing. Honing grinds the edge; stropping finishes it.

7. What are modern safe methods for removing hair clippings?

Modern safe methods include towel folded around hand, paper neck strips, and vacuum systems. Traditional neck dusters can spread germs unless disinfected after each use.

8. What size shear do most barbers prefer?

Most barbers prefer shears between 6½" and 7½" for optimal control and versatility.

9. Which comb material is antistatic and heat/chemical resistant?

Carbon combs are antistatic and resistant to heat and chemicals. Hard rubber is flexible but deteriorates in disinfectant.

10. What does the tension screw on shears control?

The tension screw adjusts the distance and pressure between the blades. Proper tension is critical for smooth cutting.

11. Which handle design reduces stress on the hand and wrist?

The crane handle design reduces stress on the hand and wrist. Offset is ergonomic; opposing is traditional.

12. What blade size gives the closest cut?

The 00000 blade size gives the closest cut. Blade sizes vary, with more zeros indicating a closer cut.

13. Which clipper motor type is best for thick, coarse, or damp hair?

Rotary motors have high power and speed, making them ideal for thick, coarse, or damp hair. Pivot motors are quieter; magnetic motors are for fine textures.

14. What is a disadvantage of cordless clippers?

Cordless clippers are convenient and portable but may have less power than corded models. Battery life can also be a concern.

15. What is the main difference between a barber chair and a styling chair?

Barber chairs are hydraulic/motorized, reclining, with headrest and footrest — designed for shaves and full services.

16. What are shampoo capes typically made of?

Shampoo capes are made of waterproof vinyl to protect clients from water and products. Haircutting capes use nylon/synthetic for comfort.

17. Why are terry cloth towels preferred for shampooing?

Cotton terry towels are absorbent and hold heat well, making them ideal for shampooing and hot towel services.

18. What is the minimum size for disposable paper towels?

Disposable paper towels for barbering should be at least 12" × 24" — used as neck barriers, for wiping, and sanitary surfaces.

19. What is the purpose of neck strips?

Neck strips create a sanitary barrier between the cape's neckband and the client's skin, preventing irritation and cross-contamination.

20. What is an electric latherizer?

Electric latherizers create shaving lather quickly and hygienically. Traditional lather mugs and brushes (#3 size) are also used.

21. What ingredient in shaving soap improves lathering qualities?

Coconut oil is a key ingredient in shaving soap that improves lathering qualities and provides skin conditioning.

22. What should you check on blowdryer attachments before use?

Always check blowdryer attachments for sharp points on comb teeth or bristles that could scratch or irritate the client's scalp.

23. What should you never do with a blowdryer?

Never use a blowdryer near water — risk of electric shock. Always keep it moving, use protective agents, and avoid contact with water.

24. What are the four basic parts of thermal irons?

Thermal irons (curling/flat/Marcel) have rod handle, shell handle, barrel, and shell. Test temperature on tissue before use.

25. How do you test if a thermal iron is too hot?

Always test thermal iron temperature on tissue paper. If it scorches or browns in 5 seconds, it's too hot for hair.

26. What should you place between the client's scalp and a thermal iron?

Use a hard rubber comb as a barrier between the scalp and thermal iron. Never use metal combs — they conduct heat and can burn the scalp.

27. Who discovered the high-frequency machine?

Nikola Tesla discovered high-frequency current. High-frequency machines stimulate blood flow and product absorption.

28. What is a comedone extractor used for?

A comedone extractor is a small metal tool with a loop used to press out blackheads during facials. Requires proper training.

29. Why is the neck duster no longer considered safe?

Traditional neck dusters can harbor bacteria and spread germs between clients unless thoroughly disinfected after each use. Modern methods are safer.

30. What is a safe alternative to neck dusters?

Safe alternatives include towel-wrapped hand, paper neck strips, and vacuum systems. These are either disposable or easily sanitized.

31. What are the two main towel wrapping methods?

The two main towel wrapping methods are cloth towel wrap and paper towel wrap — both protect the hand during services.

32. What should you do before using thermal irons on a client?

Always test thermal iron temperature on tissue paper and ensure you've received proper training before using on clients.

33. What type of hair withstands less heat than normal hair?

Fine, lightened, or badly damaged hair is more fragile and withstands less heat. Use lower temperatures and extra care.

34. What is the main function of a galvanic machine?

Galvanic machines use direct current to introduce water-soluble products into the skin during facial treatments. Requires training.

35. Which shear edge needs sharpening every 4–6 months?

Convex edges are very sharp but need sharpening every 4–6 months. Beveled edges last 9–12 months but can't be used for slide cutting.

36. What is the proper finger position for holding shears?

Ring finger goes in the finger grip (still blade), thumb in thumb grip (moving blade). Little finger rests on the brace/tang.

37. What is the "set" of shears?

The "set" refers to blade alignment — how the two blades meet and overlap. Proper set is essential for clean cuts.

38. Which razor grind is preferred for sharpening and detecting resistance?

Concave (hollow-ground) razors are preferred for sharpening and detecting resistance during the shave. Wedge grinds are for coarse beards.

39. What is the premium finish for conventional straight razors?

Crocus finish is the premium finish for conventional straight razors, indicating high-quality craftsmanship.

40. What should you do if you drop your shears?

Never drop shears — it can damage the alignment, edges, or tension. If dropped, inspect carefully and have them professionally checked.
