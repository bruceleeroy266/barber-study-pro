Chapter 16: Men's Hairstyling

Click any card to flip and reveal the definition

Hairstyling

The art of arranging and shaping hair into a desired look using various techniques and products.

Blow Dryer

An electrical tool that uses heated air to dry and style hair simultaneously.

Vent Brush

A brush with openings that allow air to flow through for faster drying and smoothing.

Round Brush

A cylindrical brush used to create volume, curl, and smoothness while blow drying.

Pomade

A styling product that provides shine and medium to strong hold for slicked-back looks.

Hair Gel

A styling product that provides strong hold and can create wet or spiked looks.

Wax

A styling product that provides pliable hold and texture with a matte or low-shine finish.

Clay

A styling product that adds texture, volume, and matte finish while absorbing oil.

Mousse

A lightweight foam product that adds volume and light hold to hair.

Hairspray

A finishing product that provides hold and helps maintain the styled shape.

Sea Salt Spray

A texturizing spray that creates beachy waves and matte texture in hair.

Volume

The amount of fullness and lift in a hairstyle, created through cutting and styling techniques.

Texture

Visual and tactile quality of hair created by cutting techniques and styling products.

Hold

The ability of a styling product to maintain the shape and position of a hairstyle.

Shine

The amount of light reflection in hair; can range from high gloss to matte finish.

Quiff

A hairstyle featuring volume at the front with hair swept upward and backward.

Pompadour

A classic style with volume swept upward from the forehead and back over the crown.

Slick Back

A style where hair is combed straight back from the forehead for a polished look.

Side Part

A classic style with hair parted on one side and combed neatly in opposite directions.

Comb Over

A style where hair is grown longer on top and combed over to one side.
